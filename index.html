why perpensa

One model, one pass, one confident answer — that isn't deliberation.

Consequential, one-off decisions don't fail for lack of information. They fail because the reasoning was never seriously attacked before someone acted on it. Research agents hand you a memo; automation platforms grind through high-volume operational calls. The layer in between — where options are weighed, dissent is surfaced instead of averaged away, and a conclusion earns its confidence — still lives in someone's head. Perpensa is software for that layer.

what makes it different

Three things no memo-generator gives you

the vault

Sealed evidence

Every source a decision rests on is captured and sealed the day you saw it — tamper-evident and frozen. When the market shifts or a vendor goes private, your evidence doesn't move. Defensibility starts here.

templates

Owned instruments

Encode a methodology — a PE diligence rubric, a capital-committee gate — as a configurable overlay, then re-run it on the next deal. Not a slide deck of a framework: a living instrument you can audit line by line and version as your thesis evolves.

it's yours

You own the IP

The reasoning, the templates you build, the accumulated decision record — they belong to you, not to a consultant's filing cabinet. Perpensa hands you a capability you keep, not a conclusion you rent.

how it works

Four passes between a question and a decision you can defend

01 · branch

Branching research

Research that reacts to what it finds, forking into the questions that matter instead of marching an outline. Everything it pulls is sealed in the vault.

02 · debate

Adversarial deliberation

Contrasting expert voices argue the options. Disagreement is treated as signal, and fresh evidence is summoned mid-debate.

03 · challenge

Red-team pass

A dedicated challenger attacks the emerging conclusion — assumptions, incentives, failure modes — before reality gets the chance.

04 · trace

Reasoning lineage

Every conclusion carries its provenance: which evidence, which arguments, which challenges it survived — drillable to the sealed source.
