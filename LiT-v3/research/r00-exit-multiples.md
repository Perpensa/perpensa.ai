# RR-09 — Exit Multiples in Process-Technology Licensor Transactions (T2)

**Item:** RR-09 (Cluster E — Valuation, multiples, capital comparables)
**Source request:** CA1 (CAPITAL)
**Executed by:** Moderator (WebSearch)
**Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** What revenue and EBITDA exit multiples are actually paid in comparable process-technology licensor transactions, and is the 8x-10x revenue / 12x-15x EBITDA range applied in the LiT model defensible for a single-product, pre-scale company?

---

## Headline finding

The marquee comparables transact on **EBITDA**, not revenue, and at roughly **11x EBITDA** even for scaled, profitable, multi-decade licensors with diversified catalyst-and-process portfolios. The recent transaction in the set was also renegotiated **down by about 26 percent** between signing and amendment. This places the LiT model's **12x-15x EBITDA** at or above the top of what a strategic buyer paid for a far stronger asset, and exposes the **8x-10x revenue** anchor as the more aggressive of the two framings the model offers. **Confidence: Medium-High** on the comparable transactions; the gap between a scaled licensor and a single-product pre-scale company is qualitative and widens, not narrows, the discount that should apply to LiT.

---

## Evidence

### Honeywell / Johnson Matthey Catalyst Technologies (2025-2026) — closest recent comparable

- Honeywell agreed (May 2025) to acquire Johnson Matthey's Catalyst Technologies business for **£1.8 billion all-cash, stated as approximately 11x estimated 2025 EBITDA**, and that 11x was explicitly **inclusive of tax benefits and run-rate cost synergies** (i.e., the standalone multiple before synergies is lower). **[Cited]**
- In **February 2026 the consideration was amended down to £1.325 billion**, a reduction of roughly 26 percent from the original £1.8 billion. **[Cited]**
- The target is an established, profitable, diversified catalyst-and-process-technology licensor being folded into Honeywell's Energy and Sustainability Solutions segment. **[Cited]**

**Read for LiT.** A premier, cash-generative, diversified process-technology licensor changed hands at ~11x EBITDA *with* synergies and tax benefits baked in, and even that price was cut by a quarter before close. The LiT model's 12x-15x EBITDA assumption sits above this benchmark while describing an asset with none of the scale, diversification, profitability, or proven adoption. **[Assumption, High confidence in direction]**

### Honeywell / UOP (2005) — the canonical process-technology licensor acquisition

- UOP is described in its own filings as "a leading international supplier and **licensor of process technology, catalysts, process plants and consulting services**" to refining, petrochemical, and gas processing. **[Cited]**
- Honeywell acquired Dow's 50 percent stake and the prior Union Carbide stake; reported figures value UOP in the region of **£/$1.6 billion enterprise value** (reported payment ~$835m for the second half-stake). Per-multiple disclosure was not public in the retrieved filings. **[Cited for valuation magnitude; multiple NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL]**

**Read for LiT.** UOP is the archetype the LiT comparables set leans on (UOP / Metso / Hatch). It validates that the *category* — a process-technology licensor — is acquirable by strategics at scale, but it is a global, multi-product, century-old franchise. Using it to justify a multiple for a single-product pre-revenue company imports the franchise's quality into LiT's price without the franchise. **[Assumption, Medium confidence]**

### Sector benchmark multiples (public-market and PE/strategic deal medians)

- NYU Stern sector data (Jan 2026) puts **specialty chemicals EV/Sales at ~2.65x** as a public-market benchmark. **[Cited]**
- Median **EV/EBITDA for PE deals in Specialty Chemicals & Performance Materials fell to 12.3x in 2025 (from 13.8x in 2024)**; strategic-deal median **rose to 10.3x (from 8.2x in 2024)**. **[Cited]**

**Read for LiT.** The broad specialty-chemicals EV/Sales of ~2.65x is a different animal from a 1-percent-NSR licensor (whose revenue is nearly all margin), so the revenue multiple is not directly transferable in either direction; but it is a reminder that 8x-10x *revenue* is several times the sector's revenue multiple and is only defensible if LiT's revenue is treated as near-pure royalty margin — which then makes the EBITDA framing the honest one, and the EBITDA framing benchmarks at ~10x-12x, not 12x-15x. **[Assumption, Medium confidence]**

---

## Synthesis for the SWOT / valuation work

1. **The multiples in the LiT model are at or above the top of the comparable range, applied to a weaker asset.** Scaled, profitable process-technology licensors transacted at ~11x EBITDA (with synergies); the model assumes 12x-15x for a single-product, pre-scale, pre-adoption company. This is a Weakness in the valuation bridge, not a Strength. **[Assumption, High confidence in direction]**
2. **The revenue-multiple framing (8x-10x) is the more aggressive of the two.** It only holds if royalty revenue is treated as near-pure margin, at which point the comparable should be expressed as EBITDA — and the EBITDA comparables are lower. The model effectively gets to pick the framing that produces the higher exit. **[Assumption, Medium-High confidence]**
3. **Live evidence of multiple compression.** The single most recent in-category deal was cut 26 percent between signing and close (May 2025 to Feb 2026), and PE EBITDA medians fell year over year. An exit thesis built on a fixed forward multiple should be sensitised downward. **[Cited trend]**
4. **Per-deal multiple disclosure for UOP, Metso, and Hatch licensing units is not public.** The exact revenue/EBITDA multiples paid in those specific transactions remain **NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL** (paid M&A databases / banker comparables). The Johnson Matthey deal is the strongest publicly-multipled anchor and it argues for caution.

---

## Residual gaps (NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL)

- **UOP (2005) implied EV/EBITDA and EV/Revenue at the transaction** — not disclosed in public filings retrieved; requires paid transaction database.
- **Metso/Outotec and Hatch process-licensing-unit deal multiples** — these are embedded in larger diversified businesses and were not transacted as standalone licensors; standalone multiples are not public.
- **Any pure-play critical-minerals process-technology licensor exit** — none located; the category has no clean public comparable, so the exit thesis rests on adjacent (refining/petrochemical catalyst) licensors whose adoption and scale are not analogous to a pre-revenue tailings-recovery licensor.
