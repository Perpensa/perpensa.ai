# RR-16 — Durability of US critical-minerals / tailings programs and offtake mechanisms

**Tier:** T2 High (Web)
**Source request:** W3 (WATERSHED); carried here because CHOKEPOINT is silent at Round 00
**Executed:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** Status and durability of US critical-minerals and tailings programs (the Interior ~$1.2B / DPA actions), and whether any government offtake or price-floor mechanism (the MP Materials NdPr floor model) could underwrite a royalty base.
**Unblocks.** The policy backdrop in Opportunities / Threats, and whether the full commodity-price exposure of LiT's royalty base could ever be policy-buffered.

---

## Headline finding

There is a **real, well-funded, and currently bipartisan-durable US policy push** to recover critical minerals from mine waste and tailings, and there is a **proven federal price-floor-plus-offtake template** (MP Materials / DoD, July 2025) that does exactly what a commodity-exposed royalty base would need: it removes downside price risk. **But the two do not yet connect for LiT.** The price-floor template has been applied once, to a single strategic-magnet producer, as a bespoke equity-and-offtake deal; the tailings-recovery programs are funding and regulatory-streamlining vehicles, **none of which contains an offtake or price-floor mechanism**. For LiT specifically:

1. **The policy tailwind is genuine** and supports the demand narrative (Opportunity), with **moderate-to-high confidence**.
2. **The durability is mixed**: the legislated money (OBBBA / DPA) is durable to its statutory horizon; the executive-order and Secretary's-Order layer is **administration-dependent and reversible**, which is a Threat.
3. **The price-floor backstop is real but not available to LiT today**, and is **structurally hard to extend to lithium-via-royalty** (precedent terms below). Treat "policy could buffer the royalty's commodity exposure" as a **low-confidence Assumption / optionality**, not a Cited mitigant.

---

## The funded programs (Cited)

- **OBBBA / DPA financing.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (**P.L. 119-21, signed July 2025**) includes **$1 billion for Defense Production Act (DPA) financing through September 2027**. This is **legislated money with a statutory horizon** — durable to that date regardless of administration. [Brownstein; FAS]
- **DOE critical-minerals funding.** In **August 2025**, DOE announced **~$1 billion** in funding opportunities across critical-mineral supply chains: **$500M** for processing and battery manufacturing, **$250M** for **industrial byproduct recovery**, and **$135M** for rare-earth supply-chain enhancement including **extraction from mining-waste streams**. The byproduct-recovery and mining-waste tranches are the ones most adjacent to LiT's category. [DOE; carboncredits.com; Pillsbury]
- **DOI mine-waste initiative.** **Secretary's Order 3436, issued July 23, 2025**, directs Interior to streamline federal regulation of critical-mineral recovery from mine waste, make mine-waste recovery projects eligible for federal funding, prioritize uranium recovery from abandoned mines, and have USGS map and inventory federal mine-waste sites. It targets **mine waste, coal refuse, tailings, and abandoned mines** for zinc, germanium, tellurium, REEs, antimony, and arsenic. **It carries no funding figure, no offtake, and no procurement mechanism**; it is a regulatory-streamlining and prioritization order whose durability "depends on administrative continuity." [DOI press release; White House EO March 2025]

**Note on the "$1.2 billion Interior" anchor.** The specific "$1.2B Interior tailings program" figure in the seed materials **could not be verified** against public records. The retrievable, citable figures are the **$1B DPA (legislated)** and the **~$1B DOE (announced)**, plus the unfunded DOI Secretary's Order. Treat the "$1.2B Interior" number as `NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL: exact appropriation line and authorizing document for the cited $1.2B Interior figure` and do not carry it into a deliverable as Cited; use the verified DPA/DOE figures instead.

## The price-floor / offtake template — MP Materials / DoD (Cited)

The **July 10, 2025** MP Materials–Department of Defense public-private partnership is the most significant US critical-minerals industrial intervention since the Cold War, and it is the concrete model behind the "could a royalty be policy-buffered" question:
- **Ten-year price floor of $110/kg on all NdPr** product sold or stockpiled; DoD pays the shortfall when market price is below the floor and **captures 30% of upside** above it. The floor is roughly **2–4x the $25–60/kg historical NdPr range** — i.e., a genuine, generous downside backstop. [SFA Oxford; Columbia CGEP; Payne Institute]
- **Ten-year offtake**: defense and authorized commercial buyers purchase **100% of the 10X facility's magnet output**, structured to guarantee a **minimum ~$140M annual EBITDA**, with profit-split above $170M. [MP Materials; Simply Wall St]
- **Equity**: DoD took a direct equity stake; the deal pairs a price floor, an offtake, and public capital in one bespoke instrument. [IER; Bipartisan Policy Center; FAS]

## Can this template underwrite LiT's royalty base? (Assessment — Assumption, low confidence)

The template proves the *government can and will* remove commodity-price downside for a strategically critical material. But applying it to LiT's model is a stretch for four structural reasons, and this is the analytically important part for the deliverable:

1. **Wrong commodity tier.** The floor went to **NdFeB magnets**, the single most China-concentrated, defense-critical chokepoint. **Lithium chemicals are strategically important but far less of a sole-source chokepoint**, and there is no public US lithium price-floor precedent of this kind. Likelihood of a lithium NdPr-style floor is low.
2. **Wrong party.** A floor underwrites the **producer's output**. LiT is a **1% NSR licensor**, not a producer. A government floor would protect the *operator's* production value, not LiT's royalty — and it would do so only at the floored price, which **still leaves LiT's royalty fully exposed to the gap between any floor and the spot price**, unless the royalty were itself separately backstopped (no precedent exists for backstopping a private royalty).
3. **Bespoke, not programmatic.** The MP deal is a **one-off negotiated equity-offtake**, not a standing facility any qualifying company can access. It required MP's scale, single-asset national significance, and a magnet plant. LiT pre-validation has none of these.
4. **Durability cuts both ways.** The legislated DPA money is durable to Sept 2027; the *price-floor instrument* was an executive action and a future administration could decline to repeat it. So even the optionality is administration-dependent.

**Net for the deliverable.** The policy backdrop is a **real Opportunity** (funding pools LiT could pursue: DOE byproduct-recovery $250M and mining-waste $135M tranches; DPA financing to Sept 2027; DOI streamlining of mine-waste permitting). The price-floor template is **evidence the government will buffer commodity risk for the right asset**, which is a **long-dated optionality**, not a mitigant LiT can underwrite its royalty on today. The royalty base's full commodity-price exposure (Stipulation 4) **remains unbuffered** in the base case. Carry the policy-floor as upside optionality with a low confidence band; do **not** let it offset the commodity-exposure Weakness/Threat.
