# RR-08 — Technology qualification and LOI-to-license timeline at majors

**Tier:** T2 High | **Retrieval class:** External-likely (public case evidence captured; firm base rates undisclosed)
**Merges reading-note requests:** R2 (ROYALTY), M1 (MILL), D3 (DISSENT)
**Prepared by:** Moderator (WebSearch/WebFetch) | **Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** What are the documented stage-gates and elapsed time for a major miner to qualify and adopt a new processing technology from first contact to commercial integration, and what is the base rate of a pre-revenue process startup reaching a first major-miner license within roughly two years of seed? Does this make LiT's Month 25 first-license timeline and no-bridge-round self-funding claim credible?

---

## Findings

### F1 — Mining is structurally a late, conservative technology adopter, and this is a documented industry trait, not a LiT-specific risk. (Cited, high confidence)
The mining sector is consistently characterized in the literature and trade press as conservative and slow to adopt new technology, with the slowness rooted in structural features, not management failure: billion-dollar capital commitments, high consequence of operational failure, remote and hazardous sites, and R&D treated as a peripheral function disconnected from board-level strategy. A widely cited dynamic is the **"race to be second"**: miners deliberately avoid being the first commercial adopter because no individual or team will justify to shareholders the risk of deploying something unproven. The practical effect is that the burden of proof sits entirely on the technology vendor, and the first reference installation is the hardest sale to close. This is a Threat-quadrant fact the investor must price into the adoption timeline.

### F2 — The Jetti case is the most directly relevant timeline anchor, and it shows years, not months, from founding to commercial deployment, and longer to scale and value. (Cited, high confidence)
Jetti Resources, the closest asset-light mineral-recovery licensor analog (see [[r00-producer-vs-licensor]]), was **founded in 2014**. Its first commercial deployment — catalyst injection into a Capstone leach pad at Pinto Valley — occurred at the **beginning of 2019**, roughly **five years** after founding, and its $2.5B valuation followed only in **October 2022** (~8 years), after a running plant proved economics. Jetti carried tier-1 producer strategic backing (BHP, Freeport) and still took ~5 years to first commercial use, and reporting separately notes its revenue lagged projections because contract negotiation is slow. The Jetti path is the optimistic comparable, and it runs in years.

### F3 — LiT's Month 25 first-license timeline is aggressive against this base rate, especially because LiT is pre-validation. (Assumption, high confidence; reasoned from F1/F2 and the sponsor stipulations)
LiT plans a first major-miner license at roughly Month 25 (~2 years from seed) while the recovery uplift that the technology rests on is, by the binding sponsor stipulations, **unvalidated at the point of investment** ([[r00-recovery-evidence]]). The realistic sequence a major requires — bench/lab confirmation, then independent or on-site piloting, then a qualification/stage-gate review, then contracting — must all occur *after* the validation the seed round is meant to fund. Setting a ~5-year founded-to-first-commercial anchor from a *better-positioned* comparable (Jetti, with producer backing and proven catalysis) against LiT's ~2-year plan from a pre-validation start makes the Month 25 milestone an **outlier on the optimistic tail**, not a central expectation. The timeline is not impossible, but the base rate is against it.

### F4 — The qualification process itself is multi-gated and adds elapsed time after a successful pilot. (Cited/Assumption, moderate confidence)
Stage-gate development processes are the dominant model for R&D and capital-project decisions in mining (adopted by ~80% of large industrial firms), meaning a new processing technology must clear sequential gates — concept, pilot/demonstration, feasibility, and capital-sanction — each with its own review and budget cycle. Layered on top, specialized processing equipment commonly runs **12-18 months** from order to delivery and mid-sized construction **12-24 months**, so even after a major says "yes," physical integration adds a year or more before royalty-bearing production. For a licensor whose revenue begins only at commercial integration, these post-decision lags push first **material** royalty receipts well beyond the date of first contractual commitment.

### F5 — The no-bridge-round, self-funding-from-first-license claim is the fragile dependency, because it chains two low-base-rate events. (Assumption, high confidence)
The financial model's claim that LiT reaches self-funding without a bridge round depends on (a) validating the recovery uplift on schedule and (b) closing a first major-miner license at ~Month 25 — each individually optimistic against the base rate, and multiplicatively so when chained. If validation slips, or the first qualification runs the multi-year course F1-F4 imply, the company faces a financing gap precisely when it has spent seed capital on validation but not yet earned royalty revenue. This is the central execution-and-financing risk: the timeline assumption and the no-bridge assumption are the same assumption viewed twice, and a slip in adoption cadence breaks both.

---

## Effect on the deliberation
- Supplies an evidenced **adoption-timeline Threat**: mining's "race to be second" culture and the Jetti ~5-year founded-to-first-commercial anchor make a ~2-year (Month 25) first license an optimistic-tail outcome, not a base case.
- Directly challenges the **no-bridge-round / self-funding** claim in the financial model; flags refinancing risk for the Weaknesses quadrant and a likely **invest-with-conditions** lever (milestone-gated capital staging) in Strategy.
- Tells the execution plan to **gate capital on validation-then-qualification milestones** rather than a calendar, and to plan explicitly for a bridge/extension scenario rather than assume self-funding from a first license.
- Reframes diligence toward the **credibility and timing of the first license** as the binding constraint (the Jetti lesson that adoption, not technology, sets the revenue clock), reinforcing [[r00-producer-vs-licensor]] F4.
- Confirms that even a successful pilot does not start royalties: qualification gates plus equipment/construction lead times add a year-plus to first material royalty receipts, which the revenue ramp should reflect.

## Residual gap
**NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** a firm-by-firm base rate (what fraction of pre-revenue mineral-process startups reach a first major-miner commercial license within ~2 years of seed) is not published as a clean statistic; the conclusion is triangulated from the Jetti case, the documented conservative-adoption literature, and stage-gate timing rather than a single dataset. Individual majors' internal qualification stage-gate calendars are non-public. Sufficient to ground the timeline-credibility critique; a precise base rate would require proprietary venture/industry data.
