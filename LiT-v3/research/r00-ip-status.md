# RR-01 — LiT's actual public IP status

**Tier:** T1 Critical | **Retrieval class:** External-likely
**Merges reading-note requests:** CL1, D1
**Prepared by:** Moderator (WebSearch/WebFetch) | **Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** Does any published LiT PCT or priority application exist in public patent records, and what is its assignee, claim scope, and current national-phase status?

---

## Findings

### F1 — No published LiT / Lithium Technologies PCT surfaced in public patent databases. (Cited, high confidence on absence; consistent with a recent filing)
WebSearch of WIPO PATENTSCOPE and Google Patents on "Lithium Technologies" PCT / spodumene returned only unrelated third-party lithium-processing filings (for example PCT/FI2011/050440 "Method for processing spodumene"; PCT/AU2016/051278 "Recovery of lithium from silicate minerals"; WO2020128619A1; WO2020257074A1). None is assigned to Lithium Technologies, Inc. or to LiT. This corroborates the locked market-research note that a public-database search "did not surface a published LiT PCT," which is the expected result when a PCT is recent, because PCT applications publish roughly 18 months after the earliest priority date.

### F2 — The IP-holding vehicle for the underlying Virginia Tech inventions is VTIP, not LiT. (Cited, high confidence)
Patents in the relevant Yoon/Noble/Huang lineage are assigned to **Virginia Tech Intellectual Properties, Inc. (VTIP)**, the university's technology-transfer vehicle, which holds a large minerals-processing portfolio (PlainPatent lists 324 granted US patents 2015-2025). The closest publicly identifiable filing from the LiT inventor team is **US20240368734A1, "Methods for extracting rare earth elements from rare earth element sources"** (publication Nov 7, 2024; priority Dec 4, 2020; inventors Roe-Hoan Yoon, Christopher Aaron Noble, Roberto McAlister Council-Troche, Wei Liu; **assignee VTIP**). It claims ≤100C base-plus-electrolyte extraction of rare earths — adjacent low-temperature critical-minerals chemistry, REE-focused rather than spodumene/lithium. The point for the investor is the **assignee**: the publicly traceable low-temperature extraction art from this team sits with VTIP, and under the sponsored-research agreement LiT must *elect to license* VTIP IP (SRA Articles 13.4-13.6); it does not own it outright.

### F3 — Public framing of LiT's own position is provisional/PCT-stage, not granted. (Cited)
The locked market research records that LiT's own website page describing its patent position is hosted at a "provisionalpatent" path and states the company is in its seed round, consistent with provisional/PCT-stage rather than granted IP. A PCT application establishes a filing date and a 30-month national-phase runway; it is not a granted, enforceable patent in any jurisdiction.

---

## Effect on the deliberation
- Confirms and grounds **Sponsor Stipulation 1** (no clean, exclusive IP to license; pending PCT, not a grant) as an evidenced fact, not a hedge. The "internationally patented" / "globally patented platform" language in the prospectus, pitch, and business plan overstates the position: the public record shows a pending application at most, and the traceable underlying art is VTIP-owned.
- Sharpens the **ownership question** for Weaknesses: even the low-temperature extraction lineage publicly attributable to LiT's team is assigned to VTIP, so LiT's enforceable position depends on (a) the unpublished LiT PCT actually claiming something novel over this VTIP art, and (b) LiT securing and paying for an exclusive license election under the SRA.
- Feeds the valuation critique: a $13M pre-money leaning on "patented platform" exclusivity is resting on a pending filing whose claims are not yet public or examined to grant.

## Residual gap
**NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** the actual LiT PCT application number, its claim text, and the examiner's written opinion (named in Sponsor Stipulation 1 as sponsor-held but not yet filed to `research/`). Until produced, claim scope and patentability over the VTIP/HHS art cannot be assessed from public sources. Diligence item for any term sheet: obtain the PCT number, the written opinion, and confirmation of LiT's license election status with VTIP.
