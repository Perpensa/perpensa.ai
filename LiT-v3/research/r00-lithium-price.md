# RR-04 — Lithium spot price and 2026-2027 supply-demand balance (T2, Web)

**Status:** Executed by Moderator via WebSearch, 2026-06-29.
**Source request:** S1 (SPOT). Tier 2 High.
**Question:** Current lithium carbonate spot price as of mid-2026 and the latest 2026-2027 balance calls (deficit vs surplus camps).
**Unblocks:** The implied price environment behind the Year 3 to 5 revenue ramp and the commodity exposure of the NSR royalty base.

---

## Findings

### 1. Spot price level, mid-2026

- **Cited.** As of June 2026, lithium carbonate traded at roughly **21.4 USD/kg (~21,400 USD/t) in Northeast Asia** and materially lower elsewhere (other-regions index ~12.7 USD/kg). China battery-grade carbonate moved in a CNY 163,000–169,000/t band in mid-to-late June 2026, with mid-June prints near CNY 163,000/t described as a near-two-month low before a rebound toward CNY 169,000/t around 22 June. (Confidence: medium-high on order of magnitude; specific daily prints are tracker snapshots, not a single audited reference.)
- **Cited.** Regional carbonate prints earlier in 2026 (March) ranged widely: Europe ~11.95 USD/kg, North America ~9.42 USD/kg, South America ~7.97 USD/kg, confirming a fragmented, region-split price surface rather than one global number.
- **Context (Assumption, medium).** Mid-2026 levels sit well below the 2022 peak (which exceeded ~70,000 USD/t equivalent in China at the cycle top) and reflect a market recovering off a 2024–2025 trough. One outlet flagged forecasts that prices "could reach up to 28,000 USD/t by 2026" on strong demand, an upside call rather than the prevailing spot.

**Bottom line for the model:** the prevailing mid-2026 carbonate price is in the low-to-mid 20s thousand USD/t in the premium Asian market and below that elsewhere. Any LiT revenue projection that assumes a sustained higher price band is making a commodity-price call, not observing the current market.

### 2. 2026-2027 balance: the camps genuinely disagree

There is no consensus. The credible analyst range spans nominal surplus to structural deficit:

- **Surplus / narrowing-surplus camp (Cited).** UBS models 2026 demand growth ~14% against supply growth ~9.9%, narrowing the global lithium carbonate surplus to ~**109,000 t LCE** in 2026. S&P Global and Wood Mackenzie similarly carry a 2026 surplus, with S&P putting it near ~109,000 t LCE and titling its outlook "surplus to narrow."
- **Deficit camp (Cited).** Other banks and trackers expect a shift toward structural deficit by 2026, with shortfall estimates spanning roughly **22,000 to 80,000 t**, depending on how fast new projects come online. Bank of America's framing is that lithium "faces surplus but [a] deficit [is] likely by 2027."
- **Structural-tightness nuance (Cited).** Several sources note that even a nominal 2026 surplus masks a structurally tight spodumene feedstock position driven by converter overcapacity. The consensus directional call is that the market tightens into 2027 as BESS and EV demand accelerate (UBS: demand +14% in 2026, +16% in 2027; BESS rising from 8% of lithium use in 2020 toward ~42% by 2035).

**Bottom line for the model:** the forward price direction is contested, with a credible path to tightening into 2027 but an equally credible 2026 surplus that caps near-term price. The investor cannot treat a rising-price tailwind as given.

---

## Implications for the deliberation (carried, not yet a deliverable claim)

1. **Commodity-price exposure is real and two-sided.** Per Sponsor Stipulation 4, the NSR royalty is indexed to commodity prices, so the royalty base carries full lithium-price exposure. At mid-2026 prices the base is far below cycle-peak economics; a Year 3–5 ramp coinciding with a 2026 surplus would compress royalty revenue even if recovery volumes hit plan.
2. **The price call is an embedded assumption in the revenue model.** Whatever price the model uses for the ramp years should be surfaced and stress-tested against both the surplus (~109 kt) and deficit (~22–80 kt) scenarios, not a single point.
3. **Region split matters.** A ~2x gap between Northeast Asia and other-region carbonate prices means the royalty value depends on where licensee product clears, an input the model likely abstracts away.
