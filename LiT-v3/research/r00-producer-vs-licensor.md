# RR-11 — Phoenix Tailings, Jetti, and the producer-vs-licensor counterfactual

**Tier:** T1 Critical | **Retrieval class:** Web
**Merges reading-note requests:** CA3 (CAPITAL), D2 (DISSENT)
**Prepared by:** Moderator (WebSearch) | **Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** What are the funding, business model, and disclosed valuations of Phoenix Tailings and Jetti, and what does the broader tailings/critical-minerals recovery competitive set imply for the choice between LiT's 1-percent-licensor model and a full-margin-producer model?

---

## Findings

### F1 — Jetti is the licensor archetype, and it reached a $2.5B valuation only after commercial proof and tier-1 producer backing. (Cited, high confidence)
Jetti Resources licenses a proprietary catalytic lixiviant that unlocks copper from primary sulfide (chalcopyrite) ores in existing heap-leach SX-EW circuits. Its model is asset-light and "plug and play": it sells the technology and integration expertise, not the metal, deployed into infrastructure the miner already owns. Jetti raised roughly $100M across four rounds and was valued at **$2.5B in October 2022** (Series D, $100M). Critically, that valuation followed **commercial deployment** (a running plant at Capstone Copper's Pinto Valley, Arizona) and is backed by the largest copper producers as strategic investors — BHP Ventures, Freeport-McMoRan, Mitsubishi, Orion, T. Rowe Price. Reporting also notes contract-negotiation pace has **delayed revenue** relative to projections.

### F2 — Phoenix Tailings is the producer archetype, capturing full metal margin and valued well below Jetti despite raising more capital. (Cited, high confidence)
Phoenix Tailings extracts and **sells finished rare-earth metals** (NdPr, Dy, Tb, expanding to Sm, Y) from mining waste, operating its own refining facilities in Massachusetts and New Hampshire. It is vertically integrated and capital-intensive: it has raised **$117.8M across four rounds** (Series B totaling ~$116.6M including $10M Nomura venture debt) and was valued at **$360M in February 2026**. Backers include Lockheed Martin Ventures, BMW i Ventures, MPower, Presidio, Traxys, Eni Next, ARPA-E. It captures the entire processing margin but must fund plant and working capital to do so.

### F3 — The two archetypes price very differently, and the gap is informative for LiT. (Assumption, moderate confidence, reasoned from F1/F2)
The licensor (Jetti) carries a roughly 7x richer valuation than the producer (Phoenix) on comparable raised capital, which superficially favors the asset-light licensing model LiT has chosen. But the drivers of Jetti's premium are exactly what LiT lacks today: (a) **commercially proven** technology in a running plant, (b) **tier-1 producer strategic validation** at the cap table, and (c) a far larger addressable market (global copper sulfide stockpiles). Strip those three and the licensor premium is not transferable. Phoenix's lower multiple buys a **de-risked, revenue-producing** asset with metal in hand. LiT today resembles neither: it is a pre-validation licensor without commercial proof, producer backing, or a captured first customer.

### F4 — The value-capture critique stands: 1 percent NSR is the thin-capture end of the spectrum. (Assumption, moderate-high confidence)
The counterfactual CAPITAL and DISSENT raised is whether licensing is even the right structure. A producer captures the full recovered-metal margin (Phoenix's entire revenue line) but pays for it in capex and operating risk. A 1-percent-NSR licensor captures a thin slice of someone else's revenue with near-zero capex. The Jetti comp shows the licensor model **can** be highly valued — but only once proven and adopted, and Jetti's own revenue has lagged because adoption negotiation is slow. For LiT the live questions become: is 1 percent of total production value enough capture to justify a $13M pre-money, and is the binding constraint actually value-capture rate or **adoption speed** (the Jetti lesson)? Both point to the same diligence focus: the credibility and timing of the first major-miner license.

### F5 — The competitive set is crowded, better-capitalized, and mostly producer-or-process-integrated, not thin-royalty licensors. (Cited, moderate confidence)
The tailings/critical-minerals recovery field entering 2026 includes Phoenix Tailings (rare earths, producer), Metallium (Rice flash-heating, multi-element, producer, 2026 facility), Lithios (MIT, brine DLE, ~$315M-class peer Lilac in the same DLE space), TerraVolta ($225M DOE-backed Smackover lithium refinery), and Lilac Solutions ($315M, DLE producer). Federal money is flowing to the category (DOE up to $69M Critical Minerals and Materials Accelerator; ARPA-E; DOE feedstock list explicitly includes mine tailings and lithium). The pattern: the well-funded players are **building and selling the output** or providing integrated extraction, not licensing a recovery delta for a 1-percent royalty. LiT's chosen structure is comparatively rare in its own competitive set, which is either an under-exploited niche or a signal that capital favors margin capture in this market.

## Effect on the deliberation
- Supplies the **producer-vs-licensor counterfactual** as an evidenced Opportunity/Threat axis. Carry into Round 01 as: licensing is defensibly asset-light and can scale (Jetti proof), but the comparable that achieved a premium did so **only after commercial validation and major-producer adoption**, neither of which LiT has.
- Sharpens the **value-capture weakness**: 1 percent NSR sits at the thin end; the well-capitalized competitive set captures full margin. The investor must test whether thin capture is a deliberate moat-light scaling choice or a structural ceiling on returns.
- Reframes the binding risk from "is the royalty rate high enough" to **"is the first major-miner license achievable and on what timeline"** — the Jetti revenue-lag lesson. Feeds directly into RR-08 (adoption timeline) and the milestone-gating logic of the execution plan.
- Provides valuation anchors for RR-09/RR-10: a proven asset-light licensor (Jetti) at $2.5B and a revenue-producing producer (Phoenix) at $360M bracket the space, with the strong caveat that LiT is pre-validation and comparable to neither at a $13M pre-money without first retiring the recovery-validation risk (see [[r00-recovery-evidence]]).
