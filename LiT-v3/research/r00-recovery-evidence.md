# RR-13 — Bench evidence behind the 16 to 19 percent recovery uplift

**Tier:** T1 Critical | **Retrieval class:** External-likely (expected confirmed absence)
**Maps to reading-note request:** C1 (CRUCIBLE)
**Prepared by:** Moderator (WebSearch) | **Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** Is there any published bench data or test report giving feed type, baseline method, particle-size distribution, and grade-recovery curves behind the headline recovery uplift that drives the revenue model?

---

## Findings

### F1 — No public LiT bench data, test report, or grade-recovery curve exists. (Cited, high confidence on absence)
WebSearch for any Lithium Technologies / LiT disclosure of spodumene recovery test results returned only third-party academic and vendor literature on spodumene beneficiation generally (Prominer flotation notes; Nature *Scientific Reports* size-deportment and coarse-particle studies; Springer low-grade spodumene recovery; MDPI extraction-technique review). None is authored by, sponsored by, or attributable to Lithium Technologies, Inc. No LiT feed characterization, baseline-versus-treated comparison, particle-size distribution, or grade-recovery curve is in the public record. The single number the revenue model rests on is not publicly evidenced.

### F2 — The absence is a confirmed fact, not a retrieval failure. (Cited to binding stipulation; high confidence)
Sponsor Stipulation 2 states that the recovery improvement is "currently undefined and unvalidated," that only "initial research and an initial proof of the figure exist," and that "lab-scale and pilot-scale validation of the recovery improvement is the explicit purpose of the seed funds being raised." The validating data therefore does not yet exist by the decision owner's own account. The empty search result corroborates the stipulation: there is nothing public to find because the work that would produce it is what the $2M round is meant to fund.

### F3 — Public spodumene-recovery literature shows recovery is highly conditional, which raises the bar on a single headline figure. (Cited, moderate confidence as context)
The third-party literature that did surface shows lithium recovery swinging widely with feed grade, liberation size, and circuit configuration — for example one calcined-spodumene study reports 89 percent recovery on the finest fraction under closed-circuit grinding versus 65 percent open-circuit for the same grinding time, and effective fine-particle flotation is confined to a narrow +38 to 150 micron window. The implication for diligence: a recovery uplift is not a single transportable constant. It is specific to a feed type, a baseline method, and a size distribution. A headline "16 to 19 percent uplift" with no stated feed, baseline, or size basis cannot be assumed to generalize across the operators the royalty model assumes will adopt it.

---

## Effect on the deliberation
- Confirms and grounds **Sponsor Stipulation 2** as an evidenced absence. The headline recovery uplift enters Round 01 as an **unvalidated Assumption (low confidence)**, never as a Strength stated at face value. No deliverable may upgrade it to a demonstrated performance number.
- Establishes the **load-bearing weakness**: the entire revenue model multiplies an unvalidated recovery delta across an assumed adoption base. If the delta is smaller, feed-specific, or not reproducible at pilot scale, the revenue ramp does not exist. This is the central kill-switch DISSENT is entitled to press.
- Frames the **seed thesis correctly**: the investor is not buying a validated technology at a $13M pre-money. The investor is funding the experiment that would retire this risk. The use of funds and the central risk are the same line item, which is the defining feature of the deal and must be priced as such (milestone-gated staging, validation as a condition precedent).
- F3 adds a diligence sharpener: even a successful pilot must specify feed, baseline, and size basis, because a number proven on one feed does not underwrite a royalty base built on many operators' varied feeds.

## Residual gap
**NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** the sponsor-referenced "initial research and initial proof of the figure" (Stipulation 2). This is named as existing and providable but is not yet filed to `research/`. Diligence item for any term sheet: obtain the initial proof, the feed/baseline/size basis behind it, and an independent metallurgical read before releasing capital tranched against validation. DISSENT has agreed (register gap review) to proceed into Round 01 treating the headline uplift's absence as an established fact provided no deliverable upgrades it.
