# RR-12 — Low-Temperature Spodumene Leaching: State of the Art (T2)

**Item:** RR-12 (Cluster B — Recovery and process-technology evidence)
**Source request:** C3 (CRUCIBLE)
**Executed by:** Moderator (WebSearch)
**Date:** 2026-06-29
**Question.** What is the published state of the art in sub-500C NaOH-roasting and other low-temperature spodumene routes, what extraction rates do they report, and how does the "no acidic byproducts" claim stand against that literature?

---

## Headline finding

Low-temperature, alkaline (NaOH-roast) spodumene processing that **avoids the conventional 1100C calcination plus sulfuric-acid bake and produces no acidic byproduct stream is an active, published, peer-reviewed research field**, with multiple 2024-2025 papers reporting **>99 percent lithium extraction at roasting temperatures around 325C**. LiT's Stage Two operates in the same conceptual space as a body of openly published academic work rather than in clear white space. The environmental claim ("no acidic byproducts") is **directionally accurate for the alkaline route as a class**, but it is a property of the chemistry that the public literature already shares, not a proprietary LiT differentiator. **Confidence: High** that the field is published and crowded at the research level; the unresolved question is engineering scale-up and reagent economics, not chemical novelty.

---

## Evidence

### The alkaline low-temperature route is established in the literature

- **Direct lithium extraction from α-spodumene using NaOH roasting and water leaching** (Subasinghe & Rezaee, Penn State; ScienceDirect / SSRN, 2025): two-stage NaOH roasting at **~325C with room-temperature water leaching extracted over 99 percent of the lithium**. The mechanism is an alkali-silica reaction near the NaOH melting point (~318C) forming a water-soluble LiNaSiO4 phase; water leaching shows fast kinetics (peak Li recovery within ~1 minute at room temperature) and regenerates NaOH, yielding a high-pH (~13) product solution that reduces downstream purification reagent use. **[Cited]**
- **Advances in Lithium Extraction from Spodumene: Alternatives to Sulfuric Acid Digestion** (MDPI Minerals, 2025, 15(6):574): a review paper whose existence and framing confirm that **displacing sulfuric-acid digestion is a recognized, surveyed research direction**, not a single-team idea. **[Cited]**
- Additional 2024 papers report low-temperature synergistic / binary-composite-salt roasting of α-spodumene (e.g., with lepidolite or composite salts) targeting the same goal of avoiding high-temperature calcination and acid digestion. **[Cited]**

### What the route claims, against LiT's framing

- **Temperature.** Published routes operate roughly **250-600C**, with the headline result near **325C** — squarely "sub-500C." LiT's low-temperature claim is consistent with, not ahead of, the literature. **[Cited]**
- **No acidic byproducts.** The alkaline mechanism produces an alkaline (pH ~13) leach solution and avoids the sulfuric-acid bake entirely, so "no acidic byproducts" is **a true class property of the alkaline route**. It is shared by the published academic processes, so it does not by itself distinguish LiT. **[Cited]**
- **Recovery.** Published >99 percent lab extraction sets a high bar; any LiT Stage Two recovery claim must be benchmarked against this published performance, not against the conventional acid route alone. **[Cited]**

## Synthesis for the SWOT

1. **Stage Two is a Strength only at the engineering/integration level, not the chemistry level.** The underlying low-temperature alkaline approach is openly published with strong reported recoveries, so novelty cannot rest on "low-temperature, no-acid" as a concept. Any defensible Stage Two advantage must come from a specific, protectable engineering implementation, reagent-recovery economics, or integration with Stage One — not from the general chemistry. **[Assumption, High confidence]**
2. **The environmental narrative is credible but not exclusive.** "No acidic byproducts / no high-temperature calcination" is a real benefit of the alkaline class and supports the ESG story, but because it is shared by published work it is a category claim, not a moat. **[Assumption, High confidence]**
3. **Open-literature performance is a competitive threat to the value-capture story.** With >99 percent lab extraction already public at ~325C, a major miner or rival could pursue the alkaline route without licensing LiT unless LiT's specific implementation is both materially better and protectable. This sharpens the IP-moat and freedom-to-operate questions already flagged in RR-01/RR-02. **[Assumption, Medium-High confidence]**
4. **The gap between lab and commercial remains the real differentiator.** None of the retrieved sources demonstrate commercial-scale operation; reagent (NaOH) consumption, regeneration efficiency at scale, materials handling, and economics are unresolved. Stage Two's investability rests on closing that lab-to-plant gap, the same risk class as the Stage One recovery uplift (RR-13). **[Assumption, Medium confidence]**
