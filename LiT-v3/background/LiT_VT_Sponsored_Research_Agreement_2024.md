# Attachment A — Conceptual Study for a Lithium Hydroxide Production Facility

**Date:** February 24, 2024
**Submitted to:** Brian Keenum, CEO, Lithium Technologies, Inc. (202-930-5464; bkeenum@lithiumtechnologies.co)
**By:** Roe-Hoan Yoon, Aaron Noble and Kaiwu Huang, Department of Mining & Minerals Engineering, Virginia Tech, Blacksburg, VA 24060

## 1. Introduction

Lithium (Li) is the lightest alkaline metal in the periodic table with a soft texture, silver white color, high inflammability, and high reactivity. Given its exceptional chemical and physical properties, lithium has been widely used in the manufacture of batteries, glass, ceramics, lubricants, grease, etc. Currently, consumption of lithium for batteries is increasing exponentially, given the growing demand for electric vehicles, potable electronics, and grid storage applications (USGS, 2022). According to the International Energy Agency, each new electric vehicle contains 8.9 kg of lithium, and the total growth in demand from 2020 to 2040 is projected to be 42x, a value larger than that of any other mined commodity (IEA, 2021). Identified lithium resources in the U.S. total 9.1 million tons (USGS, 2022), and due to the rise of demand for lithium, the price of the battery-grade lithium carbonate increased from $12,700/ton in 2020 to $17,000/ton in 2022 (USGS, 2022). To meet the increasing demand for lithium, it is crucial to develop novel technologies for lithium extraction from different sources.

Spodumene (LiAl(SiO3)2) from pegmatite deposits is expected to be a promising source of lithium due to its high lithium grade (Tadesse et al., 2019). Pure spodumene contains 8% Li2O, 27.4% Al2O3, and 64.6% SiO2, while the lithium concentration in most brine deposits ranges from just 200 to 700 ppm (0.02-0.07%) (Choubey et al., 2016). Current industrial practices utilize three major steps in the extraction of lithium from spodumene ores. First, the raw spodumene ore is subjected to a series of physical separation processes, such as crushing/grinding, gravity separation, magnetic separation and flotation, to obtain a spodumene concentrate with >6% Li2O grade. The spodumene concentrate is subsequently calcinated at 1100 C, during which the natural spodumene in alpha-phase with low reactivity will be converted to more reactive and leachable beta-spodumene (Han et al., 2022). After calcination, lithium can be extracted from beta-spodumene using the acid-baking and water leaching processes. Lithium carbonate and/or lithium hydroxide are then precipitated from the pregnant leaching solution as final products. The requirement for high temperature and intense leaching makes this flowsheet costly and challenging for lithium production from spodumene.

To address these challenges, it is necessary to develop high-efficiency low-cost flowsheets for lithium extraction from spodumene. The proposed work seeks to develop a novel flowsheet for lithium extraction from spodumene concentrate that reduces the need for aggressive conversion and leaching conditions and improves overall environmental and economic outcomes. Our approach is to identify promising processes for extracting lithium from spodumene ores by conducting a comprehensive review of the literature. The potential candidates for the processes that will be reviewed and evaluated will include but not limit to acid treatment, alkaline treatment, mechanical treatment, hydrofluoric acid treatment, and salt roasting (Karrech et al., 2020). Based on the information obtained in the literature, we will develop one or more processes to produce lithium hydroxide from spodumene and perform the material balance that shows the flow rate for feed and various products. Technical and economic analysis will also be carried out for the developed flowsheets based on the data obtained in the material balance. Future follow on work will seek to reduce these process designs to practice while integrating increased efficiencies and process improvements that proceed from our research program.

## 2. Proposed Work

**2.1 Task 1: Literature Review and Process Identification.** Virginia Tech will conduct a comprehensive review of the technical literature and patents to identify promising processing approaches for extracting and recovering lithium carbonate and lithium hydroxide from spodumene ores. The review will focus on methods of converting alpha-spodumene to beta-spodumene, methods of extracting soluble lithium from spodumene, methods of solution purification, methods for recovery as lithium carbonate, and methods for conversion of lithium carbonate to a final lithium hydroxide product. Technical data from the various processing approaches, including process intensity (temperature and pressure), reagent consumption, metallurgical efficiency and yield, relative cost, relative scalability, and relative environmental impact will be recorded and summarized. Review of the extraction processes will leverage existing data sets, such as that compiled by Karrech et al. (2020) and will be extended to include both commercialized and novel processes.

**2.2 Task 2: Preconcentration Testing.** Virginia Tech will conduct exploratory testing to evaluate the potential to recover lithium concentrates from mine and aggregate tailings. Virginia Tech will work with the sponsor to acquire tailings materials from sites in and around Virginia. Upon delivery, the material will be analyzed to determine the raw lithium content, and additional laboratory testing will evaluate means of concentrating the material to grades suitable for processing at a lithium refinery, nominally greater than 6% lithium. Potential methods processing methods may include crushing, grinding, screening, flotation, and others.

**2.3 Task 3: Flowsheet Development.** Using data from the literature review, experimental test work, and other sources, Virginia Tech will develop one or more conceptual process flowsheets for the concentration and refining of lithium products from various sources using the flexible feedstock approach described in the sponsor's background IP. End products will include lithium carbonate and lithium hydroxide, while potential feedstocks will include spodumene, mine waste rock, mine tailings, and any other materials identified through the course of the study. The flowsheets will be used to develop a block flow diagram outlining the major unit operations and will include a preliminary material balance showing the feed requirements and various product flow rate. To the extent practicable, the material balance will leverage peer reviewed literature data; however, additional engineering estimates and assumptions will be included as needed to complete the material balance.

**2.4 Summarized Deliverables**

- Technical report on lithium extraction and recovery technologies.
- Technical report on the experimental work, including material analyses and pre-concentration testing from mine tailings.
- Preliminary block flow diagram and material balance for lithium concentration and refining processes that utilizes the sponsor's background IP and integrates any new improvements or upgrades identified through this research.
- Detailed research plan and cost estimate for next project phase.
