# LiT Deliberation — Sponsor Stipulations (Binding)

**Provenance:** Brian Keenum, CEO, Lithium Technologies, Inc.
**Submitted:** June 29, 2026
**Status:** Binding stipulations from the decision owner for the company-framed deliberation. The committee accepts items 1, 2, and the accepted part of 3 as established facts, and applies the correction in 3. Preserve this provenance in the working record. These stipulations settle facts; the committee still weighs their implications and preserves dissent on those implications.

---

## Stipulation 1 — There is no clean, exclusive IP to license

Accepted as established: Lithium Technologies has no clean, exclusive intellectual property available to license.

Sponsor-provided evidence (referenced, to be filed in `research/` when provided): a PCT application and the examiner's written opinion. A PCT application is a pending filing that preserves a priority date and a national-phase runway; it is not a granted patent and does not by itself confer exclusive rights in any jurisdiction.

Effect on the deliberation: the IP-moat weakness is confirmed as a fact, not a contingent or data-room-dependent finding. Remove any hedge that treated this as unresolved; state it plainly. The company position is a pending PCT, not an enforceable exclusive right.

## Stipulation 2 — The recovery improvement is undefined and unvalidated

Accepted as established: the recovery improvement that drives the entire revenue model is currently undefined and unvalidated.

Sponsor context: initial research and an initial proof of the figure exist and can be provided. Lab-scale and pilot-scale validation of the recovery improvement is the explicit purpose of the seed funds being raised.

Effect on the deliberation: treat the headline recovery uplift as an unvalidated assumption, not an established performance number. Validation is a use of funds, not a present capability. The financial model's dependence on this single unvalidated input is a load-bearing weakness, and the seed raise exists to retire that risk.

## Stipulation 3 — Revenue-model inputs are aggressive and compounding, with one correction

Accepted as established: every quantitative input to the revenue model is aggressive, and the errors compound.

Correction, binding: any finding that states or implies the NSR royalty does not account for commodity pricing is incorrect and must be corrected. A net smelter return royalty is, by definition, a percentage of the net realized value of metal sold, so it is fully indexed to commodity prices. When prices rise the royalty rises; when prices fall it falls.

Correct framing to carry forward: because the NSR is fully indexed to commodity prices, the royalty revenue base is directly and fully exposed to commodity-price volatility. The concern is exposure to price swings, not an omission of price from the model. This exposure compounds with the other aggressive inputs rather than standing apart from them.

---

## How the committee applies these

- Treat 1, 2, and the accepted part of 3 as established facts. Remove the contingent or low-confidence hedges that these stipulations resolve.
- Apply the Stipulation 3 correction everywhere the NSR characterization appears. Restate the point as full commodity-price exposure of the royalty base, not as the model ignoring price.
- Continue to deliberate the implications. DISSENT and every seat retain the right to argue how serious these confirmed weaknesses are, including whether the unvalidated recovery number is a kill-switch risk for the company's plan.
- In the client deliverables (`synthesis/`), present these as plain facts about the company's position. Do not attribute them as a decree; integrate them into the analysis.
