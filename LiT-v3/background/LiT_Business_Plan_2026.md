# Executive Summary

## The Opportunity

The global demand for critical minerals like lithium, rare earth elements (REEs), and a growing roster of materials essential to technology and the energy transition is accelerating at a pace that existing mining infrastructure cannot match. New mines take decades to permit, finance, build, and bring to production. The demand for these minerals requires a more immediate way to address the market.

LiT has developed a two-stage critical materials processing platform that addresses this gap. Rather than building new mines, LiT's technology dramatically improves the recovery, yield and economic viability of existing mine operations and the vast volumes of previously discarded tailings and fine-particle waste streams that surround them. The result is more critical minerals from assets that are already in the ground, already permitted, and already operating, using substantially less energy, less environmental harm, and far less capital than greenfield alternatives.

LiT is a process technology company with a globally patented platform, a licensing-first business model, and a mission to make the world's existing mineral assets work harder while doing so cleanly, efficiently, and at scale.

## The Platform

LiT's integrated two-stage platform achieves 16% to 19% recovery improvements over conventional method by combining:

- A proprietary physical separation process that is feedstock-agnostic, enabling efficient separation and concentration of target minerals from hard rock ore, REE deposits, and fine-particle tailings streams.
- A low-temperature chemical extraction process, bench-proven for lithium, that operates well below 500°C, which eliminates the extreme energy demands of traditional high-heat methods, reduces greenhouse gas emissions, and produces no acidic byproducts.

Both stages are described and protected under an internationally filed PCT patent. The integrated platform has been successfully validated at bench scale across lithium spodumene, rare earth hard rock ores, and tailings feedstocks, in collaboration with Virginia Tech's Department of Mining and Minerals Engineering.

## The Business Model

LiT licenses its technology to mining operators worldwide. The model combines an upfront licensing fee with an ongoing production royalty negotiated at signing, a structure that aligns LiT's success directly with the operator's performance. CapEx and OpEx requirements for the technology are minimal relative to the value unlocked; LiT's process can be delivered in a modular mobile format or integrate into existing plant infrastructure, rather than requiring greenfield construction.

Every major mining company in the world operating in lithium, rare earths, or adjacent critical minerals is a potential customer. The operators who would be considered LiT's competitors in a production-focused model like Albemarle, Piedmont Lithium, Livent, and others are now among the most natural licensees.

LiT's long-term ambition is to be the global standard for low-energy, high-recovery critical minerals processing with a technology platform that enables the world's existing mineral assets to produce more, waste less, and operate more sustainably than has previously been possible.

Moreover, we wish our legacy to be one where we are able to not only reduce the impact of mining and mineral processing, but to help repair the past damage that the industry has done to the areas where mines have operated around the world.

# Opportunity

## Problem & Solution

### The Problem Worth Solving

The energy transition runs on critical minerals. Lithium, rare earth elements, cobalt, nickel, and a range of other materials are foundational to electric vehicles, stationary energy storage, defense systems, and advanced electronics. Demand for these minerals is growing at 25% to 30% annually. Lithium demand, as an example, is projected to reach 3.3 million metric tons of lithium equivalent annually by 2030.

The conventional response to this demand to build more mines faces a fundamental structural problem. New mine development in the United States and allied nations takes twenty to twenty-five years or more from discovery to production. Foreign producers, particularly those concentrated in China, Australia, Chile, and Argentina, currently account for over 90% of global critical mineral production, creating supply chain vulnerability for those countries that are not included in this small list.

The supply gap is not only a pipeline problem. Existing mine operations around the world are leaving enormous value in the ground and in the tailings pond. Conventional physical separation processes struggle with fine-particle material, meaning that a significant fraction of the target mineral in any given ore body is being discarded into tailings impoundments rather than recovered. These tailings piles represent decades of accumulated, unrecovered value. At the same time, the chemical extraction processes used to refine concentrated ore into battery-grade or refined mineral products are energy-intensive, thermally extreme, and environmentally costly, consuming vast quantities of heat energy and producing acidic waste streams.

The result is a global critical minerals sector that is simultaneously under-supplied and under-optimized. More minerals are needed. More minerals are being wasted. And the processes used to recover what is captured are more damaging and costly than they need to be.

### Our Solution

LiT's two-stage platform attacks two dimensions of this problem. The physical separation stage recovers target minerals from feedstocks that conventional methods discard, including fine-particle fractions in active mining operations and existing tailings impoundments. The chemical extraction stage refines those concentrates using a low-temperature process that dramatically reduces energy consumption, eliminates acidic byproducts, and shortens processing time by approximately 20% compared to conventional methods.

Critically, LiT's approach is designed so that it can operate within or alongside existing mine or refinery infrastructure. The platform can be delivered in a mobile format to rehabilitate active or abandoned tailings deposits, for others it can be integrated into current operations, improving the yield and economics of assets that are already in production. This means value can be delivered as quickly as the technology licensing agreement and implementation roadmap allow, without a lengthy permitting and compliance timeline.

LiT's physical separation technology is feedstock-agnostic, with bench-scale validation across lithium spodumene, rare earth hard rock ores, and tailings materials. The chemical extraction process has been bench-proven for lithium and is designed to be adaptable to other mineral streams with targeted research and development. Together, the two stages form an integrated platform that addresses the full recovery chain from ore in the ground to refined mineral product ready for its intended use.

The platform is protected by an internationally filed PCT patent covering the integrated two-stage process, with sufficient technical specificity in both the physical and chemical stages to establish meaningful protection across the core innovations.

### The Tailings Opportunity — Our Lead Value Proposition

Of all the use cases LiT's platform addresses, none is more immediate or more economically compelling than tailings and small particle recovery. Mining operations worldwide have generated decades of tailings impoundments, which are the fine-particle waste streams discarded when conventional separation methods reach the limits of their effectiveness. These tailings piles contain significant concentrations of unrecovered critical minerals. They also represent an environmental liability: unstable, potentially leaching into and occupying land that would otherwise could be returned to productive and beneficial use.

LiT's physical separation process is specifically effective in the fine-particle size ranges where conventional methods fail. Applied to existing tailings impoundments, it can recover target minerals that have already been excavated, and they are recovered at a fraction of the cost and environmental impact of greenfield extraction. For mining operators, this represents a new revenue stream from a sunk cost, combined with a reduction in the long-term environmental liability of managing those impoundments.

This tailings opportunity exists today, at scale, across every major mining region in the world. The technologies can be licensed and integrated into existing operations and unearthed materials, producing value while bypassing new mine development timelines. We believe this represents the fastest path to meaningful commercial deployment and the most differentiated value proposition in our portfolio.

# Business Model

## Licensing-First Strategy

LiT's primary commercial model is technology licensing. LiT licenses its two-stage processing platform to mining operators and mineral processors globally, enabling those operators to improve recovery yield from existing operations and tailings assets without building new mine capacity or processing facilities.

The licensing model is strategic for several reasons. It requires dramatically less capital than a production-ownership model, allowing LiT to deploy commercially without the $300M–$500M construction cost of a purpose-built processing facility. It is scalable across geographies and mineral types without proportional increases in LiT's own operational footprint. And it creates aligned incentives because LiT earns more as its licensees produce more, which means LiT's commercial success is directly tied to delivering measurable performance improvement to existing operations.

## Fee Structure

LiT's licensing agreements are structured as a combination of:

- An upfront licensing fee reflecting the value of process access, technical integration support, and the competitive advantage conferred by LiT's patented technology.
- An ongoing royalty of 1% of the operator's total mine production value on a Net Smelter Return (NSR) basis, which is the same reporting basis operators already use for government royalties, streaming agreements, and financial reporting.

This structure provides LiT with near-term revenue through upfront fees while building a growing, recurring royalty base as the licensing portfolio expands. It also gives licensees a clear return on investment framework: the cost of the license is 1 cent for each dollar of product produced, when they can expect a 16 to 19 cent improvement per dollar as a result of the use of LiT's technology.

## Capital Requirements

The licensing model fundamentally transforms LiT's capital profile relative to a production-ownership approach. LiT does not need to build, own, or operate processing facilities at commercial scale. The capital required is focused on advancing the technology through pilot-scale validation, building the commercial and technical team to support licensing relationships, protecting and prosecuting the patent portfolio, and funding the R&D program to extend chemical extraction capability to additional mineral streams.

For licensees, LiT's process integrates into existing plant infrastructure, meaning the capital requirement on the operator's side is also substantially lower than greenfield alternatives. This low-friction integration profile accelerates adoption and shortens the decision cycle.

# Financial Plan

## Overview

LiT's financial model reflects the fundamental advantages of a licensing-first commercial strategy: minimal capital requirements, high margins at scale, and a revenue structure that compounds as the licensing portfolio grows. The projections presented here reflect a five-year operating horizon beginning from the date of initial funding, with a two-year focused program of pilot scale validation and IP protection, with revenue and commercial licensing activity commencing in Year 3.

The detailed financial projections are provided as addenda to this document. The narrative that follows describes the key drivers, assumptions, and financial logic underlying those projections.

## Revenue Model

LiT's revenue is generated through two complementary streams that operate at different stages of the licensing relationship:

### LOI and Exclusivity Retainers

In advance of a commercial licensing agreement, prospective licensees who have completed initial technical evaluation and wish to secure exclusivity pay an exclusivity retainer of $250,000. This retainer is credited in full against the commercial license fee upon signing. It generates near-term revenue and signals serious commercial intent from the operator. Two such retainers are projected to be received in Months 23 and 24, representing the first revenue for the company as the Year 2 business development program matures.

### Commercial License Fees

Upon execution of a full commercial licensing agreement, the licensee pays the balance of the upfront license fee: $1,750,000 for small and mid-tier operators ($2,000,000 total upfront, inclusive of the prior retainer) and $9,750,000 for the major operator ($10,000,000 total upfront). These fees reflect the value of process access, technical integration support, and the competitive advantage conferred by LiT's patented technology, and do not vary with production volume.

### Production Royalties — Net Smelter Return Basis

LiT's ongoing royalty is structured as 1% of the operator's total mine production value on a Net Smelter Return (NSR) basis, which is the same reporting basis operators already use for government royalties, streaming agreements, and financial reporting. This structure was deliberately chosen for three reasons.

First, it is independently verifiable. NSR is a standard, audited figure in every mining company's financial statements. LiT's royalty is calculated from numbers the operator already produces and reports to third parties. This eliminates any need for additional metrics negotiation and validation, which can be difficult to enforce.

Second, it eliminates the measurement complexity inherent in improvement-based royalties. An incremental royalty requires establishing a recovery baseline, continuously measuring against it, and attributing variations to specific causes. Ore grade variability, equipment maintenance cycles, operational decisions, and market factors all affect recovery independently of LiT's technology, creating a chronic source of measurement dispute that is outside LiT's control and serves neither party.

Third, it aligns with industry convention. Academic and commercial literature consistently establishes that process technology royalties apply to the sales value of the product produced using the licensed technology. LiT's 1% NSR structure is consistent with this convention while remaining operationally simple for operators to evaluate, approve, and administer.

At 1% of total NSR, LiT's royalty costs the operator one cent on every dollar of production — in exchange for a technology that recovers 16 to 19 additional percentage points of mineral content that would otherwise be discarded. Annual royalties per small and mid-tier operator are modeled at $2,286,000 per year (1% of $229 million in estimated total annual mine production value). The major operator generates $22,860,000 per year in annual royalties (1% of $2.29 billion in estimated total production value). For the purposes of the financial projections presented here, new operators generate 50% of the full-year royalty in their signing year to reflect production ramp time.

This investment will take many forms, including internal development of technologies and empowering additional related startups and organizations through grant funding and collaborative projects.

### Operational Overhead
