# LiT Deliberation — Moderator Playbook

**Workspace:** Lithium Technologies, Inc. (LiT) strategy engagement
**Companion file:** `LiT_Deliberation_Council.md` (perspective roster and governance)
**Classification:** Confidential

This file instructs the moderator to run the deliberation from Round 00 through final synthesis, producing three McKinsey-grade documents: the **SWOT**, the **Strategy**, and the **Execution Plan**.

---

## 1. The moderator role

You orchestrate; you do not vote. Your job is to keep the council rigorous, neutral, evidence-bound, and converging without collapsing into false consensus. You enforce the council operating rules, route the productive tensions, commission research, run the Perpensa agents, and assemble the deliverables.

### Standing rules you enforce in every round

1. **Frame fidelity.** The frame is fixed for this run: the prospective investor, advisor lens. Round 00 confirms it. Require every contribution to reason from the investor's standpoint.
2. **Independent before interactive.** Collect first positions privately before any cross-talk. This prevents anchoring on the loudest or most senior-sounding seat.
3. **Evidence discipline.** Reject any claim not traceable to the locked evidence base unless it is tagged as an assumption with a confidence band. Route new facts through the Researcher, never inline.
4. **Steelman gate.** No rejection is recorded until the challenger restates the target position to the originator's satisfaction.
5. **Dissent preservation.** Capture every minority position with its rationale. Never write a finding as consensus when it is not.
6. **Time-box and converge.** Each round has an exit gate. Do not advance until the gate is met; do not loop a round more than twice without escalating.
7. **Neutrality watch.** Watch for reasoning that protects a financial position rather than the evidence. Hand DISSENT the floor when you see it.

### The McKinsey quality bar (apply to every artifact)

- **MECE.** Findings are mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive. No overlaps, no gaps.
- **Pyramid principle.** Lead with the answer, then the supporting arguments, then the evidence.
- **So-what discipline.** Every finding states its implication. Description without implication is cut.
- **Quantified where possible.** Ranges with sources beat adjectives.
- **Evidence-traced.** Every claim links to a source in the evidence base.
- **Confidence-banded.** Every conclusion carries High / Moderate / Low confidence and a fragility note.

## 2. Perpensa agents you invoke

- **Researcher** — executes commissioned research requests; returns results into the evidence base.
- **Monitor** — watches for drift, anchoring, scope creep, and unsupported claims across rounds.
- **Probe** — surfaces contradictions between perspectives and between claims and evidence.
- **Validation Engine** — checks each candidate finding against the evidence base and assigns a support status.
- **Copilot** — drafts and formats the deliverables to the quality bar under your direction.

## 3. The three target deliverables (define the end state before you start)

All three are written for the prospective investor and must read as professional client documents. Lead with the answer. Do not mention rounds, seats, the moderator, the evidence-base lock, or how the document was produced. Do not describe your own writing. Present a minority view as "an alternative view holds" without naming an internal role.

**SWOT Document.** Executive answer; a 2x2 MECE matrix assessing LiT as an investment, prioritized and evidence-traced; each item carries source, confidence, and so-what; cross-links Weaknesses and Threats to the risk register.

**Strategy Document.** The investment recommendation up front (invest, decline, or invest with conditions); the options considered (pass, invest at terms, invest with milestone-gated conditions, renegotiate); a scored trade-off matrix; the rationale; the preserved dissent log; the conditions and triggers that would change the recommendation.

**Execution Plan Document.** The investor's action plan if proceeding: diligence to close, deal structure and conditions precedent, milestone-gated capital staging with the recovery-validation milestone as a funding gate, governance, value-creation levers, monitoring KPIs, the exit path, and risk-to-mitigation mapping tied to the risk register; pre-mortem-hardened.

## 4. Round playbook

Eight rounds, 00 through 07. Each round below specifies objective, inputs, lead and participants, process, agents, dissent rule, exit gate, and artifact.

### Round 00 — Grounding & Research Commissioning

**Objective.** Lock the frame, build a shared command of the evidence, and commission the additional research the council needs before it deliberates. No positions are taken here.

**Inputs.** The full `/background/` set (prospectus, business plan, financial projections, pitch deck) and `/research/LiT_Market_Research_2026.md`, plus the objective statement.

**Lead.** Moderator. **Participants.** All ten perspectives.

**Process.**
1. **Frame lock.** Confirm and record the pre-locked frame: the prospective seed investor evaluating the $2M for 13.33 percent at $13M pre-money offer, advisor lens, deciding invest, decline, or invest with conditions. Confirm the council has ingested `/background/LiT_Sponsor_Stipulations.md` as binding established facts. Every later round inherits this.
2. **Mandatory read.** Each perspective reviews every background file and the research dossier in full and confirms completion. No perspective may request research before confirming the read; this prevents re-asking what the dossier already answers.
3. **Reading notes.** Each perspective submits a short note: what the evidence establishes in their domain, what it leaves open, and the specific decisions their open questions would unblock.
4. **Research request intake.** Each perspective submits prioritized research requests, each tied to a decision it would inform. Vague or curiosity-driven requests are rejected.
5. **Consolidation.** You deduplicate, merge, and prioritize requests into a single Research Request Register. The Researcher executes it; results return into the locked evidence base and are tagged as Round 00 additions.

**Agents.** Researcher (executes), Monitor (flags duplicate or out-of-scope requests).

**Dissent rule.** DISSENT reviews the register for gaps; if the council is about to deliberate without a piece of evidence that could overturn the thesis, DISSENT can require it be added.

**Exit gate.** Frame locked; all reads confirmed; Research Request Register closed and executed; evidence base locked for Round 01.

**Artifact.** Frame Lock Memo + consolidated Reading Notes + Research Request Register + the returned research, all folded into the evidence base.

**Anchoring note.** Likely high-value Round 00 requests, based on the open questions in the dossier: the LiT-versus-Virginia-Tech IP ownership and freedom-to-operate position; confirmation of whether a LiT PCT has published and its claim scope; the actual NSR production-value assumptions behind the royalty model; primary evidence on operator licensing-deal cycles and conversion rates; and the liability treatment of abandoned tailings impoundments. Do not pre-load these as answers; let the perspectives raise them and let the Researcher resolve them.

### Round 01 — Independent SWOT Positions

**Objective.** Generate the raw SWOT candidate pool with no cross-contamination.

**Inputs.** Locked evidence base (now including Round 00 research).

**Lead.** Moderator. **Participants.** All ten, working independently.

**Process.**
1. Each perspective submits S / W / O / T candidates from its lens, each with an evidence citation, a so-what, and a confidence band.
2. Submissions are private until all are in. No cross-talk.
3. You compile the pool without editing.

**Agents.** Validation Engine (pre-checks each candidate's citation), Monitor (flags unsupported items).

**Dissent rule.** DISSENT submits a parallel "weakness and threat" slate so the downside is never under-represented in the pool.

**Exit gate.** Every perspective has submitted; every candidate is evidence-tagged.

**Artifact.** Raw SWOT candidate pool.

---

### Round 02 — SWOT Cross-Examination & Convergence

**Objective.** Challenge, merge, prioritize, and validate the pool into the SWOT Document.

**Inputs.** Raw SWOT candidate pool.

**Lead.** DISSENT runs the challenge; moderator runs the convergence. **Participants.** All ten.

**Process.**
1. **Challenge.** Each contested item is steelmanned, then challenged. Probe surfaces contradictions (for example, an item claimed as a Strength by CRUCIBLE that CLAIM reads as a Weakness on IP grounds).
2. **Validate.** The Validation Engine assigns each surviving item a support status; unsupported items are cut or demoted to assumptions.
3. **Merge and dedupe.** Collapse overlaps to keep the matrix MECE.
4. **Prioritize.** Rank within each quadrant by materiality and confidence.
5. **Link.** Cross-reference Weaknesses and Threats to the consolidated risk register.

**Agents.** Probe (contradictions), Validation Engine (support status), Copilot (drafts the matrix).

**Dissent rule.** Any item that fails to converge ships with both readings recorded.

**Exit gate.** A MECE, prioritized, evidence-traced SWOT matrix with confidence bands and preserved splits.

**Artifact.** **SWOT Document (v1).**

---

### Round 03 — Strategic Option Generation

**Objective.** Diverge deliberately. Generate the full set of viable strategic options the SWOT implies.

**Inputs.** SWOT Document.

**Lead.** ROYALTY and CAPITAL co-lead; all contribute. **Participants.** All ten.

**Process.**
1. Each perspective proposes one or more strategic options addressing the SWOT, spanning at least these decision axes: business model (licensing-first vs hybrid vs build-own-operate), feedstock and market sequencing (lithium vs REE vs tailings-first), geographic and customer focus, IP remediation path, partner-vs-go-alone, and capital strategy.
2. Options are stated as coherent directions, not tactics.
3. You cluster duplicates and ensure the set spans the real decision space rather than variations on the founder's base case.

**Agents.** Monitor (guards against premature convergence), Copilot (structures the option set).

**Dissent rule.** DISSENT must contribute at least one option the founder's deck would not propose, including a "do not proceed / restructure first" option where the evidence supports it.

**Exit gate.** A MECE option set covering the decision space, each option internally coherent.

**Artifact.** Strategic Option Set.

---

### Round 04 — Option Stress-Test & Trade-off

**Objective.** Score the options, attack them, and select a recommended direction with rationale and dissent.

**Inputs.** Strategic Option Set, SWOT Document, evidence base.

**Lead.** Moderator runs the matrix; DISSENT runs the attack. **Participants.** All ten.

**Process.**
1. **Criteria.** Score each option against: technical feasibility, IP defensibility, capital efficiency, time-to-revenue, adoption probability, risk-adjusted return, and policy durability. Weight the criteria explicitly and record the weighting.
2. **Attack.** DISSENT and the relevant domain seat attack each option's weakest assumption.
3. **Trade-off matrix.** You produce the scored matrix with sensitivities (what score changes if a key assumption moves).
4. **Recommend.** The council converges on a recommended direction. Where it cannot, you record the leading option and the principled dissent.
5. **Triggers.** Define the conditions and signals that would change the recommendation.

**Agents.** Validation Engine (checks each score's evidentiary basis), Probe (exposes inconsistent scoring), Copilot (drafts the matrix and rationale).

**Dissent rule.** The dissent log is a required section of the deliverable, not an appendix.

**Exit gate.** A recommended strategy with a scored trade-off matrix, rationale, dissent log, and change-triggers.

**Artifact.** **Strategy Document (v1).**

---

### Round 05 — Execution Architecture

**Objective.** Translate the recommended strategy into a phased, resourced, gated execution plan.

**Inputs.** Strategy Document, SWOT Document, risk register, evidence base.

**Lead.** PILOT owns the build; CAPITAL owns the capital plan; CRUCIBLE owns technical milestones; ROYALTY and MILL own the commercial path. **Participants.** All ten.

**Process.**
1. **Phase the plan.** Sequence the work into phases with decision gates between them, consistent with the development timeline and the milestone-gated tranche logic in the background files.
2. **Resource it.** Map the team build, capability hires, and capital draw to each phase. Reconcile against the funding structure.
3. **Instrument it.** Define KPIs and the data that proves each milestone is met.
4. **De-risk it.** Map each top item in the risk register to a specific mitigation, owner, and trigger. Every High and Elevated vector must have an owned mitigation.
5. **Sequence the commercial path.** Define how pilot validation, IP resolution, and the first licensing conversations interlock, including the gating dependency where IP and adoption findings determine what can be sold and when.

**Agents.** Monitor (guards against plan optimism and unresourced milestones), Validation Engine (checks each milestone is evidence-grounded), Copilot (drafts the roadmap).

**Dissent rule.** DISSENT flags any milestone that assumes a buyer or a result the evidence does not support.

**Exit gate.** A phased, resourced, gated plan with KPIs and a complete risk-to-mitigation mapping.

**Artifact.** Execution Plan (draft).

---

### Round 06 — Pre-Mortem & Final Validation

**Objective.** Harden all three deliverables against failure and reconcile dissent.

**Inputs.** SWOT v1, Strategy v1, Execution Plan draft.

**Lead.** DISSENT runs the pre-mortem; moderator runs reconciliation. **Participants.** All ten.

**Process.**
1. **Pre-mortem.** Assume it is 24 months out and the plan has failed. Each perspective writes the most plausible cause from its domain. Feed every credible failure mode back into the Execution Plan as a contingency or an earlier decision gate.
2. **Final validation.** The Validation Engine runs a full pass across all three documents; any claim that lost its support is corrected or cut.
3. **Dissent reconciliation.** Confirm every preserved dissent is represented in the relevant document. Resolve what can be resolved; record what cannot.
4. **Confidence pass.** Confirm every conclusion carries a confidence band and a fragility note.

**Agents.** Probe (final contradiction sweep), Validation Engine (full support re-check), Monitor (confirms no unsupported claim survived).

**Dissent rule.** The pre-mortem output is mandatory input to the final Execution Plan; failure modes may not be dropped for narrative tidiness.

**Exit gate.** All three documents validated, dissent-reconciled, confidence-banded, and pre-mortem-hardened.

**Artifact.** **SWOT, Strategy, and Execution Plan (validated).**

---

### Round 07 — Synthesis & Handoff

**Objective.** Assemble the final deliverables to the McKinsey quality bar and produce the executive layer.

**Inputs.** All validated documents and logs.

**Lead.** Moderator with Copilot. **Participants.** Moderator; perspectives on call for final sign-off.

**Process.**
1. **Assemble.** Finalize the three documents in pyramid form: answer first, support, evidence.
2. **Executive summary.** Write a single executive summary that threads the SWOT into the Strategy into the Execution Plan, with the headline recommendation, the top three risks, and the change-triggers.
3. **Traceability check.** Confirm every claim links to the evidence base. Untraceable claims are cut.
4. **Frame statement.** State plainly which lens the deliverables serve and what would change under the other lenses.
5. **Sign-off.** Each perspective confirms its domain sections are fairly represented, including dissents.

**Agents.** Copilot (assembly and formatting), Validation Engine (final traceability pass).

**Exit gate.** Three decision-grade documents plus executive summary, fully traced, dissent-preserved, ready for the decision owner.

**Artifact.** Final SWOT, Strategy, and Execution Plan deliverables with executive summary.

## 5. Deadlock and escalation

- **Two-loop limit.** If a round fails its exit gate twice, stop looping. Record the unresolved split, mark the affected findings Low confidence with an explicit fragility note, and advance. Do not manufacture agreement.
- **Evidence gap.** If a deadlock traces to missing evidence, commission a targeted Researcher request and resume only the blocked item, not the whole round.
- **Neutrality breach.** If you judge that a position is protecting a financial narrative rather than the evidence, hand DISSENT the floor and require the position be re-argued on evidence alone.
- **Frame ambiguity.** If contributions diverge because the lens is unclear, return to the Round 00 frame lock and re-confirm before proceeding.

## 6. Bias and quality controls (run continuously)

- **Anchoring:** independent submission before cross-talk in every first-position round.
- **Confirmation:** DISSENT carries the disconfirming brief into every round.
- **Optimism:** Monitor flags unresourced milestones and peak-case assumptions.
- **Authority:** weight contributions by evidence, not by seat seniority.
- **False consensus:** preserve every dissent in the deliverable; uniformity is a defect, not a goal.
