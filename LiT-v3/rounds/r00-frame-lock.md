---

## 1. Decision owner and lens

- **Decision owner:** a prospective seed investor evaluating the offer currently on the table.
- **The offer:** $2,000,000 for 13.33 percent of Lithium Technologies, Inc. (LiT), implying a $13,000,000 pre-money / $15,000,000 post-money valuation.
- **Lens:** independent advisor to that investor, neutrality-bound. Not LiT management, not the founders, not a promoter of the round.
- **The decision being informed:** invest, decline, or invest with conditions.
- **The downstream product if the investor proceeds:** a value-creation and diligence roadmap (the Execution Plan deliverable).

Every finding and recommendation in this deliberation is made from the investor's standpoint. Where evidence would read materially differently for LiT management, that divergence is reserved for a brief note in the final frame statement (Round 07) and is not re-argued throughout.

## 2. The three deliverables (all the investor's)

1. **SWOT** — LiT assessed as an investment, MECE and evidence-traced.
2. **Strategy** — the recommendation: invest, decline, or invest with conditions, with options weighed (pass, invest at terms, invest with milestone-gated conditions, renegotiate), a scored trade-off matrix, and a preserved dissent log.
3. **Execution Plan** — the investor's action plan if proceeding: diligence to close, deal structure and conditions precedent, milestone-gated capital staging with the recovery-validation milestone as a funding gate, governance, value-creation levers, monitoring KPIs, exit path, and risk-to-mitigation mapping.

## 3. Binding established facts (sponsor stipulations ingested)

The council confirms it has ingested `background/LiT_Sponsor_Stipulations.md` and accepts the following as binding established facts from Round 00 onward. These settle facts; the council still weighs their implications and preserves dissent on those implications.

1. **No clean, exclusive IP to license.** LiT's position is a pending PCT application plus an examiner's written opinion. A PCT preserves a priority date and a national-phase runway; it is not a granted patent and confers no exclusive rights in any jurisdiction. The IP-moat weakness is a confirmed fact, not a contingent, data-room-dependent finding. State it plainly; remove hedges that treat it as unresolved.

2. **The recovery improvement is undefined and unvalidated.** The headline recovery uplift that drives the entire revenue model is, at present, an unvalidated assumption, not an established performance number. Lab-scale and pilot-scale validation of it is the explicit use of the seed funds. The model's dependence on this single unvalidated input is a load-bearing weakness; the seed raise exists to retire that risk.

3. **Revenue-model inputs are aggressive and compounding, with one binding correction.** Every quantitative input to the revenue model is aggressive and the errors compound. **Correction (binding):** any finding that states or implies the NSR royalty ignores commodity pricing is incorrect. A net smelter return royalty is by definition a percentage of net realized metal value and is therefore fully indexed to commodity prices. The correct framing is that the royalty revenue base is **fully exposed to commodity-price volatility**, and that exposure compounds with the other aggressive inputs rather than standing apart from them.

The Stipulation 3 correction is applied everywhere the NSR characterization appears, in every round and every deliverable.

## 4. Standing rules carried into every round

- **Frame fidelity** — reason from the investor's standpoint.
- **Independent before interactive** — first positions are collected blind; no anchoring.
- **Evidence discipline** — every claim tagged Cited / Assumption (with confidence band) / Unsupported. Unsupported claims may not enter a deliverable. New facts route through the Moderator as research requests, never inline.
- **Steelman gate** — no rejection recorded until the target position is restated to the originator's satisfaction.
- **Dissent preservation** — every minority position is captured with its rationale; false consensus is a defect.
- **Neutrality watch** — the engagement may originate from a party holding or considering a position in LiT. This is treated as a neutrality hazard, not a thesis. DISSENT polices reasoning that protects a financial narrative over the evidence.
- **Conflict of interest** — no perspective softens an adverse finding to protect an investment narrative.
