The Deliberation Construct: Four Rounds to a Hardened Thesis

The operational design for the FedRAMP wedge deliberation. Each round has one objective, a defined sequence, and a hard exit condition. The deliberation feeds the strategy document. It does not write it.
Scope

What This Construct Does and Does Not Do

The objective is to design the company that enters the FedRAMP market at Series A and builds a defensible position through Series C, targeting cloud-native SaaS companies pursuing their first federal authorization. The deliberation must resolve the inputs that determine what to build first, who to sell to first, what to say to them, and what compounding advantage to build.

The Boundary

The deliberation feeds the strategy document. It does not write it.

The output is a set of resolved tensions, validated hypotheses, surviving claims, and explicit open questions. A separate downstream step synthesizes those into the strategy. If any round starts producing strategy prose, such as roadmaps or pricing tables, that is scope creep and the round has lost its way. Each round's job is to harden the foundation the strategy will later stand on.

The Construct

Four Rounds, Four Objectives, Four Exits

Round
1
RESEARCH
Objective
Each member commits an independent, written position on the five anchored conclusions and the central tension, through their own lens, with no cross-talk. Surface disagreement honestly before social dynamics smooth it over.

Participant Actions

Each of the six members produces a position statement (under 400 words) covering: which of the five anchored conclusions they accept, doubt, or reject and why; their single sharpest concern about the wedge as framed; and one falsifiable claim they will stake in their role. No member reads another's statement until all six are committed.

Do Not

Discuss, rebut, or converge. This round is parallel and silent.

Exit Condition

Six committed position statements exist, and at least three distinct points of disagreement are explicitly named. If fewer than three surface, the round failed: the conclusions are being accepted too easily and must be pushed harder.

Hands to Round 2: the disagreement map and the six staked claims.
Round
2
DISCUSS
Objective
Produce the strongest articulation of the wedge, then subject it to financial and channel reality. The output is a refined wedge thesis with soft spots identified, not defended away.

Sequence (Strict Order)

  • Advocates present (Mariana + James). Build the strongest integrated case: what to build first, why the combination is defensible, why cloud-native Series A-C is the right beachhead. Single combined case, under 700 words.
  • Skeptics respond (Diana + Rafael). Diana attacks the economics (TAM ceiling, three-pillar margins, Guide Rail cost-to-serve, the ACV question). Rafael attacks the GTM (channel dependence, sales-cycle cash gap, who the buyer is). Each under 500 words.
  • Advocates rebut once (under 400 words combined), applying the steel-man rule: restate the strongest objection before answering it.
Do Not

Let the Red Team in yet. This is the advocate/skeptic axis only. Do not resolve the TAM tension here. Surface it sharply and carry it forward.

Exit Condition

A written refined wedge thesis exists that states the strongest case, lists every skeptic objection that landed, and marks each as answered, partially answered, or open. No objection may be silently dropped.

Hands to Round 3: the refined wedge thesis with its labeled soft spots.
Round
3
CHALLENGE
Objective
Try to break the wedge. Construct concrete failure scenarios and competitive-response models. The objective is not to be fair, it is to find the conditions under which this company dies, so the strategy can be built to avoid them.

Required Scenarios

Sarah (competitive) constructs at least three: The Acquisition (Vanta buys Paramify before scale), The Fast Follower (an incumbent ships OSCAL-native plus a basic environment layer within 12 months), and The Bundle (Vanta discounts FedRAMP bundled with SOC 2 to a shared prospect).

Connor (regulatory) constructs at least two: The Slip (Phase 3 slips to mid-2027 and the cash gap opens), and The Reference Implementation (the PMO ships or blesses free 20x tooling). For each: state trigger, mechanism of damage, calibrated probability, and survival verdict.

Do Not

Propose fixes. Round 3 names the kill conditions; it does not solve them. Solving happens in Round 4. And no fairness theater: softening the attack defeats the round's purpose.

Exit Condition

Every scenario documented with trigger, mechanism, probability, and a verdict (survives / survives-if / fatal). At least one scenario must be rated fatal as currently framed. If everything survives cleanly, the thesis is being protected rather than tested.

Hands to Round 4: the ranked kill-condition list, each tagged survives / survives-if / fatal.
Round
4
EVOLVE
Objective
Rebuild the wedge thesis so it survives the Round 3 attacks, or explicitly accept the risks it cannot design away. The output is the hardened input package for the strategy document. This is where the deliberation ends.

Participant Actions (Full Committee, Roles Relaxed for Synthesis)

For each fatal and survives-if kill condition, the committee must do exactly one of: restructure the thesis so the condition no longer kills it, hedge the condition with an explicit mitigation and its cost, or accept the risk plainly as one the strategy must carry. Re-test the five anchored conclusions (survived / modified / discarded). Resolve or formally escalate the carried TAM tension, ending as either resolved-with-detail or escalated-with-data-needed.

Do Not

Write the strategy. Produce the inputs to the strategy. "The moat is the OSCAL component-definition library plus LaunchPad because acquisition cannot replicate infrastructure switching cost" is a deliberation output. "Here is our 18-month roadmap and pricing table" is strategy, and out of scope.

Exit Condition (All Five Must Exist)

Hardened wedge thesis; resolved-tensions log; validated-hypotheses list; open-questions list; risk register. When all five exist, the deliberation is complete.

Hands to: the strategy document phase (separate, downstream).
The Deliverable

The Handoff Package

When Round 4 exits, the deliberation emits a single structured handoff. This is the entire deliverable and the only thing that crosses into the strategy phase. Five sections, nothing more.

deliberation-handoff.md

01
Hardened Wedge Thesis
The surviving core claim: what to build, who for, why defensible. Under 250 words.
02
Resolved Tensions
Every objection with its resolution type: restructured, hedged, or accepted.
03
Validated Hypotheses
The falsifiable claims that survived, each with its test condition.
04
Open Questions
What the strategy phase must still resolve, and the data needed to close each.
05
Risk Register
The accepted risks the strategy must carry forward, with triggers and probabilities.
Run Discipline

The Checklist That Keeps It Honest

Round 1 ends with at least three named disagreements, not premature consensus.
Round 2 ends with every skeptic objection labeled answered, partial, or open.
Round 3 ends with at least one fatal kill condition, not everything surviving.
Round 4 ends with all five handoff sections populated.
No round produced strategy prose (roadmaps, pricing tables, plans).
The TAM tension ended as resolved-with-detail or escalated-with-data-needed.
Every claim that left the deliberation carries a falsification condition.

Anti-Patterns That Stop the Run

Premature convergence: members agreeing early to avoid conflict. Strategy leakage: any round drafting the plan instead of resolving inputs. Unfalsifiable claims: "the market is huge" without a condition under which it is false. Silent drops: a Round 2 objection that never gets resolved in Round 4. Fairness theater: the Red Team softening its attacks. Role collapse: an Advocate conceding or a Skeptic agreeing before exit conditions are met.

The Next Step

What Happens After the Deliberation

The handoff package is not the strategy. It is the validated, stress-tested foundation a strategy is built on. Once the deliberation emits its five sections, a separate synthesis step (a fresh Claude Code run or a human strategist) consumes the hardened thesis, the resolved tensions, the validated hypotheses, the open questions, and the risk register, and turns them into the actual strategy document: the positioning, the messaging, the sequenced build plan, the pricing architecture, the go-to-market motion, and the capitalization plan.

Keeping these two steps separate is deliberate. The deliberation's job is to make sure the strategy is built on claims that survived genuine adversarial pressure rather than on optimism. A strategy built directly, without the deliberation, would inherit every unexamined assumption in the briefing materials. A strategy built on the handoff package inherits only what survived the committee. That is the entire point of the construct.
