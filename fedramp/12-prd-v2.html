V2 — Defend & expand

V2: Defending the Wedge When the Incumbent Arrives

The product the company ships once a well-capitalized incumbent has entered the FedRAMP automation space. V2 leans into exactly the things the incumbent cannot quickly replicate, and turns the platform into a channel.
Trigger

Assumed competitive motion

The planning assumption that triggers V2

A Vanta-class incumbent ships OSCAL-native FedRAMP, bundled and discounted

V2 assumes that roughly 18 to 24 months after our launch, a well-capitalized horizontal GRC incumbent (Vanta-class: ~$300M ARR, already 20x certified itself, 16,000 customers) ships a credible OSCAL-native FedRAMP module inside its existing platform. It is "good enough" rather than best-in-class, but it arrives with three advantages we cannot match head-on: breadth (FedRAMP as one framework among thirty-five), an installed base of thousands of customers it can cross-sell, and price, bundling FedRAMP with SOC 2 at a steep combined discount.

A secondary, compatible assumption: the incumbent may instead acquire an OSCAL-native specialist (Paramify-class) to reach the same position faster. Either way, the effect on us is the same. The thing that was our wedge, OSCAL-native generation, is now table stakes. V2 is the response.

The strategic logic of V2

The incumbent can win the net-new authorization market on breadth and price. It cannot quickly replicate two things: a deep, production-validated control-implementation library, and an installed continuous-monitoring base that cannot be migrated without a compliance event. V2 pours everything into those two, plus the one differentiator the incumbent structurally lacks: verifiable decision provenance for auditors.

Summary

What V2 is

V2 is the defend-and-expand product. It stops competing on the now-commoditized OSCAL generation and competes instead on depth (the proprietary component-definition library), stickiness (continuous-monitoring switching costs), breadth-of-frameworks (the expansion tiers the shared NIST substrate makes cheap), provenance (auditor-grade decision lineage), and channel (opening the platform to 3PAOs and advisory partners as a white-label revenue line).

Positioning shift

How the message changes in V2

In MVP and V1 the message was consistency and speed against the manual alternative. In V2, with the incumbent claiming the same OSCAL-native ground, the message moves up a level: depth, provenance, and permanence. "Anyone can generate an OSCAL package now. We hold the validated library that makes yours pass the first time, we prove to your auditor exactly how every decision was made, and we run your monitoring for the life of the system so you never re-solve this." The company stops being the cheaper-faster option and becomes the deeper-safer-permanent one, which is the only defensible ground once generation is commoditized.

Out of scope

Still out of scope

V2 does not chase the incumbent into horizontal breadth (SOC 2, ISO 27001, general GRC). Competing on the incumbent's home ground is the losing move. V2 stays federal-deep rather than going commercial-wide. It also does not pursue the Vanta installed base directly; the greenfield-plus-expansion motion holds.

Dependencies

Dependencies & assumptions

  • A V1 installed base large enough that ConMon switching costs and library depth are already accumulating before the incumbent arrives.
  • The component-definition library architected as platform-resident from the start, so the provenance and depth moats are real rather than retrofitted.
  • Series B capital to fund the expansion-tier build and the channel platform.
  • CMMC, GovRAMP, and FISMA mappings tractable on the shared NIST substrate (validated in V1's first equivalency mapping).
  • 20x High pilot completing on the expected H2 2027 timeline to unlock Class D.
Release criteria

When V2 is done

V2 has succeeded when the installed ConMon base holds against incumbent pressure with sub-5% logo churn, when Expand-tier attach pushes net revenue retention above 110% and blended ACV toward $150K+, when the decision-provenance capability is winning competitive deals on a basis the incumbent cannot match, and when at least three white-label partners are live and generating indirect revenue. At that point the company has converted a commoditization threat into a deeper, more defensible, multi-channel business.
