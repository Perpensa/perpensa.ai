MVP — Land the first customer

MVP: The Consistency Engine for a First 20x Authorization

The smallest product that takes one design-partner customer through a FedRAMP 20x Class C (Moderate) authorization and proves the single-model consistency thesis. Everything that is not on the critical path to that outcome is deferred.
Summary

What the MVP is

An OSCAL-native, single-data-model authorization engine that generates a complete, internally consistent FedRAMP 20x Moderate package (SSP, SAP inputs, SAR inputs, POA&M) from one source of truth, with every piece of evidence captured, hashed, and linked to the control it satisfies. The MVP exists to do one thing: get a single cloud-native SaaS company through a 20x Class C authorization faster and with fewer 3PAO review iterations than the manual alternative, and to prove that the resulting package cannot be internally inconsistent because it is generated from one model.

The MVP thesis in one sentence

First-time authorizations fail on inconsistency between the SSP, boundary, data flows, SAR, and POA&M; a single OSCAL model eliminates that failure mode, and the MVP proves a paying design partner will pay to never face it.

Problem

The problem the MVP solves

A cloud-native SaaS company with a federal contract requirement must achieve FedRAMP authorization or be disqualified. They arrive in crisis: over budget, on a deadline, unable to hire scarce FedRAMP expertise. The dominant alternative is a consultant plus a pile of disconnected Word and Excel artifacts, where the boundary diagram, the data-flow diagram, the control narratives, the assessor's findings, and the remediation plan are authored separately and drift out of sync. That drift is the single most common reason packages are sent back for rework. The MVP removes the drift by construction.

Users

Who the MVP serves

  • Primary: the design-partner customer. One cloud-native Series A-C SaaS company, on AWS GovCloud or Azure Government, pursuing 20x Class C (Moderate), triggered by a real federal contract. The kind of customer who is "ready to sign" and has end customers waiting.
  • Secondary: the customer's compliance lead and lead engineer. The two people who will live in the product daily during the sprint.
  • Adjacent: the 3PAO assessor. Not a user to delight yet, but the MVP must produce packages an assessor can review without friction, because fewer review iterations is the proof point.
Goals

Goals & success metrics

1 partner

Cross-document inconsistencies found in assessor review (the core thesis)

The MVP succeeds if the design partner reaches an assessable package, the assessor confirms the package is internally consistent, and the partner says they would have paid for this. It is explicitly not trying to prove ACV, retention, or channel yet; those are V1 questions.

Scope

Capabilities

CapabilityPriDetail
Single OSCAL data modelP0One canonical model holding catalog, profile (FedRAMP 20x Moderate baseline), component definitions, SSP, and POA&M. Every artifact is a projection of this model; nothing is authored independently.
Authorization-boundary builderP0Define the boundary once; the boundary diagram, data-flow diagram, and SSP narrative all derive from it, so they cannot disagree. This is the highest-leverage failure point in the market.
Evidence capture & linkingP0Ingest evidence (configs, scans, screenshots, attestations), content-address it so each artifact has a verifiable identity, and link each to the control and KSI it satisfies.
Package generationP0Generate the 20x machine-readable package plus human-readable SSP/POA&M from the model. Reconciliation between human and machine forms is guaranteed because both derive from one source.
KSI mapping (core set)P1Map the Moderate KSIs to evidence and surface coverage. MVP targets the readily-automatable KSIs; full 70% automation is a V1 goal.
POA&M generation from findingsP1Generate the POA&M directly from assessor findings using shared model syntax, so findings flow into remediation tracking without re-keying.
Assessor export / handoffP1A clean, reviewable export the 3PAO can work from. Not a collaboration workspace yet, just a defensible handoff.
Single-tenant deploymentP2One customer, one isolated instance. Multi-tenancy is deferred to V1.
Out of scope

Explicitly deferred

The MVP deliberately does not include: managed infrastructure / LaunchPad, full ConMon automation and monthly-deliverable scheduling, the procurement-signal pipeline, multi-framework support (CMMC, GovRAMP, FISMA), an AI-augmented Guide Rail advisory layer at scale, multi-tenancy, billing, and a self-serve onboarding flow. Advisory in the MVP is delivered by a human founder or senior expert working directly alongside the design partner. These are V1 and beyond.

Dependencies

Dependencies & assumptions

  • A signed design-partner customer who is genuinely pursuing 20x Moderate with a real contract trigger.
  • A cooperating 3PAO willing to review the MVP-produced package (the assessment proof depends on this).
  • CR26 / 20x Class C path stable and accepting submissions on the expected timeline.
  • Access to the FedRAMP 20x OSCAL baseline and KSI definitions.
  • At least one senior FedRAMP expert available to deliver hands-on advisory during the sprint.
Release criteria

When the MVP is done

The MVP ships when the design partner has a complete, single-model-generated 20x Moderate package; a 3PAO has reviewed it and confirmed internal consistency; review iterations came in at or below two; and the partner has validated they would pay for the product. At that point the company has its first reference customer and the evidence to build V1 against a real ACV question.
