V1 — The repeatable commercial product

V1: From One Authorization to a Repeatable Compliance Platform

The product that can be sold and delivered repeatably, not just to a design partner. V1 turns the authorization sprint into the on-ramp for the recurring infrastructure subscription that carries the business.
Starting point

Expected minimal launch state (what MVP delivered)

Inherited from the MVP

V1 begins from this baseline

The MVP shipped a single OSCAL data-model engine that generated a complete, internally consistent 20x Class C (Moderate) package, an authorization-boundary builder from which the boundary diagram, data-flow diagram, and SSP narrative all derive, and evidence capture with content-addressed linking to controls and the core KSI set. It took one design-partner customer through a full authorization with a cooperating 3PAO, reaching an assessable package in two or fewer review cycles with zero cross-document inconsistencies found.

What the MVP did not have, and what V1 must add: no managed infrastructure, no ConMon automation, no multi-tenancy, no self-serve onboarding, no procurement signal, no productized advisory. Advisory was a human founder working beside the customer. V1's job is to make all of that repeatable and to open the recurring revenue line.

Summary

What V1 is

V1 is the commercially complete compliance infrastructure platform: it lands a customer through the authorization sprint, then expands them into a recurring subscription built on continuous-monitoring automation and inheritable compliant infrastructure. V1 is where the business model becomes real, the authorization is priced as customer acquisition near cost, and the recurring Operate tier becomes the revenue.

The shift from MVP to V1

MVP proved the package can be consistent. V1 proves the company can repeatably land customers and convert each into a recurring infrastructure relationship that the customer cannot easily leave once continuous monitoring is live.

Out of scope

Deferred to V2

V1 does not include: deep multi-framework expansion beyond a first CMMC-equivalency mapping, a white-label / partner platform, the decision-provenance audit-lineage product as a standalone differentiator, FedRAMP High (Class D) support, or any defensive repositioning against a major competitor. Those are V2, triggered by competitive motion.

Dependencies

Dependencies & assumptions

  • MVP reference customer and a working 3PAO relationship to seed the channel.
  • Series A capital sufficient to fund the heavier V1 build and an 18-24 month path to recurring revenue at scale.
  • 20x Phase 3 open and processing Moderate at volume (migration product is the hedge if it slips).
  • Senior FedRAMP advisory talent hired to seed Guide Rail and hold the advisor ratio.
  • Cloud provider control-inheritance data (Azure Policy initiatives, AWS GovCloud mappings) accessible and current.
Release criteria

When V1 is done

V1 ships as a sellable product when a new customer can be onboarded without founder heroics, taken through an authorization sprint, and converted to a recurring Operate subscription with live ConMon automation; when KSI automation clears the 70% threshold; and when at least one 3PAO partnership is producing qualified referrals. The decisive read is the first-cohort blended ACV: V1 has succeeded commercially only if it clears $100K and shows the expansion path into the Operate and Expand tiers.
