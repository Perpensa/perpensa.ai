how it works

Evidence goes in sealed. A judgment comes out defensible.

Perpensa runs a structured deliberation over your decision and keeps a complete, drillable record of how it got there. Here's the motion, the trust model underneath it, and why what you walk away with is yours.

the motion

Research, discuss, challenge, evolve

A deliberation moves through four phases. You're in the room the whole time — interject, redirect, summon evidence, or overrule. The phases give the argument structure; you keep control of it.

01 · research

Branch on what matters

Inquiry that reacts to its own findings, forking toward the live questions rather than a fixed outline. Each source is captured and sealed as it arrives.

02 · discuss

Argue the options

Distinct expert voices make the strongest case for and against. Tensions are surfaced and held, not smoothed into a bland average.

03 · challenge

Attack the conclusion

A dedicated red team runs premortems and stress tests on the emerging position — assumptions, incentives, failure modes — before reality does.

04 · evolve

Revise and record

The position updates in response to what survived. The final judgment ships with its reasoning lineage attached — the path is the product as much as the answer.

the vault

You can't defend a decision on evidence that moved

Most tools treat sources as disposable — a link, a paste, a snapshot that drifts. Perpensa treats the evidence behind a consequential decision as something that has to hold up later: in a board review, an audit, a post-mortem when the call turns out to matter.

Sealed the day you saw it

When research captures a source — a filing, a page, a transcript, a chart — the vault freezes it: tamper-evident, version-locked, and provably the thing you saw on the date you saw it. Alter it and the seal breaks loudly; nothing changes quietly.

This matters most exactly when it's least convenient. A competitor goes private and pulls its filings; a vendor quietly rewrites a pricing page; a study gets retracted. Your decision still rests on what was true at the time — and you can show it.

Every conclusion in Perpensa drills down to a sealed source. That's not a feature bolted on the side; it's the floor the whole reasoning chain stands on.

the trust model

Three planes, never confused

The architecture keeps three kinds of thing strictly apart, because conflating them is how reasoning systems quietly lie. What's evidence stays frozen; what's interpreted stays rebuildable; what's asserted stays versioned.

frozen

Evidence

Raw captured bytes in the vault. Immutable. Provably the thing you saw, on the day you saw it.

rebuildable

Interpretation

Tags, extractions, graph edges, the deliberation lineage. Derived from evidence — and fully reconstructable from it.

living, versioned

Knowledge

The curated assertions you stand behind. Evolves as you learn, but every past state is recoverable. Decisions pin a snapshot.

evidence → interpretation → knowledge · trust never flows backward

templates

Methodology, made executable — and yours

The deliberation engine is strong for general business decisions. Its real leverage shows when you overlay a domain on top of it: a sector's vocabulary, its expert personas, its challenge rubric, its red-team prompts, all configured once and re-run forever.

A library that gets smarter with every engagement

Run a capital-committee review, or a PE diligence pass on an acquisition. The reusable residue of that work — the persona set, the calibrations, the rubric that actually separated good calls from confident-sounding wrong ones — gets extracted as an overlay. The next deal of that shape runs at a fraction of the effort.

No single template is the moat. The compounding library is: dozens of calibrated overlays, tuned by real outcomes, is something no competitor reproduces from a demo. The instrument sharpens as you use it.

An asset on your books, not a slide in someone's deck

An overlay isn't a framework described in a PowerPoint. It's a configured, auditable instrument you can open, inspect line by line, version as your thesis shifts, and re-run on demand. It behaves like software you own, because it is.

That's the whole difference from consulting. A firm sells you the output of its method and keeps the method. Perpensa puts the method in your hands.
