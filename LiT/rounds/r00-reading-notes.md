## CRUCIBLE — Process Metallurgist / Separation Scientist

**What the evidence establishes.**
- LiT claims a two-stage platform: Stage One, a surface-chemistry physical separation effective across the full particle range including fine and ultrafine fractions; Stage Two, a low-temperature leach operating "well below 500C" versus 1,000C-plus conventional, with roughly 30% lower energy, ~20% faster, and no highly acidic byproducts. **Cited** (Prospectus 47-49; Business Plan 171-197).
- The headline performance claim is a "16% to 19%" recovery improvement over conventional methods, stated at bench scale, and a "feedstock-agnostic" capability bench-validated across lithium spodumene, REE hard-rock ores, and tailings. The chemical stage is bench-proven for lithium only; REE and other streams are R&D. **Cited** (Business Plan 32-37, 119, 177-197; Prospectus 47-49).
- The only executed technical agreement in the base is the VT Sponsored Research Agreement: a fixed-price $52,075 study, period of performance March 11 to June 7, 2024, scoped in Attachment A as (1) a literature/patent review of lithium-from-spodumene routes, (2) exploratory preconcentration testing of Virginia-area tailings, and (3) one or more conceptual flowsheets with a preliminary block flow diagram and material balance. Deliverables are technical reports plus a next-phase plan. VT "will not guarantee any particular outcome or specific yield" and disclaims all warranties. **Cited** (SRA 8-9, 245-272, 93-97).
- The development plan targets pilot-scale testing of physical separation at Month 10 and of chemical separation (Li), REE, and tailings at Month 16, with a scale-up engineering study at Months 13–36. **Cited** (Prospectus 124-137; Business Plan 268-279).

**What it leaves open.**
- The "16–19%" figure is undefined: percentage points versus relative percent, against which baseline process, on which feedstock, and at what particle size. **Unsupported** as stated.
- The locked base contains no primary bench dataset, recovery curve, grade-recovery tradeoff, or feedstock assay. The VT study as scoped is a conceptual/desk study plus exploratory preconcentration, which does not by itself substantiate validated recovery across three feedstocks. The gap between "bench-validated" (Prospectus) and the actual contracted scope is the central technical question. **Assumption (High)** that primary bench data exists only outside the locked base.
- Whether LiT's Stage One surface chemistry is mechanistically distinct from established fine-particle methods (the research names Jameson Cell, IsaMill, Metso Concorde Cell for fine/ultrafine, and Yoon's HHS as "no lower particle size limit") is unresolved. **Cited** that the field is crowded (Market Research 104-116, 181-185).
- Whether the sub-500C chemistry differs from already-published low-temperature spodumene routes (NaOH roast at 325C with room-temperature water leach, >99% extraction) is unresolved. **Cited** that such routes are published (Market Research 187-189).

**Decisions these unblock.** Whether the technology claim is underwritable enough to condition a tranche on (binds Round 05 milestone gating); the realistic scope, cost, and duration of pilot validation; and whether the Month 10 / Month 16 pilot timeline is credible for fine-particle flotation scale-up.

**Research requests.**
1. **[Web]** Typical bench-to-pilot timelines, scale-up failure modes, and recovery-degradation patterns for fine/ultrafine froth-flotation and surface-chemistry separation processes, to test the Month 10 pilot claim. *Unblocks: Round 05 milestone realism.*
2. **[Web]** Published low-temperature (sub-500C) spodumene leaching routes and their reported Li extraction, energy, and acid profiles, to benchmark Stage Two novelty against prior art. *Unblocks: technical-differentiation finding in SWOT; CLAIM's FTO input.*
3. **[Company]** LiT's primary bench data defining the 16–19% claim (baseline, feedstock, particle-size basis, grade-recovery data) and any independent third-party validation beyond the VT conceptual study. *Unblocks: confidence band on the core Strength.* Likely `NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL`.

## SPOT — Critical-Minerals Economist

**What the evidence establishes.**
- LiT's revenue is a 1% NSR royalty on the licensee's total mine production value, plus upfront fees. The royalty is therefore directly exposed to commodity-price movement. **Cited** (Prospectus 104-106; Business Plan 344-356).
- The lithium market just ran a violent cycle: output up 192% 2020–2024 with >150kt surplus in 2023 and 2024, prices down 80%+ from late-2022, bottoming near $8,259/t in June 2025, then rebounding to ~$13,003/t by Nov 2025 and ~$26,278/t in early 2026. The 2026 balance is contested (Morgan Stanley deficit ~80kt; UBS ~22kt deficit; Fitch oversupplied; Wood Mackenzie surplus 153kt). **Cited** (Market Research 36-38).
- The new-lithium-project pipeline (the source of upfront-fee customers) collapsed to fewer than 10 feasibility studies in 2025. **Cited** (Market Research 39).
- REE is strategically critical but small in tonnage (196.97 kt in 2025 to 273.30 kt by 2031, 5.61% CAGR), China-dominated (~60% mining, ~91% refining). **Cited** (Market Research 44-46).
- Tailings is physically vast (~16 Bt/yr produced, ~282 Bt inventory) with management/circular-economy markets in the tens of billions, but adoption is early (only ~25% of qualified persons have evaluated reprocessing). **Cited** (Market Research 50-54).
- Model assumptions: 10% penetration of accessible small/mid-tier operators; small-operator royalty modeled at $2.286M/yr (1% of $229M production value); major at $22.86M/yr (1% of $2.29B); new operators at 50% royalty in signing year. **Cited** (Business Plan 354, 411-413; Financials 29-34).

**What it leaves open.**
- The derivation of the per-operator NSR base ($229M small / $2.29B major) is not shown. Whether these reflect total mine production value, the relevant commodity, or realistic mid-tier output is unverified. **Unsupported** as derived.
- The model holds royalty value flat across years; it does not appear to flex the NSR base for the documented 3x lithium price swing. The sensitivity of Year 5 revenue and the exit to a price downcycle is not modeled in the base. **Assumption (High)** that no price sensitivity is embedded.
- Whether "1% of total mine production" (whole-mine NSR) versus "1% of incremental production enabled by LiT" is what an operator would actually concede materially changes the revenue base. The deck assumes whole-mine NSR. **Cited** that whole-mine NSR is the stated basis (Prospectus 104-106).

**Decisions these unblock.** The realistic revenue-base range and its volatility band (binds Strategy market sizing and Round 04 sensitivity); whether feedstock sequencing should lead with lithium, REE, or tailings given price and tonnage; and whether the exit math survives a commodity downcycle.

**Research requests.**
1. **[Web]** Consensus 2026–2030 lithium and REE price and supply/demand-balance forecasts with ranges, to build a price-sensitivity band on the royalty base. *Unblocks: Round 04 revenue sensitivity; exit realism.*
2. **[Web]** Typical whole-mine NSR royalty rates conceded by operators for process-improvement technology versus government/streaming NSR norms, to test whether 1% of total production is realistic versus 1% of incremental. *Unblocks: revenue-base validity.*
3. **[Company]** The build-up behind the $229M and $2.29B per-operator NSR bases (which operators, which commodities, which output). *Unblocks: confidence band on revenue.* Likely `NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL`.

## CLAIM — IP & Patent Strategist

**What the evidence establishes.**
- The prospectus and business plan repeatedly claim an "internationally filed PCT patent" covering the integrated two-stage process, with "sufficient specificity" to protect each stage independently. **Cited** (Prospectus 45, 127, 201; Business Plan 77, 169, 199-201).
- The independent research found LiT's own patent page hosted at a "provisionalpatent" path and no published LiT PCT in a public-database search; a PCT publishes ~18 months after priority and is not a granted, enforceable patent in any jurisdiction. **Cited** (Market Research 191-193).
- The surface-chemistry separation core overlaps Virginia Tech's IP. Dr. Roe-Hoan Yoon (70+ patents; HHS process for fine-particle recovery, "no lower particle size limit") has the HHS branch licensed worldwide to Minerals Refining Company; relevant patents (Yoon, Luttrell) are assigned to Virginia Tech Intellectual Properties, Inc. (VTIP). **Cited** (Market Research 181-185).
- The VT Sponsored Research Agreement resolves several ownership facts directly. The PI is Dr. Yoon. VT owns all VT-personnel IP; the Sponsor gets only a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free license **for internal research purposes** in VT IP. To commercialize, the Sponsor must elect a non-exclusive or a royalty-bearing exclusive license within 6 months of a disclosure and then sign a separate license within 3 months, or rights expire. VTIP may otherwise license VT IP to third parties. **Cited** (SRA 126-153).
- The Sponsor's named Background IP is "Extraction and Refinement of Lithium into Lithium Carbonate and Lithium Hydroxide Utilizing Flexible Feedstock and Non-Acidic Reduced-Temperature Leaching Methods" — i.e., the Stage Two chemistry, not the Stage One surface-chemistry separation. **Cited** (SRA 160).
- The low-temperature lithium field is densely published and partly patented by others (e.g., 325C NaOH roast / room-temp leach, >99% extraction; patented alpha-spodumene low-temp NaOH route). **Cited** (Market Research 187-189). IP budget across US/Australia/India/Brazil is $235K. **Cited** (Prospectus 152; Business Plan 464).

**What it leaves open.**
- Whether LiT holds a license, an option, or nothing to the VT/Yoon surface-chemistry IP that underlies Stage One. The only executed instrument grants internal-research rights only and required a separate commercialization license that is not in the base. **Assumption (High)** that no executed commercial license to VT IP exists in the locked base.
- Whether LiT's Stage Two background IP is filed, provisional, PCT-stage, or granted, and its claim scope versus the published prior art. **Unsupported** beyond the "provisional/PCT" inference.
- Freedom to operate on Stage One given MRC's worldwide HHS license and VTIP assignment. **Cited** that the overlap exists; FTO conclusion open.

**Decisions these unblock.** Whether the "globally patented platform" claim is defensible as written (binds SWOT Strengths/Weaknesses and Strategy IP posture); whether an IP remediation path (license election, separate VTIP license, design-around, or trade-secret) is a precondition to proceed; and whether $235K is adequate for contested multi-jurisdiction prosecution.

**Research requests.**
1. **[Web]** Whether any LiT (Lithium Technologies, Inc. / Keenum) PCT or national-phase application has now published, with claim scope if available. *Unblocks: granted-vs-provisional confidence band.*
2. **[Web]** Yoon/Luttrell/VTIP fine-particle surface-chemistry and HHS patent family and the scope of the Minerals Refining Company worldwide license, to assess FTO and overlap. *Unblocks: Stage One defensibility and FTO finding.*
3. **[Web]** Published and patented sub-500C spodumene leaching prior art mapped against LiT's named Stage Two background IP title. *Unblocks: Stage Two novelty assessment.*
4. **[Company]** Any executed VTIP commercial/exclusive license, LiT patent filing receipts, and the IIT (ISM) Dhanbad agreement and its IP terms. *Unblocks: ownership and FTO certainty.* Likely `NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL`.

## ROYALTY — Licensing Deal Architect

**What the evidence establishes.**
- Model: upfront $2.0M per small/mid-tier ($250K exclusivity retainer + $1.75M at signing) and $10.0M per major, plus 1% NSR ongoing. LOI retainer credited against the license fee. **Cited** (Prospectus 102-106; Business Plan 336-342).
- The 1% NSR basis is deliberately chosen for verifiability and to avoid improvement-based measurement disputes; it is benchmarked to process-tech licensors UOP/Honeywell, Hatch, Metso. **Cited** (Business Plan 344-352; Prospectus 171-174).
- Comparable precedents in the research: NovaCell flotation licensed to Jord International; the Jetti catalytic-leaching copper analog (commercial at Pinto Valley since 2019); DLE's technology-as-a-service / build-own-operate segment leads at 36% share. **Cited** (Market Research 116, 124-126, 73-75).
- The plan assumes first license executed at Month 25, second/third-tier at Months 25–36, reaching 16 licensees (15 small/mid + 1 major) by Year 5. **Cited** (Prospectus 124-137, 170; Business Plan 360, 405).

**What it leaves open.**
- Whether an operator will pay $2M/$10M upfront plus 1% of whole-mine NSR for a pre-pilot, pre-granted-patent process. The upfront-plus-whole-mine-royalty structure is aggressive against the comparables, where royalties typically attach to product made with the licensed technology. **Assumption (Moderate)** that the structure is above market as written.
- LOI-to-license conversion rates, sales-cycle length, and exclusivity mechanics are not evidenced. The model implies retainer-to-signature within months. **Unsupported** as to timing.
- Whether licensing-first beats a hybrid or build-own-operate model given that the best-funded tailings comparator (Phoenix Tailings, $117.8M) captures full metal margin while LiT captures 1%. **Cited** that the structural contrast exists (Market Research 131, 210-212).

**Decisions these unblock.** The recommended deal architecture and whether terms should change as a condition of proceeding (binds Strategy model and Round 04); the realistic time-to-first-license (binds Round 05 commercial sequencing and the tranche-to-revenue bridge).

**Research requests.**
1. **[Web]** Process-technology licensing norms in mining/metals: typical upfront fees, royalty bases (product-of-licensed-process vs whole-mine NSR), and LOI-to-license conversion timelines (UOP, Hatch, Metso, Jetti, NovaCell analogs). *Unblocks: deal-structure realism and time-to-revenue.*
2. **[Web]** Technology-as-a-service / build-own-operate versus pure-licensing economics for recovery technologies sold into miners (DLE precedent, Phoenix Tailings producer model). *Unblocks: business-model option set for Round 03.*
