## CRUCIBLE — Process Metallurgist / Separation Scientist (leads S / W)

### Strengths

**CRUCIBLE-S1 — Two-stage architecture is coherent and addresses a real metallurgical gap (fine/ultrafine loss).**
Conventional flotation loses recovery on fine and ultrafine particles (the elephant-curve problem); a surface-chemistry route with "no lower particle size limit" targets a genuine, well-documented loss zone, and pairing it with a lower-temperature leach is a logical flowsheet. *Tag: Cited (Business Plan 171-197; Market Research 104-116, 181-185). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the problem LiT aims at is real, so the thesis is not a solution in search of a problem; the question is ownership and proof, not relevance. *Fragility: collapses to a Weakness if the mechanism is not distinct from incumbent fine-particle methods.*

**CRUCIBLE-S2 — Stage Two low-temperature leach direction is directionally credible against the physics.**
Operating "well below 500C" versus 1,000C-plus calcination is consistent with published sub-500C spodumene routes, so the energy-reduction direction is physically plausible rather than fantastical. *Tag: Cited (Business Plan 171-197; research/r00-lowtemp-spodumene-priorart.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the energy claim is not disqualified on first principles; it is a novelty and substantiation question, not a feasibility one.

**CRUCIBLE-S3 — Feedstock-agnostic framing, if it survives pilot, widens the addressable feedstock set.**
A single physical-separation front end claimed to work across spodumene, REE hard rock, and tailings would let one platform attack multiple streams. *Tag: Assumption (Low) — bench-proven for lithium only; REE and other streams are R&D (Business Plan 32-37, 119, 177-197). Confidence: Low.* **So-what:** this is an option value, not a present capability; it should be scored as upside contingent on multi-feedstock pilot data, not as an existing strength.

### Weaknesses

**CRUCIBLE-W1 — The headline "16% to 19% recovery improvement" is undefined and therefore not underwritable as stated.**
The figure does not specify percentage points versus relative percent, the baseline process, the feedstock, or the particle-size basis. *Tag: Unsupported as stated (Business Plan 32-37; Prospectus 47-49). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the core performance claim cannot be converted into a revenue or recovery model until it is defined and sourced; a capital provider cannot gate a tranche on a number with no basis. *Fragility: would upgrade to a defined Strength if primary bench data (C1) is retrieved with baseline and basis specified.*

**CRUCIBLE-W2 — No primary bench dataset exists in the locked base; "bench-validated across three feedstocks" outruns the executed record.**
The only executed technical instrument is the VT study: a fixed-price $52,075, three-month conceptual/desk study (literature/patent review, exploratory tailings preconcentration, conceptual flowsheets), with VT guaranteeing no outcome or yield. *Tag: Cited (SRA 8-9, 245-272, 93-97); the validation gap is Assumption (High).* **So-what:** the central technical Strength rests on data the council cannot see; until C1 is retrieved, validated recovery must be treated as an assumption, not a fact.

**CRUCIBLE-W3 — Stage Two is not category-novel; it sits in a crowded, partly-patented low-temperature field.**
Published routes already report sub-500C spodumene leaching (e.g., NaOH roast at ~325C with room-temperature water leach, >99% extraction), some patented by others. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 187-189; research/r00-lowtemp-spodumene-priorart.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the leach stage is an incremental improvement at best, which weakens both the technical-moat narrative and the freedom-to-operate position.

**CRUCIBLE-W4 — Pilot timeline (physical separation Month 10, chemical/REE/tailings Month 16) is not credible as a validated-recovery milestone.**
Fine/ultrafine flotation and surface-chemistry analogs show validated scaled recovery years out, with piloted recovery below bench, and operator-qualification cycles measured in multiple years per feedstock. The Month 10/16 dates are campaign-start dates, not dates of a validated number. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-flotation-scaleup.md; research/r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** any milestone gate or tranche release keyed to "pilot complete at Month 16" is mis-specified; it gates on activity, not on proof, and will slip against real scale-up physics.

**CRUCIBLE-W5 — Recovery typically degrades from bench to pilot to commercial on variable real feedstock; the model assumes the bench number holds.**
Real impoundments and ores carry mineralogical variability, slimes, and contaminants that erode surface-chemistry performance at scale. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-flotation-scaleup.md). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the revenue base is built on a recovery uplift that is most likely to be lower at commercial scale than at bench, which should be modeled as a downward sensitivity in Round 04.

## SPOT — Critical-Minerals Economist (leads O / T)

### Opportunities

**SPOT-O1 — Critical-minerals demand and declining ore grades create a structural, multi-decade pull for recovery and reprocessing technology.**
Tailings volumes (~16 Bt/yr; ~282 Bt inventory) and falling head grades make incremental recovery economically attractive independent of any single price. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 50-54). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the demand backdrop is durable even if lithium prices fall, which partially de-risks the thesis from the commodity cycle if the model is sold on economics rather than on peak prices.

**SPOT-O2 — REE is strategically critical and China-concentrated, giving a Western recovery route disproportionate policy and customer attention relative to its tonnage.**
REE mining ~60% and refining ~91% China-dominated; market 196.97 kt (2025) to 273.30 kt (2031), 5.61% CAGR. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 44-46). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** REE is a small but high-salience wedge; it can open doors and government interest that lithium tonnage alone would not, even though it cannot carry the revenue base.

**SPOT-O3 — A first-mover position in tailings reprocessing exists because adoption is early (~25% of qualified persons have evaluated it).**
Low penetration means the category is uncontested enough that an early credible entrant can shape it. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 50-54, 61). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** early-category timing is real upside, but it cuts both ways: low penetration also means a long market-development cost, which belongs in the Threat column.

### Threats

**SPOT-T1 — The 1% whole-mine NSR royalty base is directly exposed to a commodity that swung ~2x to ~3x inside twelve months, and the model embeds no price sensitivity.**
Lithium bottomed near $8,259/t (Jun 2025), rebounded to ~$13,003/t (Nov 2025) and ~$26,278/t (early 2026); the deck holds royalty value flat. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 36; research/r00-li-ree-price-forecasts.md; Financials 26-34). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** Year 5 revenue and the exit are unmodeled against a downcycle; a banded royalty model is mandatory in Round 04 or the returns are not credible. *Fragility: severity depends on the per-operator NSR base (C3-adjacent), which is itself underived.*

**SPOT-T2 — The new-lithium-project pipeline, the source of upfront-fee customers, has collapsed to fewer than 10 feasibility studies in 2025.**
Upfront license fees depend on operators advancing new projects; that pipeline is thin. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 39). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the upfront-fee leg of the revenue model is exposed to a depressed greenfield pipeline, pushing more weight onto the slower royalty leg and onto brownfield/tailings retrofits.

**SPOT-T3 — Consensus 2026 lithium balance is genuinely contested (deficit vs large surplus), so the revenue base has irreducible directional uncertainty.**
Morgan Stanley deficit ~80kt; UBS ~22kt deficit; Fitch oversupplied; Wood Mackenzie surplus 153kt. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 36-38; research/r00-li-ree-price-forecasts.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** no single forecaster can anchor the base; Round 04 must score the recommendation under both a deficit and a surplus world, not pick one.

**SPOT-T4 — REE's small tonnage caps the revenue a 1% NSR can extract even at high strategic value.**
The market is tens of kt, not Mt; 1% of a small production value is a small royalty. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 44-46). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** REE can be a door-opener and a policy hook but cannot be the financial base case; sequencing that leads with REE for revenue would mis-size the opportunity.

## CLAIM — IP & Patent Strategist (leads W / T)

### Strengths

**CLAIM-S1 — A first-filed position on the integrated two-stage process, if a real application exists, has some priority value.**
The deck claims an internationally filed PCT covering the integrated process with stage-independent specificity. *Tag: Assumption (Low) — no published LiT PCT or granted patent was found on the public record; only a possibly-lapsed 2024-03 provisional path (research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md). Confidence: Low.* **So-what:** any priority value is unconfirmed and possibly lapsed; this is admitted to the pool only to be tested, and currently leans Weakness.

### Weaknesses

**CLAIM-W1 — The "globally patented platform" claim is not supported: no granted or published LiT patent is on the public record.**
A PCT publishes ~18 months after priority and is not enforceable in any jurisdiction; the public record shows only a "provisionalpatent" page and no published PCT, with a possibly-lapsed 2024-03 provisional. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 191-193; research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the moat the licensing business is sold on does not exist as represented; a capital provider cannot underwrite a license-to-others business on an unproven, possibly-lapsed filing. This is DISSENT gate (a), uncleared.

**CLAIM-W2 — Stage One surface chemistry overlaps VT/VTIP-owned, MRC-licensed IP; LiT's control of its own core is unestablished.**
The HHS fine-particle process (Yoon, "no lower particle size limit") is assigned to VTIP and licensed worldwide to Minerals Refining Company; the only executed VT instrument grants LiT internal-research rights only and is an option-to-license, not an executed commercial license (SRA Art. 13.4–13.6). *Tag: Cited (Market Research 181-185; SRA 126-160; research/r00-company-queue-disposition.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** if Stage One depends on VT/MRC-encumbered chemistry and no commercial license has been executed, the licensing business may have nothing clean to sell; IP remediation is a likely precondition to proceed.

**CLAIM-W3 — Stage Two background IP sits against dense published and patented prior art, narrowing any defensible claim scope.**
Sub-500C spodumene routes are published and partly patented by third parties. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 187-189; research/r00-lowtemp-spodumene-priorart.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** even if filed, Stage Two claims are likely narrow and design-around-able, weakening the composite moat.

**CLAIM-W4 — The $235K multi-jurisdiction IP budget (US/Australia/India/Brazil) is thin for contested prosecution against encumbered and crowded art.**
*Tag: Cited (Prospectus 152; Business Plan 464). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the budget assumes smooth prosecution; contested FTO and office actions across four jurisdictions plus a VTIP license negotiation will likely exceed it, which should appear as a use-of-proceeds risk.

### Threats

**CLAIM-T1 — VTIP can license the same surface-chemistry IP to third parties, including competitors.**
VTIP "may otherwise license VT IP to third parties"; the option-to-license window can expire if LiT does not elect and sign. *Tag: Cited (SRA 126-153; research/r00-company-queue-disposition.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** LiT's core is not exclusive to it and could be licensed to a better-capitalized rival; exclusivity is a negotiation LiT has not yet won.

**CLAIM-T2 — MRC's pre-existing worldwide HHS license is a freedom-to-operate hazard for LiT's Stage One.**
*Tag: Cited (Market Research 181-185; research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** LiT may need a license or design-around to practice its own front end, which is both a cost and a gating dependency before any customer license can be sold.

## ROYALTY — Licensing Deal Architect (Strategy/Execution lead; contributes across quadrants)

### Strengths

**ROYALTY-S1 — A 1% NSR basis chosen for verifiability avoids the improvement-measurement disputes that sink improvement-based royalties.**
LiT deliberately picked whole-mine NSR over incremental-production measurement to sidestep metering disputes, benchmarked to UOP/Hatch/Metso. *Tag: Cited (Business Plan 344-352; Prospectus 171-174). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the verifiability logic is sound deal design; the problem is the basis, not the principle.

**ROYALTY-S2 — Upfront-plus-royalty with an LOI retainer credited to the license fee is a recognized, fundable process-tech structure.**
*Tag: Cited (Prospectus 102-106; Business Plan 336-342). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the skeleton of the model matches industry practice (UOP/Honeywell, Hatch, Metso, Jetti, NovaCell), so the structure is improvable rather than alien.

### Weaknesses

**ROYALTY-W1 — 1% of whole-mine production value is off-market as a basis; process-tech royalties normally attach to product made with the licensed technology.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-licensing-norms.md; Market Research). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** an operator is unlikely to concede a royalty on its entire mine for a single recovery step; the realistic concession is a royalty on incremental or licensed-process output, which materially shrinks the modeled royalty base and must be repriced in Round 04.

**ROYALTY-W2 — $2M/$10M upfront plus 1% NSR for a pre-pilot, pre-granted-patent process is aggressive against the comparables.**
*Tag: Assumption (Moderate) (Business Plan 336-342; research/r00-licensing-norms.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the terms as written likely compress on contact with procurement; the deck's revenue per license is probably an overstatement.

**ROYALTY-W3 — LOI-to-license conversion and sales-cycle length are unevidenced; the model implies retainer-to-signature in months.**
*Tag: Unsupported as to timing (Business Plan; research/r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md shows multi-year cycles). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** first license at Month 25 and 16 licensees by Year 5 are not credible against documented operator qualification timelines; the commercial ramp is the single largest source of model fragility.

### Opportunities

**ROYALTY-O1 — A hybrid or technology-as-a-service / build-own-operate model could capture far more value than 1% licensing for the same recovery narrative.**
DLE's TaaS/BOO segment leads at 36% share; the best-funded tailings comparator (Phoenix Tailings, $117.8M) is a producer capturing full metal margin. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-businessmodel-economics.md; Market Research 73-75, 131, 210-212). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the chosen model may be leaving most of the value on the table; Round 03 must generate and Round 04 must score hybrid/BOO options against pure licensing.

**ROYALTY-O2 — Customer-as-investor / co-development is a real channel that several majors already use for processing tech.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** a strategic-investor or co-development deal with a target account could simultaneously fund validation, secure a reference site, and de-risk adoption, which is a stronger first move than a cold license sale.

### Threats

**ROYALTY-T1 — Glencore Technology runs a direct license-model business (IsaMill + Albion) and is an incumbent competitor in LiT's exact channel.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** LiT is not entering an empty licensing channel; it faces an established, patent-backed process licensor with existing operator relationships.

## MILL — Mining Operator Voice / Customer (leads O / T)

### Opportunities

**MILL-O1 — "Modular mobile deployment" and low-capital integration, if real, lower the qualification barrier that kills most processing-tech sales.**
*Tag: Assumption (Low) — claimed, not demonstrated at a high-throughput site (Prospectus 68; Business Plan 157-159). **MILL-O2 — Operators facing declining grades and critical-minerals targets have a standing economic reason to evaluate recovery uplift on existing flowsheets.**
*Tag: Cited (Market Research 50-54). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the buyer has a real pain point, so the door is open on economics; the sale is lost or won on proof and switching cost, not on whether the buyer cares.

### Threats

**MILL-T1 — Named target accounts already build in-house, buy incumbent equipment, or fund competing technologies; LiT must displace, not just sell.**
BHP and Freeport back Jetti; BHP backs Ceibo; Metso Concorde and Eriez HydroFloat run at scale (HydroFloat at BHP Carrapateena); all five primary accounts can build/buy/fund-competitor. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 61, 138, 148, 160, 210; research/r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the same logos in LiT's pipeline are also its competitive threat; concentration and self-substitution risk are compounded, and adoption probability must be scored down accordingly.

**MILL-T2 — Operator technology-qualification cycles are multi-year per site and per feedstock, and require validation on the operator's own feedstock.**
HydroFloat ran ~2–3 years pilot-to-commercial per site plus ~a decade invention-to-first-reference; Jetti ~2 years bench-to-deploy; re-qualification is per-operator and per-feedstock. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md; Business Plan 248-252). **MILL-T3 — Switching, qualification, and evaluation costs the deck omits are borne by the operator and raise the adoption hurdle.**
*Tag: Unsupported in the base (the costs exist but are not quantified) (Business Plan). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the operator's all-in cost to adopt is higher than the license fee implies, so the value proposition must clear a hurdle the deck never prices.

**MILL-T4 — A major is structurally reluctant to pay whole-mine NSR to a third party for one recovery step it could replicate or buy as equipment.**
*Tag: Assumption (High) (Market Research 210-212; research/r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the highest-value customers are the least likely to accept the highest-value term; the major-licensee line in the model is the most fragile single assumption in the customer base.

## CAPITAL — Growth Investor & CFO Lens (Strategy/Execution lead; contributes across quadrants)

### Strengths

**CAPITAL-S1 — The financial model is internally reconcilable and the tranche structure is milestone-gated, which is the right control shape for staged risk capital.**
Eight quarterly milestone-gated tranches; Year 5 revenue of $51.148M reconciles to its components; NOL applied Year 3. *Tag: Cited (Financials 26-34, 66-76; Business Plan 429-450). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** the deal gives the provider tranche-level control points, and the arithmetic is consistent; the weakness is in the assumptions feeding it, not the math.

**CAPITAL-S2 — Milestone-tranched financing is now a standardized, well-supported instrument (NVCA terms, Oct 2025), so the control structure is enforceable.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-seed-valuation-norms.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the provider can place risk capital behind specific validation and IP-remediation gates with standard documentation, which is the natural way to underwrite this deal if it proceeds.

### Weaknesses

**CAPITAL-W1 — The base case has no downside scenario; a commodity-and-conversion-gated revenue line is shown as a single optimistic path.**
*Tag: Assumption (High) — no price/conversion/timing sensitivity in the base (Financials 26-34). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** an advisor cannot recommend on a one-path model; the downside, break-even, and First-Chicago scenarios must be built in Round 04 before any verdict.

**CAPITAL-W2 — The 8–10x revenue / 12–15x EBITDA exit multiples are unsupported and likely inapplicable.**
Cleantech trades ~1.8x revenue, software median ~2.6x; recenter to 2–4x; 12–15x EBITDA is an optimistic edge and inapplicable pre-EBITDA; expect upfront-plus-earnout, not a clean multiple; named "comps" are acquirers, and Jetti's $2.5B is a venture mark, not a realized exit. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-exit-multiples.md; Prospectus 162-174). **CAPITAL-W3 — The "no bridge round" self-funding claim is knife-edge and breaks on any first-revenue slippage.**
It requires the first LOI retainer (Months 23–24) to land exactly as the final tranche is drawn (Month 22). *Tag: Cited (Financials 96-98; Business Plan 450). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** given documented multi-year sales cycles, slippage is the base case, not the tail; a bridge is likely required, contradicting the deck and diluting the stake.

**CAPITAL-W4 — At $13M pre-money the deal is seed-priced for pre-seed-level technical and IP risk.**
$13M pre is ~80% of the ~$16M median seed pre-money, but the company carries unvalidated recovery data and unresolved core IP. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-seed-valuation-norms.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the price does not compensate for the stage of risk; a restructured entry (lower pre-money, or tranches gated on C1 validation and IP remediation) is the natural condition of proceeding.

### Threats

**CAPITAL-T1 — Time-to-revenue risk dominates: revenue is gated on licensing conversions the evidence puts years later than the model.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md; Financials). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the entire return depends on a ramp that external evidence says is too fast; this is the master risk that the downside cases in Round 04 must quantify.

## WATERSHED — ESG, Remediation & Regulatory Strategist (leads O; contributes W/T)

### Opportunities

**WATERSHED-O1 — Tightening tailings governance (GISTM, GTMI with third-party audit teeth since Jan 2025) is a real and durable regulatory backdrop.**
*Tag: Cited (Market Research 83, 222-225; research/r00-esg-procurement-driver.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the governance trend is genuine and durable, but it drives dam-safety, filtration, dry-stacking, and governance, not recovery-tech procurement directly; it is a coincident accelerant, so positioning must lead with economics and use ESG as a secondary tailwind.

**WATERSHED-O2 — A credible "process the waste, restore the site" narrative is differentiating for financing, insurance, and social-license, even where it does not by itself close a sale.**
*Tag: Cited (research/r00-esg-procurement-driver.md). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** the mission framing has real value mediated through capital and liability channels; it strengthens fundraising and partner selection more than it shortens the operator sales cycle.

**WATERSHED-O3 — US critical-minerals and tailings-valorisation programs ($1.2B Interior across 60 commodities) create a non-dilutive funding and demand-signal channel.**
*Tag: Cited (Market Research 222-225). Confidence: Moderate.* **So-what:** government programs could fund validation and provide an early reference, partially offsetting the thin private greenfield pipeline.

### Weaknesses

**WATERSHED-W1 — The environmental claims as written fail substantiation standards and create greenwash exposure.**
"No acidic byproducts" is a free-of claim that fails the FTC Green Guides unless scoped to "no highly acidic" with the substitute-reagent trade-off analysed; "~30%" energy reduction is a comparative claim unsupported without a disclosed baseline; "lower carbon footprint/ton" is an implicit LCA claim with no LCA in the base; the Green Guides expressly reach B2B licensing claims, and ISO 14021 Type II requires a documented on-request evidence file (the unretrieved C1). *Tag: Cited (research/r00-green-claims-substantiation.md; Prospectus 27, 72-84). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** all three green claims collapse into the C1 evidence gap; using them in licensing or investor materials without substantiation adds a 10b-5/disclosure surface on top of greenwash risk. Deliverables must use scoped, bench-stage wording, no unqualified absolutes.

### Threats

**WATERSHED-T1 — Reprocessing abandoned/orphaned tailings is a liability hazard, not a clean asset, under strict joint-and-several retroactive CERCLA.**
US default is strict joint-and-several retroactive owner/operator liability attaching when cleanup costs are incurred; the Good Samaritan Act (PL 118-155, Dec 2024) shields CERCLA/CWA but is narrow (federal land only, no profit beyond cost recovery, 15-permit cap, 7-yr pilot, excludes NPL/active mining). *Tag: Cited (research/r00-tailings-liability.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the "rehabilitate abandoned sites" thesis carries inheritable environmental liability; a licensing-first model that keeps owner/operator liability with the miner is the safer structure, and any title/custody/BOO option needs an explicit CERCLA and closure-bond line.

## DISSENT — Parallel Weakness-and-Threat Slate (Red Team)

> Mandated parallel slate so the downside is never under-represented in the pool. These are stated at full adverse strength; they will be steelmanned and challenged in Round 02. Several deliberately sharpen or stack items other seats may raise; that overlap is intentional, not accidental.

### Weaknesses

**DISSENT-W1 — LiT may not control the IP it proposes to license to others; the licensing business may have nothing clean to sell.**
No granted/published LiT patent exists on the public record (only a possibly-lapsed 2024-03 provisional); the Stage One core overlaps VTIP-owned, MRC-worldwide-licensed surface chemistry; the only executed VT instrument is an option-to-license granting internal-research rights only (SRA Art. 13.4–13.6). *Tag: Cited (research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md; research/r00-company-queue-disposition.md; SRA 126-160). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** this is the gating defect (DISSENT gate (a), uncleared by W1). If unresolved, the recommendation space must include "do not proceed" and "restructure/secure IP first"; the central Strength is currently a Weakness.

**DISSENT-W2 — "Bench-validated across three feedstocks" is unsupported; the executed record is one $52,075 three-month conceptual desk study that expired June 7, 2024.**
The VT study was a literature review, exploratory tailings preconcentration, and conceptual flowsheets, with VT guaranteeing no outcome or yield; no primary recovery dataset is in the base; the named IIT (ISM) Dhanbad partner that the model funds with 75% of R&D has no executed agreement in the base. *Tag: Cited (SRA 8-9, 245-272, 93-97; Financials 53-54). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the central technical Strength rests on data the council cannot see and a partner with no contract on file; both must be flagged High-risk and the affected Strengths demoted to assumptions until C1/C3/C4 are retrieved.

**DISSENT-W3 — Every revenue assumption is independently fragile and they are stacked multiplicatively, not additively.**
Whole-mine NSR basis (off-market), upfront terms (aggressive), conversion timing (years short), price (unhedged), recovery (degrades at scale): each is individually weak, and the model multiplies them into one optimistic path. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-licensing-norms.md; r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md; r00-li-ree-price-forecasts.md; r00-flotation-scaleup.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the probability of all five holding simultaneously is far below the base case's implied certainty; the realistic expected value is a fraction of the headline.

**DISSENT-W4 — The deal is seed-priced but pre-seed in de-risking, and the self-funding bridge is a single point of failure.**
$13M pre-money for unvalidated tech and unresolved IP; "no bridge round" requires first revenue and last tranche to coincide to the month. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-seed-valuation-norms.md; Financials 96-98). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the provider is paying a de-risked price for an un-de-risked asset and is told no bridge is needed precisely where the evidence says a bridge is most likely.

### Threats

**DISSENT-T1 — LiT's named customers are simultaneously its competitors and its potential acquirers/funders of substitutes; "customer builds it cheaper" is a live pre-mortem failure mode.**
All five primary accounts can build/buy/fund-competitor; Freeport runs internal leach R&D and backs Jetti; Rio funds ElectraLith/Magmatic; Glencore Technology is a direct license-model competitor. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** customer concentration, competitive displacement, and FTO risk compound; the most-courted accounts are the ones most able to route around LiT.

**DISSENT-T2 — A better-funded producer captures full margin where LiT captures 1%, so the chosen model may be value-destroying relative to alternatives.**
Phoenix Tailings ($117.8M) is a producer taking full metal margin; DLE's market favors TaaS/BOO at 36% share. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 131, 210-212; research/r00-businessmodel-economics.md). Confidence: Moderate-High.* **So-what:** even if every licensing assumption held, 1% NSR may be the wrong instrument; Round 03 must test whether the model itself, not just its terms, should change.

**DISSENT-T3 — The green and remediation claims create legal and disclosure exposure rather than clean differentiation.**
"No acidic byproducts" / "~30%" / "lower carbon footprint" fail substantiation as written and reach B2B and investor materials; abandoned-site remediation carries strict joint-and-several retroactive CERCLA liability. *Tag: Cited (research/r00-green-claims-substantiation.md; r00-tailings-liability.md). Confidence: High.* **So-what:** the mission narrative that sells the thesis is also a litigation and liability surface; it must be scoped and qualified, and the remediation-as-asset framing inverted to remediation-as-liability in the risk register.

**DISSENT-T4 — Neutrality hazard: the originating party holds or is considering a position, and the deck's single optimistic path is the kind of reasoning that serves the position over the evidence.**
*Tag: Cited (Charter 2.2; frame-lock memo). Confidence: Moderate (as a process risk).* **So-what:** the council must keep "do not proceed" and "restructure first" live through Rounds 03–04 and resist any item that softens an adverse finding to protect an investment narrative.
