# Round 03 — Strategic Option Generation (Batched Divergent Unit)

**Round:** 03 Strategic Option Generation
**Unit:** Full strategic option set, all participating seats' proposals sealed and blind, then clustered MECE by the moderator
**Lens:** ADVISOR, prospective capital provider (locked in `rounds/r00-frame-lock.md`)
**Co-leads:** ROYALTY and CAPITAL. **Participants this unit:** CRUCIBLE, SPOT, CLAIM, ROYALTY, MILL, CAPITAL, WATERSHED, CHOKEPOINT (phase-active R01-R04), and DISSENT.
**Not participating (phase scope):** PILOT (active R04-R07; execution-feasibility view enters when options are scored in R04).

> Diverge deliberately. Each seat proposed one or more strategic options independently and blind to every other; no proposal references another seat. Options are stated as coherent directions, not tactics. Each proposal carries its evidence tag (Cited / Assumption with confidence / Unsupported) and the SWOT items and risk-register IDs it is built to address. Because options are forward-looking directions rather than findings, most reasoning is Assumption-tagged and grounded in cited SWOT and Round 00 research; they are scored, attacked, and selected in Round 04, not adopted here.
>
> The decision space spans six axes, stated explicitly so the set can be tested for coverage rather than for variations on the founder's base case:
> - **Axis A - Business model:** licensing-first / hybrid-TaaS / build-own-operate / producer / do-not-proceed / restructure-first
> - **Axis B - Feedstock and market sequencing:** lithium-first / REE-first / tailings-first / multi-stream
> - **Axis C - Geographic and customer focus:** US-and-allied concentration / global majors / single anchor account
> - **Axis D - IP remediation path:** secure VTIP exclusive + FTO / design-around MRC art / trade-secret on integration know-how / acquire
> - **Axis E - Partner vs go-alone:** strategic-investor/co-development anchor / government-backed / independent
> - **Axis F - Capital strategy:** accept seed as offered / restructure terms and tranche triggers / non-dilutive grant-first / strategic round
>
> DISSENT gates open into this round: gate **(a) IP** (R-IP-1 not cleared) and gate **(c) model-choice** (R-REV-9 / O1 deferred here by design). The moderator's MECE set resolves the model-choice contest into scorable directions and preserves the do-not-proceed and restructure-first options at full strength.

## CRUCIBLE — Process Metallurgist / Separation Scientist

**CRUCIBLE-OPT-1 - Validation-gated licensing: prove the recovery number on one feedstock before any commercial promise.**
Sequence the strategy so that an independent, instrumented bench-to-pilot validation campaign on a single, well-characterized feedstock (lithium spodumene tailings) defines the "16-19%" claim (percentage points vs relative, baseline, particle-size basis) and gates every downstream commercial milestone. No license is offered, and no tranche releases past validation, until a defined number is reproduced by a party other than the founder. *Tag: Assumption (High). Built to address R-TECH-1 (gate), R-TECH-2, W2, S2. Axes: B lithium-first, F restructure tranche triggers.* **So-what:** the entire revenue base is a single unproven number; defining and validating it is the cheapest risk-retirement in the plan and must precede, not parallel, commercialization.

**CRUCIBLE-OPT-2 - Narrow-and-deep technical scope: drop "feedstock-agnostic," own one flowsheet completely.**
Concentrate R&D and IP on the fine/ultrafine surface-chemistry front end for one stream where the elephant-curve loss is largest, rather than spreading thin across spodumene, REE, and tailings simultaneously. Treat multi-feedstock reach as future option value unlocked only by sequential pilots. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate). Addresses R-TECH-3, S2, the demoted CRUCIBLE-S2/S3 option value. Axes: B lithium-first, D narrow defensible scope.* **So-what:** a thin moat (R-TECH-3) gets thinner when spread across three streams; depth on one flowsheet is the only route to a defensible technical claim and a credible pilot timeline.

## SPOT — Critical-Minerals Economist

**SPOT-OPT-1 - Sell on structural recovery economics, not on the lithium price deck.**
Position the technology as a grade-and-recovery economics play that pays back on declining head grades and the ~16 Bt/yr tailings stream regardless of where lithium sits in its cycle, and model revenue under an explicit price band (deficit and surplus worlds) rather than a single deck. *Tag: Cited demand backdrop (Market Research 50-54), Assumption on positioning (Moderate-High). Addresses O2, O3, R-REV-3, R-REV-8. Axes: A any, B tailings-first economics framing.* **So-what:** the durable, cycle-independent pull is the recovery-economics story (O2); a strategy anchored to peak lithium prices is the single most fragile thing in the model.

**SPOT-OPT-2 - REE as a strategic wedge, lithium as the revenue base.**
Use REE's policy and customer salience to open doors and attract non-dilutive attention, but never position REE tonnage as the revenue base case; keep lithium-and-tailings volume as the commercial engine. *Tag: Cited (Market Research 44-46), Assumption (Moderate). Addresses O4, R-REV-4, T14. Axes: B multi-stream with lithium primary, C US-and-allied.* **So-what:** REE's small tonnage caps a 1% NSR (T14 / R-REV-4); it is a wedge for access and policy, not a base, and the strategy must use it as the former and not bank it as the latter.

## CLAIM — IP & Patent Strategist

**CLAIM-OPT-1 - IP-remediation-first: convert the option, clear FTO, and narrow the claim before selling anything.**
Make the gating IP work a precondition: exercise or replace the VTIP option-to-license into an executed, scoped exclusive (or confirm independent ownership), commission a freedom-to-operate opinion against the MRC worldwide HHS license and the crowded sub-500C art, and file a narrow, defensible improvement claim on the integration. Capital is staged behind these closing. *Tag: Assumption (High). Addresses R-IP-1 (gating), R-IP-2, R-IP-3, R-IP-4, T8, T9, W1, W8, W12. Axes: D secure exclusive + FTO, F restructure triggers.* **So-what:** a license-to-others business with no cleared, controlled, exclusive right to license is unsellable; nothing else in the strategy can be underwritten until this gate clears or is conceded.

**CLAIM-OPT-2 - Trade-secret-and-know-how posture where patent scope is weak.**
Where the public art is too crowded for a strong patent (Stage Two leach), shift defensibility to trade-secret protection on process integration, reagent regimes, and operating know-how, monetized through licensed deployment with confidentiality and field-of-use restrictions rather than through a patent monopoly. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate). Addresses R-TECH-3, R-IP-3, W8. Axes: D trade-secret on integration, A licensing or hybrid.* **So-what:** if the patent moat is incremental (R-TECH-3), the defensible asset is integrated know-how; the deal and IP posture should protect that rather than overclaim a patent position that prosecution will not deliver.

## ROYALTY — Licensing Deal Architect (co-lead)

**ROYALTY-OPT-1 - Repriced narrow-base licensing: keep the model, fix the instrument.**
Retain licensing-first, but move the royalty from an off-market whole-mine NSR to a verifiable narrow base (metered licensed-process throughput or incremental recovered metal), at recalibrated upfront and running terms benchmarked to process-tech norms, with an LOI-to-license conversion path that survives procurement. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate-High); benchmarks Cited (r00-licensing-norms.md). Addresses R-REV-1, R-REV-2, R-ADOPT-4, T6, S1, S3. Axes: A licensing-first, F restructure terms.* **So-what:** the deal structure is sound (S1) and verifiability is good design (S3); the defect is the basis, so the highest-yield fix is to re-paper the instrument, not abandon licensing.

**ROYALTY-OPT-2 - Anchor co-development deal first, license-to-the-market second.**
Lead with a single strategic-investor / co-development agreement with one operator that funds the validation campaign, provides the reference feedstock and site, and takes an early field-of-use or equity position, converting that reference into a repeatable license offer only after first proof. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate); channel Cited (r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Addresses O6, R-ADOPT-1, R-ADOPT-2, R-ADOPT-3, R-REV-5. Axes: A licensing via anchor, E strategic-investor anchor, C single anchor account.* **So-what:** a cold license sale into a multi-year qualification cycle (R-ADOPT-2) is the slowest path to revenue; an anchor that funds proof and supplies the reference site collapses validation, first-reference, and adoption risk into one move (O6).

**ROYALTY-OPT-3 - Hybrid technology-as-a-service / capacity model for higher value capture.**
Shift from a thin royalty to a TaaS or capacity-based structure (per-tonne processing fee or shared-savings on incremental recovered metal) on the licensed process, capturing materially more than 1% for the same recovery narrative, following the DLE TaaS/BOO precedent that leads that market at 36% share. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate-High); precedent Cited (r00-businessmodel-economics.md). Addresses O1, R-REV-9, T5, R-REV-1. Axes: A hybrid-TaaS, F strategic round to fund equipment.* **So-what:** if 1% NSR is potentially value-destroying (R-REV-9 / T5), a capacity model captures the upside of the recovery the operator could not achieve alone, while keeping LiT out of full ownership liability.

## MILL — Mining Operator Voice (Customer)

**MILL-OPT-1 - Buyer-led pilot-and-prove path: design the strategy around the operator's qualification gate, not around a signature date.**
Build the commercial plan from the buyer's actual decision process backward: a paid, in-circuit pilot on the operator's own feedstock, with the operator's metallurgists, against the operator's qualification criteria, priced so the recovery uplift clears the operator's all-in adoption cost (integration, downtime, personnel, audit). Accept that first commercial revenue is qualification-cycle-paced, not deck-paced. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate-High); cycles Cited (r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md). Addresses R-ADOPT-1, R-ADOPT-2, R-ADOPT-3, W6, W10, T2. Axes: A licensing via pilot, E independent-or-anchor, F realistic ramp.* **So-what:** operators buy through multi-year per-site qualification (R-ADOPT-2) and price against an all-in cost the deck omits (R-ADOPT-3 / W10); any strategy that does not embed the buyer's gate as the master schedule will miss every revenue date.

**MILL-OPT-2 - Win one credible reference, then sell the reference.**
Concentrate all early effort on a single lighthouse deployment at one cooperative operator and treat the resulting verified reference as the product, since a second operator does not inherit the first's proof but is far more likely to evaluate a peer-validated result than a founder claim. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate). Addresses MILL-O1 (demoted reference-site option value), R-ADOPT-2, R-ADOPT-5, T7. Axes: C single anchor first, B one feedstock.* **So-what:** in an occupied channel against an incumbent licensor (T7 / R-ADOPT-5), a peer-validated reference site is the only asset that shortens the next operator's evaluation; without one, every sale starts from zero.

## CAPITAL — Growth Investor & CFO Lens (co-lead)

**CAPITAL-OPT-1 - Gate capital to validation and IP, not to the calendar: restructure the tranche triggers.**
Keep the milestone-tranche architecture (it is the sound part, S1) but re-tie releases from activity dates (campaign start) to evidence events: an independent validated recovery number, an executed exclusive VTIP license with FTO opinion, and a converted first paid pilot. Reprice the entry to reflect pre-seed-level technical and IP risk, and size the round to a runway that survives a realistic, qualification-paced ramp rather than the knife-edge "no bridge" self-funding claim. *Tag: Assumption (High); terms Cited (r00-seed-valuation-norms.md, NVCA Oct 2025). Addresses R-REV-6, R-REV-8, R-REV-10, R-TECH-1, R-IP-1, W3, W14, S1. Axes: F restructure terms and triggers.* **So-what:** the tranche shape is the right instrument applied to the wrong triggers; re-keying releases to proof and repricing for stage of risk converts the deck's optimistic single path into a staged, abandonable bet.

**CAPITAL-OPT-2 - Strategic / corporate-venture round over a pure financial seed.**
Prefer a capital structure led by a strategic investor (an operator, a midstream processor, or a critical-minerals fund) that brings a reference site, qualification access, and offtake intent alongside the cash, rather than a pure financial seed that funds burn without de-risking adoption. *Tag: Assumption (Moderate); channel Cited (r00-major-miner-techprograms.md). Addresses O6, R-ADOPT-1, R-ADOPT-2, R-REV-5. Axes: E strategic-investor, F strategic round.* **So-what:** the binding constraints are validation and adoption, not cash alone; capital that also retires those constraints is worth more than a higher-valuation financial round that does not.

## WATERSHED — ESG, Remediation & Regulatory Strategist

**WATERSHED-OPT-1 - Licensing-first to keep environmental liability with the miner.**
Choose a model in which LiT supplies process technology and never takes title, custody, or operatorship of tailings, so strict joint-and-several retroactive CERCLA liability and closure obligations remain with the mine owner; treat any title/custody/BOO/producer option as carrying an explicit, priced environmental-liability and closure-bond line. *Tag: Cited liability regime (r00-tailings-liability.md), Assumption on model preference (Moderate-High). Addresses R-ESG-2, T10, R-REV-9. Axes: A licensing-first, B any.* **So-what:** remediation-as-asset inverts to remediation-as-liability the moment LiT holds the waste (T10 / R-ESG-2); the licensing model is the one structure that keeps that liability off LiT's balance sheet.

**WATERSHED-OPT-2 - Validation-scoped green positioning: substantiate, then claim.**
Build an evidence file (LCA, disclosed baseline, substitute-reagent trade-off analysis) before any energy, carbon, or "no acidic byproducts" claim enters licensing or investor materials, and until then use bench-stage qualified wording ("no highly acidic," energy/carbon claims only with disclosed baseline) to lead with economics and treat ESG as a coincident accelerant. *Tag: Cited standard (r00-green-claims-substantiation.md). Addresses R-ESG-1, W9, O7, O9. Axes: positioning across all models, F supports diligence.* **So-what:** the green claims as written fail FTC substantiation and add a 10b-5 surface (R-ESG-1 / W9); substantiation converts a liability into a financing-and-insurance differentiator (O7) and is a precondition, not a marketing afterthought.

## CHOKEPOINT — Geopolitics & Supply-Chain Security Lead (phase-active R01-R04)

**CHOKEPOINT-OPT-1 - Government-anchored allied-supply-chain play: lead with non-dilutive capital and offtake.**
Position LiT as allied-sourced critical-minerals recovery capacity (US/Virginia base, REE strategic wedge) and pursue non-dilutive grants, government offtake, and price-floor instruments as the first capital and demand layer, using policy capital to fund validation and floor the commodity-price downside before commercial licensing scales. *Tag: Cited programs (Market Research 222-225, r00-major-miner-techprograms.md), Assumption (Moderate). Addresses O4, O5, R-REV-3, R-REV-5, R-ADOPT-3, bounded by R-GEO-1. Axes: C US-and-allied, F grant-first, A any.* **So-what:** policy capital and a price floor (O5) are the one source that simultaneously funds proof, subsidizes the operator's qualification cost (R-ADOPT-3), and partially underwrites the downside half of the price band (R-REV-3), but it is a time-bounded accelerant (R-GEO-1), not a structural base.

**CHOKEPOINT-OPT-2 - Onshore the validation capability to protect the allied-positioning story.**
Move or mirror the 75%-of-R&D offshore concentration at IIT(ISM) Dhanbad onto US/allied soil (or under an export-control-compliant structure) so the national-security positioning is not undercut by an ITAR/EAR and IP-custody exposure, making the allied story defensible to a government counterparty. *Tag: Cited exposure (SRA 174-178, Financials 53-54), Assumption (Moderate). Addresses R-GEO-2, T12, R-TECH-4, tensions O4. Axes: C US-and-allied, E onshore partner.* **So-what:** the allied-positioning asset (O4) and the offshore-R&D exposure (T12 / R-GEO-2) are the same fact seen two ways; a government-anchored strategy cannot rest on a supply chain that fails its own export-control and custody test.

**DISSENT-OPT-2 - Restructure-first: a conditional, gated, repriced re-entry instead of the deck.**
If a position is to be taken, take it only on a restructured basis: a small validation-and-IP tranche (not growth capital) released only against (a) an executed exclusive VTIP license plus a clean FTO opinion, (b) an independent validated recovery number with defined baseline and basis, and (c) a converted first paid pilot or strategic anchor; royalty repriced to a narrow verifiable base; entry repriced for stage of risk; growth capital withheld behind all three gates. *Tag: Assumption (High). ## MODERATOR CONSOLIDATION — MECE Strategic Option Set

The seat proposals above cluster into **seven mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive strategic directions** on the primary spine (Axis A, business model and whether/how to proceed). The remaining axes (B feedstock, C geo/customer, D IP path, E partner, F capital) are configured *within* each direction and are specified per option below so each is internally coherent rather than a slogan. The set deliberately spans from "pass" to "own the metal" so Round 04 scores the real decision space, not variations on the deck.

The spine is MECE because Axis A is a single forced choice with these and only these coherent settings: do not proceed; proceed only after restructuring; proceed as written (licensing-first base); proceed via an anchor partner; proceed via a higher-capture hybrid; proceed as an owner/producer; or proceed as a policy-anchored allied supplier. Options 4 through 7 are distinguishable from each other not by proceeding but by *what LiT becomes and how it captures value*; an engagement could combine a configuration from one with a capital choice from another, and Round 04 will test those combinations as sensitivities, but as primary directions they do not overlap.
