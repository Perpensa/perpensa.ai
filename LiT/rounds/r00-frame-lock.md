# Round 00 — Frame Lock Memo

**Status:** LOCKED
**Issued by:** Moderator
**Binds:** Rounds 01 through 07 and all three deliverables (SWOT, Strategy, Execution Plan, Executive Summary)

---

## 1. The ruling

**Lens: ADVISOR.** The deliberation produces independent counsel, not a management self-assessment and not a one-sided investment memo.

**Decision owner: a prospective capital provider** weighing whether and how to take a position in LiT against the terms on the table in the background set (the $2M seed at $13M pre-money for a 13.33 percent stake, with the eight-tranche milestone-gated disbursement and the claimed 27x to 34x exit). The owner has commissioned independent counsel rather than relying on the founder's deck.

**Primary downstream consumer:** that capital provider's invest / structure / decline decision.
**Secondary consumer:** LiT management, because the same counsel must say what would have to be true and what management would have to execute for the thesis to pay. The Execution Plan is written as the build the capital provider would require management to deliver against, with decision gates the provider can underwrite, not as an internal company roadmap taken on faith.

## 2. Why this lens and not the others

The same evidence base supports three framings. The choice is forced by who is asking and what they need.

- **Why not the pure Company lens.** Nothing in the evidence base is a management instruction to "build the business." The originating party holds or is considering a financial position (Council Charter 2.2). A company-lens deliberation would assume the decision to proceed is already made and would optimize the build. That begs the central question the owner needs answered: whether to proceed at all, and on what terms. It also structurally weakens DISSENT, whose mandate is to flag reasoning that serves the position rather than the evidence. **Cited** (Charter 2.2, 4.2).

- **Why not the pure Investor lens.** A pure investor memo would terminate at a diligence verdict and a term recommendation and would not need a phased, resourced, gated Execution Plan. But the engagement demands all three deliverables, the Execution Plan included. The provider needs the build modeled to judge whether management can deliver the milestones that gate the tranches and the exit. A verdict without an execution view is not underwritable. **Cited** (Playbook 3, the three required deliverables; Charter 1).

- **Why Advisor.** The Advisor lens is defined as independent counsel weighing both the build and the investment (Charter 2.1). It is the only frame that (a) keeps the proceed / decline question open, (b) requires the full Execution Plan as the basis for judging deliverability, and (c) maximally protects the neutrality hazard, since independent counsel has no position to defend. It subsumes the useful outputs of the other two lenses without inheriting their blind spots. **Cited** (Charter 2.1, 2.2).

## 3. What this lens commits every later round to

1. **The recommendation space includes "do not proceed" and "proceed only if restructured."** Round 03 must generate, and Round 04 must score, at least one do-not-proceed / restructure-first option. The advisor is not obligated to find a path to yes. **Binds Rounds 03 and 04.**

2. **Findings are written for a skeptical outside capital provider, not for management morale.** Adverse findings ship at full strength. The McKinsey quality bar (MECE, pyramid, so-what, quantified, evidence-traced, confidence-banded) is enforced as a diligence standard. **Binds all rounds.**

3. **The Execution Plan is underwriting-grade, not aspirational.** Every milestone is a gate the capital provider could condition a tranche on. Every High and Elevated risk maps to an owned mitigation the provider can verify. DISSENT may strike any milestone that assumes a buyer or a result the evidence does not support. **Binds Round 05.**

4. **The deal terms are in scope, not given.** The $13M pre-money, the 1 percent NSR royalty base, the upfront-plus-royalty licensing structure, and the tranche logic are objects of assessment, not fixed inputs. The Strategy may recommend different terms or a different structure as a condition of proceeding. **Binds Rounds 03 and 04.**

5. **Frame statement is mandatory in Round 07.** The final deliverables state plainly that they serve the Advisor lens for a prospective capital provider, and state what would change under a pure Company lens (assume-proceed, optimize-build) or a pure Investor lens (terminate at term sheet, drop the execution detail). **Binds Round 07.**

## 4. What is explicitly NOT decided here

This memo locks the lens and the decision owner. It does not pre-judge the verdict, does not endorse or reject any claim in the prospectus, and does not pre-load the Round 00 research register. Positions begin in Round 01. The frame is held fixed; everything downstream of it is contested on the evidence.
