Executive SummarySWOTStrategyExecution Plan

Engagement: Seed-stage capital decision, Lithium Technologies, Inc. (LiT)

Frame: The advisor's lens — a prospective capital provider deciding whether and on what terms to deploy capital into the seed LiT offers. The yardstick is risk-adjusted return to the advisor's capital under a downside-aware lens, not the founder's growth narrative.

Prepared for: Investment Committee · Classification: Confidential · Date: 29 June 2026

1. Recommended direction (up front)

Decline the seed as offered, and convert the pass into capital only through a restructured, gated, repriced instrument.

Concretely, the advisor's action is a single conditional move, not a binary yes/no:

  1. Do not fund Option 2 (the deck as written). It rests on three unmitigated High gates (no cleared exclusive right to license, no scaled and reproduced recovery number, an off-market whole-mine-NSR instrument sold into a multi-year qualification cycle on calendar-keyed dates). Under the locked §7 gating-override it is ineligible for recommendation regardless of headline economics. (Cited gates R-IP-1, R-TECH-1, R-ADOPT-1/2; §6.)
  2. Issue a restructure-first term (Option 1) in place of a pass. Offer a small validation-and-IP tranche, not growth capital, released only against three evidence gates, with the entry repriced for stage of risk and the royalty narrowed to a verifiable base. The company's response to that offer is itself the missing fact the decision turns on (see §4). (Source: DISSENT-OPT-2, CAPITAL-OPT-1, CLAIM-OPT-1, CRUCIBLE-OPT-1.)
  3. Hold capital out of the growth round behind the gates. Growth capital releases only after all three gates clear. The default if the company will not restructure is the unconditional pass (Option 0), which leads the matrix under every weighting tested.

One-line statement of the recommendation: Pass on the deck; offer the gated restructure; convert to a staged position only when the company accepts the gates and the down-world is shown to be genuinely capped; otherwise the pass stands.

This is the only direction the locked evidence supports under the advisor lens. It is a conditional pass that designates Option 1 (Restructure-first) as the one direction it converts into. It is not a hard no and not an unconditional yes; both of those over-read the evidence.

Confidence: High that the deck-as-written is unfundable (three Cited gates). High that, if a position is taken, it must be Option 1's gated form. Moderate on the final pass-vs-convert call itself, because it pivots on an unretrieved company-conduct fact (§4). Fragility: the recommendation flips from "pass and hold" to "convert and fund" the moment the two §5 defeaters resolve; it is deliberately one fact-retrieval away from changing.

4. Rationale, including the load-bearing 0-vs-1 ruling

4.1 Why the deck-as-written is unfundable

Three of LiT's headline assets each invert to a gating defect on the locked evidence:

  • "Globally patented platform" → no cleared, controlled, exclusive right to license (R-IP-1, High): the live HHS surface-chemistry IP is VTIP-owned and MRC-licensed under undisclosed terms; the VT agreement is an option-to-license (Art. 13.4 to 13.6), not an executed license; the only LiT-side filing on the public record is a possibly-lapsed 2024-03 provisional. (Cited.)
  • "16 to 19% recovery, bench-validated across three feedstocks" → no scaled, reproduced number (R-TECH-1, High): bench is not piloted is not per-site qualified; the claim is undefined on basis (percentage points vs relative, baseline, particle-size). (Cited.)
  • "1% whole-mine NSR, exit at 27 to 34x" → an off-market instrument on a multiplicative downside (R-REV-1, R-REV-8, High): process-tech licensing does not price on whole-mine NSR, majors resist the basis, exit multiples are 3 to 5x too rich, and there is no downside scenario in the model. (Cited.)

A license-to-others business with no exclusive right to license, no validated number, and an off-market instrument is unfundable as written. The §7 override makes this formal: Option 2 is ineligible regardless of total.

4.2 Why the contest is Pass versus Restructure-first, and nothing higher-capture

The matrix sorts every proceed option along a single revealed axis: value-capture versus capital-efficiency-and-liability (DISSENT-OPT-3, matrix §4). Each step up the capture ladder is bought with lower abandonability and a heavier, liability-loaded down-world:

The joint First-Chicago downside (R-REV-8 × R-ADOPT-2: a slow qualification cycle stretching the burn across a low-price world) is survivable only where capital is staged (Option 1) or never committed (Option 0) and is amplified monotonically up the capture ladder (3 → 5 → 6). On the 22%-weighted C6 the liability and capital cost of the higher-capture options outweighs their capture gain. Hybrid and Producer are dominated under every weighting; they survive only as a fully ring-fenced Option 6-plus-7 (policy floor plus liability SPV), never standalone. The Anchor (3) and Government (7) directions are configurations, not free wins: Anchor buys time-to-first-reference with counterparty concentration; Government is defensible only as a downside-floor overlay on a base that already stands on economics. This leaves the genuine contest where the matrix puts it: 0 versus 1.

4.3 The load-bearing ruling: Option 1's down-world does NOT yet strictly beat capital-preserved

DISSENT's §6.1 condition binds: Option 1 beats Option 0 only if its down-world C6 strictly beats capital-preserved-and-redeployed. The moderator ruling on present evidence is that it does not, so the score holds at a tie (C6 = 4) and Pass leads, for two reasons:

  1. The down-world is a real loss, not zero. Option 1's down-world writes off the validation-and-IP tranche. Small and staged, but a loss. Pass's down-world is no loss at all (capital preserved and redeployable at the hurdle rate). For Option 1 to score a strict 5 it must show that the retained up-world option value more than compensates the tranche it puts at risk. That is plausible (the entry is the cheapest on offer, R-REV-10: ~80% of median seed) but it is an Assumption, not a demonstrated fact.
  2. The two facts that would cap the down-world to a genuine 5 are unretrieved or unverified. A clean C6 = 5 requires (a) the company actually accepts gating and repricing, which is the unretrieved company-conduct fact behind C1/C3/C4 and the VTIP option terms (C3), and (b) the three gates run in parallel and read within roughly 12 months for a bounded spend, rather than entangled and serial across a multi-year tie-up (DISSENT's entanglement attack, the C4 drag that keeps Pass ahead at the tie). Neither is established on the locked evidence.

Therefore DISSENT §6.1 is not satisfied, and Pass holds as the leading direction under all three weightings. But CAPITAL's attack is also correct: passing unconditionally forfeits the cheapest entry into a structurally pulled market (declining head grades, ~16 Bt/yr tailings stream, O2 Cited). The resolution that honours both is the conditional pass: decline the deck, issue the Option 1 term, and let the company's response retrieve the missing fact. The advisor does not wait passively for the company to clear its own gates (that forfeits first-mover terms); it puts the restructured instrument on the table and converts the moment the defeaters resolve.

Net ruling: Recommendation = Option 0 held as a conditional pass that designates Option 1 as its convert-to direction. Pass is the default that stands if the company refuses; Option 1 is the funded position that activates on the two §5 defeaters. The matrix correctly left this at the margin; the resolution is to make the offer the instrument of retrieval rather than to force a premature binary.

4.4 Binding release conditions carried forward (the gates)

If and when the recommendation converts to funding Option 1, these are binding pre-conditions on capital release, not retired facts. They are carried from R02/R03 and apply to every proceed configuration (1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7):

  • Gate G0 — Neutrality & abandon-spec (new, R06; precedes T1). Before any term is tabled, the advisor commits in writing to (i) a per-gate abandon specification naming the specific result at T2, Gate A, Gate B, and Gate C that forces a walk-away, written before the gate is approached and not editable after; and (ii) a no-position decision authority on each convert (T1, T2, Gate B pass, P2 release, P3 release) that holds no LiT position and sits outside the deal team. This is the structural counter the Council §2.2 neutrality hazard requires; without it every gate below reverts to advisor self-discipline, which the R06 pre-mortem identifies as the single deepest failure path. (R06 pre-mortem §B.1, central finding; DISSENT failure mode.)
  • Gate A — IP: an executed, scoped exclusive VTIP license (or confirmed independent ownership) plus a clean FTO opinion against the MRC worldwide HHS license and the crowded sub-500C art. The FTO opinion (limb A2) must also state whether the executed claim scope is broad enough to survive a competent design-around once the Gate B number is public (moat-durability limb, R06 §B.4); a clear FTO on a claim too thin to stop a copier is logged as a contingency on the value-capture question, not a clean pass. (R-IP-1, R-TECH-3; DISSENT gate (a), not cleared.)
  • Gate B — Validation: an independently reproduced recovery number with a defined baseline and basis, read at scale, by a party other than the founder and outside the partner R&D pool, on a campaign protocol fixed and accepted by the G0 no-position authority before it is funded. (R-TECH-1; R06 §B.2 B2 protocol lock.)
  • Gate C — First reference: a converted first paid pilot or a strategic anchor that prices the operator's all-in adoption cost and is paced to the real qualification cycle. (R-ADOPT-2/3.)

Policy capital (Option 7) is admissible only as an overlay on this gated base, priced as a downside floor (it lifts the base's C6 down-world and C3), never as the base case and never to buy points on C1/C2/C5. (DISSENT's bar; matrix §9.)

5. Change-triggers (the conditions that change the recommendation)

The recommendation is engineered to move on evidence. Each trigger names the fact, the direction it points, and the matrix cell it moves.

Pre-condition that GATES the whole conversion sequence: required before T1.

  • G0 — Neutrality & abandon-spec in place. The written per-gate abandon specification and the no-position decision authority (§4.4 Gate G0) are stood up before any term is tabled. If G0 cannot be stood up in practice, the conversion sequence does not begin and the pass stands; the gates below are not self-enforceable by the deal team. (R06 §B.1.)

Triggers that CONVERT the conditional pass into funding Option 1 (Restructure-first): both required.

  • T1 — Acceptance, against quantitative floors. The company signs a gated, repriced term sheet (small validation-and-IP tranche, growth capital withheld, royalty narrowed). This retrieves the C1/C3/C4 company-conduct fact and the VTIP option terms (C3), and retires ROYALTY's acceptance attack. T1 passes only against three floors written into the committed term and reviewed by the G0 authority (R06 §B.5, §B.7, §B.9): (i) a modeled minimum-viable royalty base below which the repapered narrow-base structure does not clear the return threshold — easy acceptance on a self-defined base beneath this floor is a fail, not a partial win; (ii) the repriced entry committed in the term itself, not held as a posture to be traded away against a claimed rival bidder; (iii) a dual-world return range showing the surplus-world and deficit-world IRRs weighted near-equally — a single-point headline return number in the committed materials is a fail. The VTIP option-window length (Art. 13.4-13.6, C3) is retrieved and the Gate A conversion deadline calendared at T1 signing, not discovered at Gate A. (R06 §B.4, §B.5.)
  • T2 — Capped down-world, evidenced by a named vendor quote. T2 passes only on an actual written quote/scope from a named, outside-the-partner-pool validation authority for an operator-relevant scaled campaign that reads within ~12 months for a bounded (<~$0.5 to 1M) spend — not on a sequencing analysis the advisor authors for itself. The independence (outside the partner pool) and operator-relevant-feedstock constraints bind together on the same vendor; if no single vendor satisfies both, T2 fails. No quote, no T2, the pass holds. A passing T2 sets Option 1's down-world C6 to a genuine 5, at which point it overtakes Pass under pure-return. (R06 §B.3; PILOT/CAPITAL failure modes.)

Trigger that ESCALATES the convert-to direction from Restructure-first (1) to Anchor (3):

  • T3 — Speed binds, concentration ring-fenced. If time-to-first-reference is the binding constraint (founder speed-first weighting, under which Anchor already outranks Restructure-first) and an anchor term ring-fences field-of-use and bars in-house replication or competitor funding for a defined period and a second operator's evaluation interest is evidenced contingent on the reference. Absent the ring-fence, the anchor collapses into a single-customer concentration dependency (R-ADOPT-1) and the trigger does not fire.

Trigger that ADDS a policy overlay (Option 7) to the chosen base:

  • T4 — Awarded, durable instrument. A concrete, awarded (not prospective) grant, government offtake, or price-floor with a term longer than the qualification cycle, plus onshoring of the Dhanbad R&D to retire the export-control / IP-custody exposure (R-GEO-2). Overlays on Option 1 or 4 as a downside floor; never converts the base to policy-dependence.

Triggers that HOLD the hard pass (Option 0 stands, no conversion):

  • T5 — Refusal. The company declines gating or repricing, or can raise the unrestructured deck elsewhere (itself an adverse-selection signal). The conditional pass becomes a standing pass.
  • T6 — Disqualifying retrieval. C1/C3/C4 retrieval surfaces a disqualifying IP fact (VTIP option lapsed or encumbered, no clean FTO path) or a validation fact that no bounded spend can retire.
  • T7 — Gate cost blows the abandonable threshold. Gate-clearing time or cost estimates exceed the small-abandonable-bet threshold, collapsing the staging that is Option 1's entire advantage.

Standing overrides (held as guardrails):

  • T8 — §7 bar. Any unmitigated High gate still open at the point of funding bars recommendation outright (the Option 2 condition).
  • T9 — No naked high-capture. Hybrid (5) and Producer (6) are revisited only as a fully ring-fenced Option 6-plus-7 (policy price-floor and a liability SPV with bonded closure and an impoundment-owner indemnity), never standalone. Absent both, they stay dominated.
  • T10 — No pass on momentum (new, R06). Any gate "pass" (T2, Gate A, Gate B, Gate C, P2/P3 release) argued from sunk cost or pipeline progress rather than from the named artifact is automatically a fail. The decision authority must be able to name, in advance and in writing, the specific result at each gate that would force a walk-away. This is the operative rule that makes the G0 abandon-spec binding rather than aspirational. (R06 §B.1, the amplifier on every other seat's failure mode.)

7. Confidence and fragility (document-level)

  • Robust: the deck-as-written is unfundable (three Cited High gates, §7-ineligible) — High confidence, fragile only to a wholesale IP/validation retrieval that reverses R-IP-1 and R-TECH-1 at once (low probability, would be caught by Gate A/B).
  • Robust: Pass leads the matrix under all three weightings, and the higher-capture options (3/5/6/7-standalone) are not the answer — High confidence, survives advisor-caution, pure-return, and founder-speed lenses.
  • Marginal by design: the pass-versus-convert call itself — Moderate confidence, deliberately one fact-retrieval (T1+T2) away from flipping. This is the intended fragility: the recommendation is built to move when the company acts, not to pretend certainty the evidence does not support.
  • Single largest unretrieved dependency: company conduct on gating and repricing, and the VTIP option terms (C1/C3/C4, C3) — flagged NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL; the recommendation's structure makes the offer the means of retrieval.
  • Deepest fragility (R06): the gates are sound, but several are enforced by advisor judgment at the points the neutrality hazard is strongest. The G0 neutrality control and the T10 "no pass on momentum" override convert that self-discipline into structure; if G0 cannot be stood up in practice, every gate reverts to advisor self-discipline and the deepest failure path reopens. This is the one fragility the recommendation cannot price away by evidence; it can only be controlled by governance.
