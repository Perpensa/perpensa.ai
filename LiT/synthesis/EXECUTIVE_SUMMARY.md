# Executive Summary

**Engagement:** Proposed seed investment in Lithium Technologies, Inc. (LiT)

**Prepared for:** Investment Committee · **Classification:** Confidential · **Date:** 29 June 2026

---

## Recommendation

**Decline the seed on its current terms. Reserve any capital for a restructured, gated, and repriced instrument, to be offered only if the company accepts hard evidence gates and the downside can be shown to be genuinely capped. Absent that, the pass stands.**

This is a single conditional move, not a binary choice between yes and no:

- **Do not fund the offering as written.** It rests on three unresolved, high-severity defects and does not qualify for investment regardless of its headline economics.
- **In place of a simple pass, table a restructured term.** Offer a small validation-and-IP tranche, not growth capital, released only against three evidence gates, with the entry price reset to the true stage of risk and the royalty narrowed to a base we can verify. The company's response to that offer is itself the decisive fact the decision turns on.
- **Hold all growth capital behind the gates.** It releases only once every gate has cleared. If the company will not restructure, the default is a clean pass, which is the strongest-scoring option under every test we ran.

In short: decline the deck, offer the gated restructure, and convert to a staged position only when the company accepts the gates and the downside is demonstrably contained. This is a conditional pass with one designated path to yes, not a hard no and not an unconditional commitment.

## The offer on the table

LiT is raising $2M at a $13M pre-money valuation (13.33% of the company), released across eight milestone-linked tranches, against a projected 27x to 34x exit. The business is to license a two-stage minerals-recovery process to mine operators and collect a 1% royalty on whole-mine net smelter returns (NSR). For this assessment the deal terms, the royalty basis, the licensing structure, and the tranche schedule are all matters under examination, not fixed inputs. The test applied throughout is risk-adjusted return to our capital under a downside-aware lens, not the founder's growth narrative.

## Why we decline as offered

The thesis addresses a real problem with a coherent technical approach. But each of its three headline assets inverts under examination into a material weakness.

**There is no clean, exclusive intellectual property to license.** No granted or published LiT patent exists on the public record. The core surface chemistry is owned by Virginia Tech's IP arm and is already licensed worldwide to a third party; the only instrument LiT holds is an unexercised option to license, granting internal research rights only. A business built on licensing a moat to others is being sold on a moat whose existence, exclusivity, and freedom to operate are all unestablished.

**The recovery improvement that drives the entire revenue model is undefined and unvalidated.** The claim of a 16 to 19% recovery uplift, "bench-validated across three feedstocks," rests on no primary dataset. The only completed technical work is a $52,075, three-month desk study, and the research partner expected to generate the validating data is not yet under contract.

**Every quantitative input to the revenue model is aggressive, and the errors compound.** The 1% whole-mine NSR basis is off-market for process-technology licensing; the terms are steep for a pre-pilot process; the model carries no price sensitivity against a commodity that swung two- to threefold within a single year; the adoption ramp is years shorter than documented operator-qualification cycles; and the exit multiples are roughly three to five times too rich. Multiplied together, the realistic expected value is a fraction of the headline.

## What genuine value remains

The case is not empty once the headline claims are set aside. Four things survive scrutiny:

- **A sound deal and control structure.** An upfront fee plus running royalty, released through milestone-gated tranches, is a fundable and enforceable shape. The defects sit in the inputs, not the architecture, so the instrument can be repapered rather than abandoned.
- **Real demand-side pull.** Declining ore grades and critical-minerals targets give operators a standing economic reason to evaluate any credible recovery uplift.
- **A better value-capture model than 1% licensing.** A hybrid service, tolling, or build-own-operate structure could capture far more of the value the technology creates, if the economics hold.
- **Bounded policy and ESG tailwinds.** Allied-sourcing pressure, non-dilutive government funding, and tightening tailings governance open doors and aid fundraising, though none moves an operator through qualification.

None of this offsets a gating defect. It describes the value that exists only once the three defects above are cleared.

## How we reached the recommendation

We generated seven mutually exclusive directions, from a clean pass through to LiT becoming a full producer, and scored each against seven weighted criteria, with risk-adjusted return weighted most heavily. A pass leads under all three weightings we tested — advisor-caution, pure-return, and a founder-friendly speed-first lens — so the result is not an artifact of conservatism. The offering as written is disqualified outright under a standing rule that bars any option carrying an unresolved high-severity defect, regardless of its economics. Every higher-value-capture alternative is either dominated or viable only as a configuration layered on a base that already stands on its own economics, because each step up the value-capture ladder is bought with less ability to walk away and a heavier, liability-laden downside.

The genuine contest narrows to two options: a clean pass, or the restructured, gated re-entry. On the current evidence the restructured option does not yet beat a clean pass: its validation tranche is a real loss, and the two facts that would cap that downside to zero — the company actually accepting the gates and repricing, and the gates running in parallel within roughly twelve months — are not yet established. A pass therefore holds as the default. But passing unconditionally would forfeit the cheapest available entry into a structurally attractive market. The resolution that honors both is the conditional pass: decline the deck, put the restructured term on the table, and convert the moment the missing facts resolve.

## How capital would be deployed if the recommendation converts

If and when the recommendation converts, the structure is not a build schedule but a capital-staging architecture: four phases separated by three binding, evidence-based decision gates, where every release is small and abandonable and no growth capital exists until all three gates clear. The gates are tied to evidence, not dates. LiT's own milestone calendar marks the start of work, not proof of results, and is not adopted as a release schedule. The sequence runs strictly from the company's acceptance, through the IP and validation gates in parallel, to a first paying reference, and only then to growth capital. Our entire pre-committed exposure is bounded to a roughly $0.9M validation-and-IP tranche.

The central deployment finding is that governance, not metallurgy, is the binding control. The gates are sound, but several depend on our own judgment at exactly the point where our neutrality is most at risk. The plan answers this with a structural safeguard: a written, pre-committed specification of what result forces a walk-away at each gate; a decision authority that holds no position in the deal and sits outside the deal team; and a standing rule that any gate cleared on momentum rather than evidence is automatically treated as a failure.

## Principal risks

1. **Time to revenue is the dominant risk, and it cannot be accelerated.** Operator qualification is multi-year, runs separately for each operator and each feedstock, and a second customer does not inherit the first's proof. Combined with the compounding revenue downside, a slow qualification cycle that stretches the cash burn across a low-price market is the most likely failure scenario. The plan paces and bounds this exposure but cannot shorten it.
2. **Customer concentration compounded by self-substitution.** The named target accounts are simultaneously the sales pipeline, the most capable competitors, and potential acquirers or funders of a substitute. Their revealed preference is to take equity, build in-house, or buy offtake, not to pay a whole-mine royalty. The most-courted accounts are the least likely to adopt on LiT's terms and the best placed to route around it.
3. **The neutrality hazard, and the deepest fragility.** The originating party holds or is weighing a position, the offering presents a single optimistic path, and the gates that protect against every risk above depend on our own judgment under deal pressure. No amount of further diligence retires this risk; it is controllable only by the governance structure above. If that structure cannot be stood up in practice, every gate reverts to self-discipline and the deepest failure path reopens.

The IP and validation defects sit above these as hard release conditions rather than residual risks: they bar funding until cleared.

## What would change our view

The recommendation is built to move on evidence, not to feign a certainty the evidence does not support.

- **Before any term is offered:** the written walk-away specification and the independent, no-position decision authority must be in place. If they cannot be, the conversion does not begin and the pass stands.
- **To convert the conditional pass into the restructured investment (both required):** the company signs the gated, repriced term against three quantitative floors — a minimum viable royalty base, the repriced entry committed in the term itself, and a return range modeled across both high- and low-price worlds — and a named, independent validation house issues a written quote showing the IP and validation gates can be read in parallel within roughly twelve months for the bounded spend.
- **To escalate from a pure restructure to an anchor co-development:** time-to-first-reference becomes the binding constraint, and an anchor partner contractually ring-fences the field of use, bars in-house replication and competitor funding, and a second operator's interest is evidenced.
- **To add a policy overlay:** a concrete, awarded, durable instrument longer than the qualification cycle, never as the base case.
- **To hold the hard pass:** the company refuses the gates or repricing (itself an adverse signal); diligence surfaces a disqualifying IP or validation fact; or the cost or time to clear a gate exceeds the small, abandonable threshold that is the restructured option's entire advantage.

The binding release conditions on any path to funding remain three: an executed exclusive IP right with clean freedom to operate; an independently reproduced, defined, and scaled recovery number generated outside the company's own research partners; and a first paid reference priced to the operator's true all-in cost of adoption at the real qualification cadence.

## Confidence and the central fragility

We hold **high confidence** that the offering as written is unfundable, and that a pass leads under every weighting tested; this would reverse only in the unlikely event that a full data-room review overturns both the IP and the validation findings at once. We hold **moderate confidence** on the pass-versus-convert call itself, which is deliberately one fact away from flipping, by design, so the recommendation moves when the company acts. The single largest open dependency is the company's own conduct on the gates and repricing, which the restructured offer is engineered to surface. The deepest fragility is not evidence but governance: if the independent, no-position control cannot be established in practice, the gates revert to our own discipline at exactly the point our neutrality is most exposed. That is the one risk we cannot price away; it can only be controlled by structure.

This assessment does not record a consensus to proceed. It converges on a conditional pass with a single, gated path to yes, with every dissent, the neutrality hazard, and the dominant time-to-revenue and concentration risks preserved as live conditions rather than resolved.
