# W10 — Majors' In-House and Venture-Funded Processing-Tech Programs Inside LiT's Named Accounts

**Round:** 00 Grounding & Research Commissioning
**Unit:** [Web] item W10 (P2)
**Priority:** P2
**Requesting seat:** MILL 2
**Decision it unblocks:** Buy-vs-build-vs-fund-competitor risk; customer-concentration / displacement finding (SWOT Threats, Round 04 adoption scoring, Round 03 channel strategy)
**Named accounts examined:** BHP, Freeport-McMoRan, Rio Tinto, Glencore, Teck
**Lens:** ADVISOR, prospective capital provider

---

## Bottom line for the deliberation

Every one of LiT's five named target accounts already runs a substantial in-house processing-technology capability, a venture/accelerator arm that funds external recovery technology, or both. This is the central MILL 2 finding: LiT is not selling into a technology vacuum. It is selling into buyers who can build, who can buy a competitor, or who can fund a rival startup, and who in several cases are doing all three today. **Cited.**

This cuts two ways and both belong in the SWOT:

- **Validation / channel signal (Opportunity-leaning).** Majors are visibly willing to fund and adopt external recovery and extraction technology (Rio Tinto's Ventures Fund and accelerator, Freeport's adoption of Jetti's external catalytic leach tech, BHP's Xplor minority-stake model). A credible external technology *can* get in the door and even get funded by the customer. **Cited.**
- **Displacement / concentration risk (Threat-leaning).** These same accounts have deep internal R&D, existing technology partners, and in Glencore's case a 40-year in-house processing-technology *licensing business of its own*. A 1%-NSR external licensor with a single unvalidated recovery claim is competing for the same recovery uplift the customer is already pursuing internally, and can be designed around, out-waited, or simply funded as a minority stake on the major's terms. **Cited / Assumption (High).**

The net read-through: customer concentration on five majors is riskier than a raw account list suggests, because each account is also a potential builder and competitor-funder, not just a buyer. This pressures Round 04 adoption probability and argues in Round 03 for a channel/structure that does not depend on displacing a major's internal program.

## Account-by-account evidence

### BHP — accelerator + minority-stake venture model + adopter of external CPF

- **BHP Xplor** is an accelerator launched in 2021 offering ~US$500,000 equity-free grants over a 3-to-9-month program; participants then pitch BHP for investment, with **BHP taking a minority interest on mutually agreed terms**. Focus is heavily exploration/discovery (copper, nickel, critical minerals), not primarily downstream processing. **Cited** (discoveryalert.com.au; theassay.com).
- BHP has a long in-house hydrometallurgy history: bacterially-assisted **BIOCOP™** leaching commercialised at the Alliance Copper plant in Chile, and the **Escondida** ammonia/ammonium-sulfate sulfide-leach process. Demonstrates standing internal capability to develop recovery chemistry. **Cited** (sec.gov BHP filings; sciencedirect.com S0304386X06000661). These are older programs; treat as evidence of capability, not current pipeline.
- BHP is an **adopter of external processing tech**: it commissioned Eriez's HydroFloat coarse-particle-flotation plant at Carrapateena (commissioned 2025), per W9. **Cited** (cross-ref `research/r00-operator-qualification-cycles.md`).

**Read-through:** BHP's revealed model for external tech is a minority equity stake via an accelerator, not a clean third-party royalty license. A licensor seeking a whole-mine NSR is offering the opposite of what BHP's own programme is structured to do. **Assumption (Moderate-High).**

### Freeport-McMoRan — large in-house leach-innovation program + external partnership (direct analog)

- Freeport runs a flagship in-house **"Leach to the Last Drop" innovation program** targeting incremental copper from existing leach stockpiles and material previously classed as waste. It identified **~38 billion lbs of contained copper** in stockpiles previously deemed unrecoverable. Run-rate reached ~200M lbs/yr in 2024 (up from 144M in 2023), targeting 300M by end-2025, 400M by late-2026, long-term 800M lbs/yr. **Cited** (miningweekly.com 2025-01-27; im-mining.com 2023-02-07).
- Freeport holds its own granted patents (cyclic metal-recovery process) and won an **$80M U.S. DOE grant**, and runs deep-raffinate injection, targeted drilling, and geothermal stockpile heating in-house. **Cited** (fcx.com 032224; mining-technology.com; research.arizona.edu).
- Critically, Freeport **also advances Jetti Resources' external patented catalytic leach technology** as part of the same program. So a named LiT account simultaneously runs a massive internal recovery R&D effort *and* partners with an external recovery-tech startup. **Cited** (miningweekly.com 2025-01-27).

**Read-through:** This is the single most instructive account. It proves (a) external recovery tech can win a major's adoption (good for LiT's thesis), and (b) the bar is high because the customer already extracts recovery uplift internally and already has a chosen external partner (Jetti) in the adjacent copper-leach niche. Freeport is a buyer, a builder, and a competitor-backer at once. **Assumption (High).**

### Rio Tinto — dedicated Ventures Fund + accelerator + direct extraction-tech investments

- Rio Tinto created a **dedicated venture capital fund in 2021** and runs two platforms: a **Ventures Fund** and an **Accelerator Program** (the latter with Founders Factory; "six new startups" cohort reported April 2026, an earlier cohort Sept 2024-2025). One of its five named investment themes is explicitly **"metals processing and recovery — unlock new, or better recover existing resources with novel processes."** **Cited** (riotinto.com growth-ventures; metaltechnews.com 2026-04-01; im-mining.com 2025-09-19; globalminingreview.com 2024-10-16).
- Rio Tinto **invested in ElectraLith**, a DLE/refining electro-filtration startup (raising ~A$29M/US$19M), and backs **Magmatic** (metal-binding proteins, initially lithium recovery). **Cited** (carboncredits.com; theglobeandmail.com; riotinto.com ventures).

**Read-through:** Rio Tinto is the clearest case that a major will *fund* external recovery/extraction tech, and specifically lithium-extraction tech. For LiT this is a genuine channel (a customer who invests, not just licenses). But Rio's instrument is an equity stake through its own fund on its terms, again the opposite of a passive whole-mine NSR, and it already has lithium-recovery bets (ElectraLith, Magmatic) that compete for the same internal attention. **Cited / Assumption (Moderate-High).**

### Glencore — owns an in-house processing-technology *licensing business* (the most direct competitive threat)

- **Glencore Technology (GT)** is a 40-year-old technology business embedded in Glencore (roots at Mount Isa Mines) that develops processing technology and **licenses it to the wider market** — i.e. Glencore is itself a process-tech licensor, GT's director stating "there is actually no company like GT in the world." **Cited** (glencoretechnology.com who-we-are; glencore.com.au insights).
- GT's portfolio includes the **IsaMill** (ultrafine grinding to ~10-12 microns, directly relevant to fine/ultrafine liberation, the same regime as LiT's HHS surface-chemistry play) and the **Albion Process™** (ultrafine grind + atmospheric oxidative leaching, developed 1994, with HyperSparge oxygen injection), plus active US patent grants (USPTO 12480178, 12553106 sulphide-oxidation leaching). **Cited** (glencoretechnology.com technologies; uspto.gov patent PDFs).

**Read-through:** Glencore is not just a potential buyer that could build; it is an incumbent *competitor* in the business model LiT proposes (license processing technology to miners), with patents, references, an installed base, and ultrafine-grinding/atmospheric-leach IP adjacent to LiT's own claimed niche. For a fine-particle recovery licensor, Glencore Technology is the closest direct competitive analog among the named accounts and a freedom-to-operate/displacement flag. **Assumption (High).** Feeds the IP/competitive-threat finding alongside `research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md`.

### Teck — large internal technology-transformation program + dedicated technology centres

- Teck's **RACE21™** technology-transformation program was projected to deliver **~$1.1 billion in annualized benefits**, including process-recovery gains: ML/automation lifted **copper recovery by up to 3%** at Highland Valley and mill throughput up to 15%, and grinding-stability gains of 9% at Red Dog. **Cited** (teck.com 2022 release; mining.com).
- Teck operates **three dedicated technology centres**: CESL (hydrometallurgy), Applied Research and Technology (ART), and the Product Technology Centre (PTC). CESL is a notable in-house hydrometallurgical-process developer. **Cited** (teck.com/technology; teck.com Connect vol.22 2018).
- Note: Teck is the subject of an Anglo American acquisition approved by Canada in 2025, an ownership-change overhang on its procurement and tech roadmap. **Cited** (discoveryalert.com.au 2025). Treat Teck's future tech-buying posture as uncertain pending that transaction.

**Read-through:** Teck pursues recovery uplift primarily through internal digital/automation transformation and in-house hydromet (CESL), i.e. a build-internally posture, with an ownership change adding adoption uncertainty. **Assumption (Moderate).**

## Direct implications for the LiT thesis (tagged)

- **Customer-concentration risk is compounded, not just additive.** Each named account is simultaneously a prospective buyer, a capable builder, and (for several) a funder of competing startups. The probability that all five convert to passive royalty licensees is lower than an account-list view implies. **Assumption (High).** Feeds Round 04 adoption scoring and the SWOT Threat on concentration.
- **The whole-mine 1% NSR license is structurally mismatched to how these accounts buy external tech.** The revealed instruments are minority equity (BHP Xplor, Rio Ventures), in-house deployment (Freeport, Teck, Glencore), or product-of-process partnership (Freeport/Jetti). None is a passive whole-mine royalty. This reinforces W4's finding that 1% whole-mine NSR is off-market and supports re-structuring toward equity-aligned or product-of-process terms in Round 03/04. **Assumption (High).** Cross-links `research/r00-licensing-norms.md`.
- **Glencore Technology is a direct competitive-model threat and a freedom-to-operate flag.** A 40-year licensor with IsaMill (ultrafine grinding) and Albion (atmospheric leach) patents occupies the exact business model and an adjacent technical niche to LiT's fine-particle recovery play. This belongs in the competitive landscape and the IP-risk picture. **Assumption (High).** Cross-links `research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md`.
- **There is a real, evidenced channel: customer-as-investor.** Rio Tinto funding ElectraLith (lithium extraction) and Freeport adopting Jetti show external recovery/extraction tech *can* be funded and deployed by these majors. This is a legitimate Opportunity and a plausible Round 03 strategic option (raise from / co-develop with a strategic major rather than license at arm's length). **Cited / Assumption (Moderate-High).**
- **The internal-recovery-uplift programs are LiT's true competition for budget.** Freeport already converts "waste" stockpiles to 200M+ lb/yr internally; Teck books 3% recovery from ML; BHP/Glencore have in-house hydromet. LiT must clear not just a technical bar but an *incremental-over-what-the-customer-already-does-internally* bar. **Assumption (High).** Feeds Round 04 value-proposition and the pre-mortem (Round 06) "customer builds it cheaper" failure mode.
