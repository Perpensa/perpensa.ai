## Why price matters to this specific model

LiT's royalty is **1% of total mine production value on an NSR basis** (Cited, LiT_Financial_Projections_2026.md). Revenue is therefore directly proportional to the dollar value of the licensee's output, which is metal volume times metal price. A 1x move in price is a ~1x move in royalty revenue, before any volume change. The model's Year-5 royalty run-rate (~$57.2M, Cited) implicitly assumes a price environment for the underlying commodities; the base does not show which price deck or which commodities. The commodities in scope follow from LiT's recovery thesis: **lithium** (Stage Two spodumene leaching) and **fine-particle / rare-earth and base-metal fines** (Stage One HHS surface chemistry). Both are volatile, and both are in a structurally different place in 2026 than when a static projection would typically be built.

## Lithium: violent volatility, 2026 deficit consensus, wide forecast spread

- **Spot, mid-2026:** battery-grade lithium carbonate surged in Q1 2026 to roughly **$26,278/t**, after roughly doubling off the 2025 trough. Cited (InvestingNews Q1 2026 Lithium Market).
- **Forward decks diverge by ~2x.** UBS: average battery-grade carbonate ~**$18,900/t in 2026**, ~**$28,000/t in 2027**, ~**$25,200/t in 2028** (135k / 200k / 180k RMB per tonne). An earlier Goldman Sachs deck had carbonate far lower at ~**$13,250/t in 2026** rising to ~**$17,077/t by 2028**. Cited (carboncredits.com / SunSirs re: UBS; InvestingNews re: Goldman). The gap between these two reputable decks is roughly 2x, which is itself the headline finding: there is no tight consensus to anchor a single revenue line.
- **Balance:** UBS sees the market flipping from a 2025 **surplus** to a **continuous deficit across 2026-2028**, with 2026 supply growth ~17% against demand growth ~29%. Cited (SunSirs re: UBS). Longer run, demand is projected at ~**4.6M tonnes LCE by 2030**, led by EVs plus grid storage. Cited (InvestingNews re: Arcane Capital). Energy storage (ESS), not just EVs, is now a primary incremental driver (412 GWh in 2026, 544 GWh in 2027 cited). Cited.

**Read for SPOT/Round 04:** the directional macro is favorable to a price-linked royalty (deficit, structural demand growth), but the level is unknowable to better than roughly a 2x band over the forecast horizon, and the 2026 spot is a sharp spike off a trough, not a settled level. Any royalty-revenue line tied to lithium must be modeled as a band, not a point, with a downside leg at least back toward the Goldman-style ~$13k/t deck. Assumption (High) that a 2x sensitivity band is the minimum honest treatment.

## Rare earths: deficit and scarcity premium, but extreme intra-year swings

- **Spot, mid-2026:** Pr-Nd (NdPr) oxide ~**$90,317/t (~$90.3/kg)** as of 1 June 2026; neodymium metal ~**$121.95/kg** on the SMM China benchmark. Cited (critical-minerals-news.com; rare-earth-mining.com).
- **2026 path is violently nonlinear:** NdPr opened 2026 at ~**$53/kg**, spiked to ~**$136.7-139.6/kg** by end-April (~160% YTD), then corrected to ~$90/kg by 1 June, still ~**70% above the year-open**. Cited (rare-earth-mining.com). This is even more volatile than lithium and cautions hard against any straight-line REE assumption.
- **Balance:** NdPr is in **supply deficit for a second consecutive year in 2026**, with EV/wind/robotics demand outpacing Chinese quota growth and Western ramp; ~**8.4% CAGR for NdPr to 2035** projected. Deficits flagged as especially acute for **dysprosium and terbium**. Cited (critical-minerals-news.com; strategicmetalsinvest.com).
- **China control and price bifurcation (overlaps CHOKEPOINT/W-geo themes):** China controls ~**92% of refined NdPr** and ~**98-99% of heavy REE (Dy, Tb)**; an ex-China price premium of up to **5x** is emerging for heavies. Cited (InvestingNews / AD Little re: control shares; rare-earth-mining.com re: bifurcation). For a recovery technology positioned as non-China, allied-supply-relevant, this is a tailwind to the strategic narrative but does **not** stabilize the price line.

**Read for SPOT/Round 04:** REE balances favor a recovery-technology thesis (deficit, scarcity premium, supply-security demand), but realized REE prices swung ~2.6x within the first five months of 2026 alone. A royalty tied to REE output is even more band-dependent than lithium and should be sensitivity-tested across a wider range.

## Findings for SPOT

1. **The royalty base is a leveraged bet on volatile commodity prices, and the projections appear to be a single point on that distribution.** Because royalty = 1% x volume x price, and both lithium and REE prices carry roughly 2x (lithium) to ~2.6x-intra-year (REE) uncertainty, the Year-5 ~$57.2M run-rate is one draw, not a base case. Round 04 must replace it with a banded revenue model: a downside leg (lithium back to ~$13k/t Goldman-style, REE off its spike) and an upside leg (UBS 2027 lithium peak, REE deficit premium). Assumption (High).

2. **Direction supports the thesis; level does not support the projection.** The structural story (deficits in both lithium and NdPr, demand led by EVs + ESS + wind, China-concentration premium for ex-China supply) is genuinely favorable to a recovery technology and to a price-linked royalty, and should be recorded as an Opportunity. But favorable direction is not the same as a defensible point forecast; the base's apparent single-deck assumption is the weakness, not the macro. Cited (direction) + Assumption (High) (level critique).

3. **Commodity mix is undocumented and matters.** The base never states how the $229M/$2.29B per-operator production-value bases split across lithium vs REE vs base-metal fines (that derivation is [Company] C2). Because lithium and REE are on different price clocks, the revenue sensitivity depends on the unstated mix. Until C2 is retrieved, Round 04 should sensitivity-test the mix itself, not just the price level. Assumption (Moderate-High). Cross-link: C2.
