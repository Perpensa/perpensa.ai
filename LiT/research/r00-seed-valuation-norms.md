## 2. How pre-revenue deep-tech is actually priced

There is no revenue or cash flow to anchor a multiple, so practitioners use **structured-narrative methods** rather than formulas. Named methods include **Berkus** (milestone-driven), **First Chicago** (probability-weighted scenario blend of downside/base/upside), and scorecard/risk-factor adjustments. The disciplined form is: *peer-stage baseline ~$X; claimed uplift 1.Y× on scorecard factors; risk table adjusts ±$Z.* **Cited** (The Scenarionist, "Pre-Revenue Valuation in Deep Tech").

**Implication for Round 04.** The royalty/returns model should not be argued from a single point valuation. It should be built as a **First-Chicago-style scenario blend** (downside / base / upside), which also dovetails with C4 (no downside sheet exists in the locked base — Round 04 must construct downside cases de novo). **Assumption(High)** that this is the right Round-04 method.

## 3. Milestone-tranche norms (the term-structure recommendation)

**A tranched financing** funds a committed round in installments, with **later tranches closing only if agreed milestones are hit.** **Cited** (Allied VC; NY Venture Hub).

**Standardization (new, material).** The **NVCA published standardized tranched-financing terms in October 2025.** Practitioner takeaways: **Cited** (NY Venture Hub, 2025-11-17):
- The hard part is **milestone specificity** — e.g. "$2M ARR by Q2 2026" is ambiguous (GAAP vs annualized-from-a-quarter vs collected cash; who certifies). Milestones must be **Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Relevant, Time-bound.**
- Verification should use **board certification or independent third-party validation** to reduce gamesmanship and closing risk.
- Standard mechanics include a **grace period (~60 days)** for milestone achievement.
- Pricing: simple deals **price every tranche the same**; customized deals **re-price later tranches** (discount/premium tied to hitting/missing the milestone).

**Why it fits this deal.** Tranching lets the investor **cap exposure** — if the first proof milestone fails, later tranches are not released. It also **reduces founder dilution** versus one large up-front round priced at the risky early stage. Life-science investors have used this for decades because clinical milestones invite it; the same logic applies to a **bench → pilot → first-license** technology path. **Cited / Assumption(High).**

**Recommended milestone ladder for LiT (for Round 04/05 to ratify).** **Assumption(Moderate)** — derived from this deal's own proof gaps, not from a retrieved comp:
- **Tranche 1 (close):** funds independent third-party validation of the 16–19% recovery dataset (resolves C1) and an IP remediation step (resolves the W1/C3 gate item — see `research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md`). These are the two thesis-overturning unknowns; the largest risk capital should sit *behind* them, not in front.
- **Tranche 2 (release on validated bench data + clean/filed IP):** funds the pilot campaign. Tie release to **board certification or third-party sign-off**, not a self-reported number.
- **Tranche 3 (release on a piloted recovery result and a converted LOI/first license):** funds scale-up. Note from W3/W9 that Month 10/16 milestones are campaign *entry*, not validated revenue — the tranche trigger should be the *result*, not the *start*, of the campaign.
