## What LiT claims for Stage Two (from the locked base)

- Stage Two is a "low-temperature, low harm leaching process" that "operates well below 500C," with ~30% energy reduction and ~20% faster processing versus conventional, and "no acidic byproducts." Bench-proven for lithium; designed to adapt to other minerals later. Cited (background/LiT_Pitch_2026.md, LiT_Business_Plan_2026.md).
- The platform is described as protected under a single integrated PCT filing; W1 found no granted/published LiT claim on the public record. The Stage Two novelty question therefore matters twice: for technical differentiation and for whether anything patentable remains unclaimed by prior art. Cited (research/r00-ip-patent-landscape.md).

## The conventional baseline LiT is measured against

- The dominant commercial spodumene route calcines alpha-spodumene at ~1050-1100C to drive the alpha-to-beta phase conversion (decrepitation, ~30% lattice expansion that makes the otherwise inert ore reactive), then performs a sulfuric acid roast at ~250C, followed by water leach. Cited (feeco.com; MDPI Minerals 2024 "Aspects of Spodumene Lithium Extraction Techniques," 16/19/8513; ResearchGate roasting review 344447441).
- So the energy-intensive step conventional processing is criticized for is the ~1050C decrepitation, not the acid roast (which is already ~250C). Any route that avoids high-temperature decrepitation is the meaningful low-temperature competitor. Assumption (High) — direct reading of the process description above.

## Findings for CRUCIBLE and CLAIM

1. **The "well below 500C" claim is not category-novel.** Multiple published routes already extract lithium from spodumene at 250-325C with 90-99% reported recovery, several of them acid-free (NaOH, composite-salt, CaO/NaOH). LiT's headline number (operating below 500C, no acidic byproducts) describes a space that academic groups have already occupied. Novelty, if any, must live in the specific chemistry, kinetics, or the integration with Stage One physical separation, not in the temperature or "no acid" framing alone. Assumption (High) — supported by the prior-art map; the exact LiT chemistry is in the [Company] queue (C1/C3) and unretrievable, so a definitive overlap/clearance read is not possible here.

2. **The energy-saving story is directionally real but the ~30% figure is unverifiable and possibly conservative against the literature.** Conventional energy intensity is dominated by ~1050C decrepitation; routes that avoid it can plausibly cut more than 30%. LiT's ~30% claim is therefore credible as a floor but is Unsupported as a specific number absent the bench dataset (C1). Assumption (Moderate) for direction; Unsupported for the exact 30%.

3. **Prior art sits at the same technology-readiness level as LiT.** Every competing route above is bench/lab scale, with no spodumene low-temperature route identified at commercial scale. This cuts both ways: LiT is not behind a deployed competitor, but it also cannot claim a maturity lead, and the well-known bench-to-pilot mortality for these processes (see W3) applies to LiT equally. Assumption (High).

4. **"No acidic byproducts" is defensible only for the alkaline variants.** If LiT's Stage Two is alkali/caustic based (like the NaOH or CaO routes), "no acidic byproducts" is technically coherent but introduces caustic-handling and silicate-residue streams that are their own environmental and cost burden, a point WATERSHED should carry into claim-substantiation (W13). If LiT's route is acid-based (phosphoric/maleic class), the "no acidic byproducts" claim is in tension with the chemistry. Which family LiT uses is unretrievable (C1/C3). Assumption (Moderate) — flagged for cross-examination in Round 02.
