## Bottom line for the gate

1. LiT's only retrievable IP is a single **provisional** patent application, self-announced 2024-03-26. No PCT or national-phase **publication** for Lithium Technologies, Inc. / Keenum can be retrieved in the public record. A provisional is an unexamined 12-month placeholder, never "granted." On the public record the **"globally patented platform" claim is Unsupported.**
2. The named Stage One **surface-chemistry / fine-particle background IP** maps to the **Virginia Tech Intellectual Properties (VTIP) Hydrophobic-Hydrophilic Separation (HHS)** family invented by Yoon (and Luttrell on the older flotation art). The commercially live family has **priority 2012-06-11, expiry ~2033-06-11**, and is **owned by VTIP, not by LiT.**
3. **Minerals Refining Company (MRC)** is VTIP's named licensee/commercializer of HHS. The **scope of MRC's license (exclusive vs non-exclusive, worldwide, and the field-of-use boundary between coal, "minerals," and lithium) is not disclosed in any public source.** This is the single most important unresolved FTO question and it gates whether LiT has clean room to license the surface-chemistry step at all.

## 1. LiT's own filing

**Cited.** LiT's patent page describes one filing titled *"Extraction and Refinement of Lithium Compounds Utilizing Flexible Feedstock and Non-Acidic Reduced-Temperature Leaching Methods,"* assignee **Lithium Technologies, Inc. (Forest, VA)**, announced **2024-03-26**, covering a lithium extraction process effective on flexible feedstock including waste rock below 1% Li and hard-rock spodumene. The page characterizes it as a **provisional** patent. Contact: Brian Keenum, bkeenum@lithiumtechnologies.co. [lithiumtechnologies.co/provisionalpatent]

**Cited (record absence).** No PCT (WO), US non-provisional publication, or foreign national-phase publication naming Lithium Technologies, Inc. or Keenum for this subject matter was retrievable via WebSearch on patents.google.com, justia, or USPTO-indexed results. The only retrievable LiT IP artifact is the company's own announcement page. [WebSearch, multiple queries, 2026-06-29]

**Assumption (Moderate-High).** A US provisional expires 12 months after filing. A provisional "filed and granted as of 2024-03-26" had a conversion deadline of roughly **2025-03-26** to file a non-provisional and/or PCT claiming its priority. The website's phrase "filed and granted" is **technically incorrect**: provisionals are never examined or granted. *Why:* this is settled USPTO procedure, not a fact about LiT. *Use:* treat "granted" language in LiT materials as a marketing overstatement, not evidence of an enforceable right.

**Assumption (Low-Moderate) — do not overclaim the negative.** The absence of a published non-provisional today is **not conclusive proof none was filed.** A non-provisional or PCT filed near the 2025-03 deadline would not publish until ~18 months later (≈2026-09 or later), so it could exist and still be unpublished as of 2026-06-29. *Use:* the correct finding is "no granted or published right exists on the public record," **not** "LiT definitely abandoned the filing." The distinction matters for how hard to flag the Strength.

**Unsupported (flag).** "Globally patented platform." There is no retrievable granted patent in any jurisdiction, and a provisional confers no enforceable rights anywhere. Any deliverable language implying issued or global protection is unsupported and must be demoted.

> **NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** (i) whether LiT converted the 2024-03 provisional into a non-provisional and/or PCT before the 2025-03 bar, and the application serial/PCT number; (ii) USPTO filing receipts. Look in: USPTO Patent Center / Public PAIR by applicant name, WIPO PatentScope, and LiT's data room. This is [Company] item **C3** and remains gated.

## 2. The VTIP / Yoon-Luttrell HHS patent family (LiT's named Stage One background IP)

The "fine-particle surface-chemistry" and "HHS" art LiT names as Stage One background traces to Virginia Tech's Center for Advanced Separation Technologies (Yoon, Luttrell).

**Cited — foundational, EXPIRED:**
- **US4,981,582A** "Process and apparatus for separating fine particles by microbubble flotation…" — inventors **Yoon, Adel, Luttrell**; assignee **VTIP**; priority **1988-01-27**; granted **1991-01-01**; **status Expired (lifetime), ~2008-2011.** [patents.google.com/patent/US4981582A]
- **US5,814,210A** (Yoon et al., VTIP) and **US5,459,786A** (dewatering fine coal with recyclable non-polar liquids) are older Yoon column-flotation/dewatering patents; on their 1990s priorities these are **Assumption(High): expired.** [WebSearch; vtechworks.lib.vt.edu]

**Cited — the commercially live HHS family (this is the one that matters):**
- Title **"Methods for Separating and Dewatering Fine Particles"** — inventor **Roe-Hoan Yoon**; assignee **VTIP**; **priority 2012-06-11**, filed 2013-06-11; **status Active; anticipated expiry 2033-06-11.**
- Family members: **US10,913,912B2** (granted 2021-02-09), **CA2875024A1**, **WO2013188419A1**, **EP2861352B1** (granted 2019), **AU2013274408B2**, **CN104582856B**, **BR112014030622B1**, plus US app **2019/0338209** ("Methods for Separating and Dewatering Fine Particles," VTIP). It is a **multi-jurisdiction family** (US, EP, CA, AU, CN, BR). [patents.google.com/patent/CA2875024A1; freepatentsonline.com/y2019/0338209.html]
- Claim core: a **two-step hydrophobic-liquid agglomeration-then-dispersion** process that removes entrapped water and hydrophilic impurities from fine particles. Enumerated feeds: **coal, base-metal sulfides, precious metals, platinum-group metals, rare-earth minerals.** **No mention of lithium.** [patents.google.com/patent/CA2875024A1]

**Implications:**
- **Cited/Assumption(High):** The live HHS background IP is **owned by VTIP, not LiT.** If LiT's Stage One depends on HHS surface chemistry, LiT needs a **license or sublicense from VTIP** to practice it. LiT's own provisional cannot grant rights over VTIP's prior, broader family.
- **Assumption (Moderate):** The HHS claims enumerate minerals but **not lithium**, which *may* leave a lithium-bearing-feed field-of-use gap. Whether that gap is real depends on (a) the literal claim language (agglomeration of any hydrophobic fine particle would read broadly) and (b) whether MRC's license already encloses "minerals." Do not treat the lithium silence as a clean opening without a claim-chart and the MRC license terms.
- **Assumption (High):** Expiry **2033** means the core HHS family runs ~7 more years. Anything LiT licenses now sits under VTIP's term, not a fresh 20-year LiT term.

## 3. Minerals Refining Company (MRC) and the license scope

**Cited:** MRC was **launched in 2012** by industry figure E. Morgan Massey with Yoon and Luttrell, to commercialize the HHS process developed at Virginia Tech; a 100-lb/hr proof-of-concept was built at VT in 2013 and the process underwent ~1,200 hours of field/pilot testing at a Southwest Virginia coal preparation plant (2015-2017). MRC states the **HHS process is "licensed by MRC."** Yoon is credited with "more than 70 U.S. and international patents." [mineralsrefining.com/about-mrc/, /hhs-technology/, /hhs-pilot-plant-completes-safety-testing/]

**Cited (record absence) — the critical gap:** No public MRC or VTIP source discloses whether MRC's license is **exclusive or non-exclusive, its territory, or its field-of-use limits** (coal only, all minerals, or inclusive of lithium-bearing ores). MRC's public materials center on **coal**, with general references to "fine coal and minerals." [WebFetch mineralsrefining.com/about-mrc/, 2026-06-29]

**Why this is the gate within the gate:**
- If MRC holds a **worldwide exclusive over the HHS family across "minerals,"** lithium-ore separation may fall **inside MRC's exclusive field**, and LiT would need MRC's consent/sublicense, not just VTIP's. That converts a claimed Strength into a **third-party-dependency Weakness/Threat.**
- If MRC's exclusivity is **field-limited to coal**, VTIP could license the **minerals/lithium field** to LiT cleanly. That preserves the Strength but still makes LiT a **VTIP licensee, not an owner.**
- Either way, LiT does **not own** the live surface-chemistry background IP.

> **NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** the actual VTIP→MRC license (exclusivity, territory, field of use, sublicensing rights) and any VTIP→LiT license/option. Look in: VTIP/Virginia Tech tech-transfer records, MRC corporate filings, and LiT's data room. Overlaps [Company] item **C3**. Not web-retrievable; gated.

## 4. What this resolves for the DISSENT gate and Round 01

- **Gate (a) — does LiT control clean IP in Stage One and hold a published/granted filing?** Answer on the public record: **No granted or published filing exists.** LiT holds, at most, an **unexamined provisional that may have lapsed**, and the live surface-chemistry background IP is **VTIP's**, commercialized through **MRC under undisclosed terms.** Gate (a) is **not cleared by web evidence**; it stays open pending [Company] items C3 (and the FTO read).
- **SWOT consequence (carry into R01):** the "globally patented platform" item enters as a **Weakness/Threat-leaning contested item**, not a Strength. Per the standing DISSENT ruling, if C1/C3 cannot be retrieved, the affected Strengths are **demoted to Assumptions and flagged High-risk / Low-confidence**, and IP remediation (convert/refile, secure a written VTIP±MRC license with a defined lithium field of use) is treated as a **precondition to proceed.**
- **Possible upside to test, not assert:** a lithium field-of-use **gap** in the HHS claims plus a coal-limited MRC exclusivity *could* give VTIP room to license LiT cleanly. This is an **Assumption(Low-Moderate)** to be resolved by claim-chart + license review, not a finding.
