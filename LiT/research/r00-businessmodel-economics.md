# Research W5 — Technology-as-a-Service / Build-Own-Operate vs Pure Licensing Economics

**Round:** 00 Grounding & Research Commissioning
**Register item:** W5 (P1). Requesting seats: ROYALTY 2, DISSENT 2.
**Decision it unblocks:** Whether LiT's licensing-first model out-returns a producer/service/hybrid model; this directly seeds the Round 03 option set and answers DISSENT gate (c) at the public-record level ("is the licensing model even the right model?").
**Method:** Moderator WebSearch over DLE and tailings-reprocessing business-model analogs (Phoenix Tailings as the producer comparator; the DLE market's product-type split as the model-mix benchmark; Koch/Aquatech Li-Pro and Standard Lithium as live licensing/JV examples).
**Confidence convention:** every line is tagged Cited, Assumption(band), or Unsupported.

## What LiT proposes (from the locked base)

- A **pure-licensing model**: upfront license fees ($2M small / $10M major) plus a 1%-of-NSR running royalty, with R&D outsourced to two universities and a lean corporate team. No plant is built, owned, or operated by LiT. Cited (background/LiT_Financial_Projections_2026.md; LiT_Prospectus.md).
- The capital ask is small ($2M equity raise, $13M pre-money) and the modeled margins are very high (operating margin 83%-93% in Years 3-5) precisely because LiT carries no production capex, no feedstock price risk, and no operating workforce. Cited (LiT_Financial_Projections_2026.md).
- First revenue is booked Year 3 (Month 25+), with the model reaching a ~$57M royalty run-rate by Year 5. Cited (LiT_Financial_Projections_2026.md).

The structural bet is therefore: **capture a thin slice of someone else's production with near-zero capital and near-zero opex**. W5 asks whether that bet out-returns the alternatives, and what the market actually chooses.

## The model spectrum, and where the market sits

Recovery-technology companies selling into miners choose along a spectrum from asset-light to asset-heavy:

1. **Pure licensing** — license IP, collect upfront + running royalty, build nothing. Asset-light, high-margin, but revenue is gated on the licensee's adoption decision and the licensor never controls the metal. This is LiT's choice.
2. **Technology-as-a-service (TaaS) / build-own-operate (BOO)** — the technology owner builds and runs the recovery unit (often on the customer's site or feedstock) and charges a service fee or takes a cut of recovered metal. Capital-heavier, but the owner controls deployment and captures more of the value created.
3. **Producer / merchant** — the company processes feedstock it controls and sells the finished metal, capturing the full spread. Most capital-intensive and most exposed to commodity price, but uncapped on upside and not dependent on a third party's adoption decision.

**The DLE market, the closest analog sector, does not default to pure licensing.** In the direct-lithium-extraction technology-and-services market, technology-as-a-service / build-own-operate is the **leading** product model at roughly **36% of the market**, explicitly because it lets producers access the technology without heavy capex while giving the technology owner recurring revenue and deployment control. Technology licensing exists alongside it but is not the dominant mode. Cited (Fact.MR DLE Technology and Services Market report). This is direct evidence against the premise that licensing-first is the natural or revenue-maximizing structure for selling recovery technology to miners.

**Live licensing analogs attach performance guarantees and full-flowsheet delivery, not just IP.** Koch's Li-Pro DLE (now folded into Aquatech's PEARL platform) is offered "under technology license as part of full flowsheet licensing offerings ... scaled-up, tested and derisked, with performance guarantees for its commercial deployment." Cited (Koch Technology Solutions). The lesson for LiT: even where licensing is used, the licensor typically delivers a proven, guaranteed flowsheet, which presupposes pilot-scale validation LiT has not yet done (see C1, W3). A license with no demonstrated plant behind it is a weaker product than the licensing comparables.

## The producer comparator: Phoenix Tailings

Phoenix Tailings is the most on-point comparator for LiT's actual feedstock thesis (recovering critical metals from mining tailings/waste), and it chose the **opposite** model: it is a **producer/refiner**, not a licensor. It recovers rare earth metals (Nd, Dy, Tb) from tailings and byproducts via electrochemistry, operates its own refining facilities in Massachusetts and New Hampshire, and ships finished metal globally. Cited (azomining.com; Phoenix Tailings press; NAI500).

What the producer route required and produced:
- **Far more capital.** Phoenix Tailings raised a ~$76M strategic round (a $33M extension on top of $43M), plus a **$66M U.S. Department of Energy grant** and additional federal awards. Cited (BusinessWire 2025-04-30; Manila Times/GlobeNewswire 2026-06-04 re: $66M DOE; BusinessWire 2025-12-04 re: $1.6M). This is two orders of magnitude above LiT's $2M ask, which is the price of controlling the metal and the plant.
- **Model variants within the producer route**: tolling (customer supplies oxide, pays a per-kg conversion fee, low working capital and low price risk) versus merchant sales (buy oxide, sell metal at a premium, higher margin but inventory and price risk). Cited (search synthesis of producer-model descriptions). These are the hybrid structures Round 03 should put on the table against pure licensing.

## Findings for ROYALTY and DISSENT

1. **The market does not validate "licensing is the obvious model."** The dominant structure in the closest analog sector (DLE) is TaaS/build-own-operate, and the closest feedstock analog (Phoenix Tailings) is a producer. Pure licensing is a real option but a minority one, chosen for capital-lightness, not for return-maximization. DISSENT gate (c) is therefore **not** closed in licensing's favor by external evidence; the model choice is genuinely contested and must be carried into Round 03 as an open decision, not an assumption. Assumption (High).

2. **Licensing trades return ceiling for capital-lightness, and the LiT model is built entirely around the trade.** LiT's headline margins (83%-93%) are an artifact of carrying no plant and no metal, not evidence of a superior model. A producer captures the full recovered-metal value (16-19 incremental cents per dollar on the treated stream, per LiT's own framing) instead of a 1% slice of whole-mine NSR; a TaaS/BOO operator captures something in between. On a per-deployment basis the licensing model very likely leaves the majority of the value LiT creates on the customer's side of the table. Whether the licensor's lower capital and faster (claimed) scaling makes up for the lower per-deal capture is exactly the Round 03/04 trade-off, and it cannot be assumed away. Assumption (High).

3. **The licensing model's revenue is adoption-gated; the producer model's is not.** A licensor earns nothing until an operator chooses to adopt, qualify, and deploy on its own feedstock (W4, W9 show this leg is long and licensor-funded). A producer earns as soon as it processes feedstock it controls. For a pre-pilot company with unproven recovery data (C1), licensing concentrates all risk at the single hardest gate (third-party adoption) while forgoing the one path (own-the-plant) that does not depend on convincing a major to change its flowsheet. This is a strategic fragility of the chosen model, not merely a slower ramp. Assumption (Moderate-High).

4. **A hybrid is the live middle option and should be a named Round 03 alternative.** The realistic alternatives to pure licensing are: (a) licensing-with-performance-guarantee delivered as a validated flowsheet (Koch/Aquatech shape), which raises the validation bar and cost; (b) TaaS/BOO on the customer's site, capturing a metal-linked service fee (DLE-market dominant shape); (c) a producer/tolling play on controlled tailings (Phoenix Tailings shape), requiring 10x-30x more capital but capturing full spread and removing adoption-gating. ROYALTY should structure at least these three against LiT's base case in Round 03, with W6/W8 feeding the return comparison. Assumption (High).
