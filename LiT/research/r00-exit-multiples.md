## What the public record actually shows on multiples

There is no clean public comp for "minerals process-tech licensing business acquired at Nx revenue." The closest observable cohorts and their multiples:

1. **All software M&A (the cohort LiT's revenue multiple most resembles in shape, a high-margin IP/licensing model):** average **6.4x revenue**, median **2.6x revenue**; average **16.3x EBITDA**, median **10.2x EBITDA**. Cited (firstpagesage.com; clearlyacquired.com, 2025 datasets).

2. **SaaS specifically:** 2024 median **~4.1x revenue**; private-equity buyers value profitable SaaS at **15x-25x EBITDA**, with top-quartile/high-growth assets reaching far higher. Cited (saasrise.com SaaS M&A Report 2025; clearlyacquired.com).

3. **IT services M&A:** median **EV/EBITDA ~8.8x-10.2x** over the last decade, Q4 2025 update **8.8x**. Cited (aventis-advisors.com IT Services Valuation Multiples).

4. **CleanTech (the cohort closest to an industrial decarbonization/recovery hardware-plus-process business):** median **EV/Revenue 1.8x** in Q4 2024; median **EV/EBITDA 10.6x** (last published Q4 2022; no updated 2024 EBITDA figure). The cohort is the CTEC ETF of ~40 listed companies, ~65% revenue-positive and ~50% EBITDA-positive. Cited (finerva.com CleanTech 2024 Valuation Multiples).

5. **Broad clean-tech / power M&A market context:** ~$105B across 234 deals in 2024; in 2025 a buyer's market with ~89% of exits being trade acquisitions (not IPOs). Cited (euci.com; cleantech.com). Relevant because it sets the likely exit as a strategic acquisition, consistent with LiT's framing, but in a buyer-favorable environment.

## Findings for CAPITAL (exit realism)

1. **The 12-15x EBITDA target is plausible-to-aggressive, defensible only if LiT is valued as a high-growth software/SaaS-like IP licensor, not as cleantech/industrial.** Software median EBITDA is 10.2x and IT services ~8.8x; PE pays 15-25x only for *profitable, high-growth* SaaS. 12-15x sits above the software median and inside the high-growth-SaaS band. For a capital-intensive minerals process business the cleantech EBITDA comp (10.6x, last seen 2022) is the more honest anchor, which puts 12-15x at the optimistic edge. Assumption (Moderate). Caveat: at exit LiT is unlikely to have meaningful EBITDA at all on the stated timeline (pre/early-revenue, see W3/W9), so an EBITDA multiple may be inapplicable and the deal would price on revenue, pipeline, or IP.

2. **The 8-10x revenue target is well above every observable industrial/cleantech comp and at or beyond the high end of software.** All-software median revenue multiple is 2.6x (average 6.4x); SaaS 2024 median ~4.1x; cleantech median EV/Revenue **1.8x**. 8-10x revenue is **~4x to 5x the cleantech revenue comp** and above the software average. A revenue multiple of that magnitude is reserved for hyper-growth software with durable gross margins, not a minerals licensor with concentrated, slow-converting customers. Assumption (High) that 8-10x revenue is unsupported as a base case and belongs as an upside-only scenario in Round 04. The base case should center nearer 2-4x revenue, with the EBITDA path applicable only if and when EBITDA exists.

3. **The named analogs (UOP, Metso, Hatch) do not substantiate the multiples and partly cut against them.** These are large established process-technology and engineering franchises, not recent acquisition targets bought at a published Nx-revenue multiple; their own transaction terms are confidential. Metso is itself a consolidator (formed 2020 from Metso Minerals + Outotec), i.e. a likely *acquirer* of a business like LiT rather than a multiple comp. Using them as exit-multiple evidence conflates "who might buy us" with "what multiple was paid." Assumption (High). `NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL: a realized acquisition of a minerals/metals process-licensing business with a disclosed EV/revenue or EV/EBITDA multiple.`

4. **Mining/critical-minerals exits are commonly structured as upfront cash plus a price-linked earnout, not a single clean multiple paid in full at close.** Example structure in the sector: Anglo American's 2024 sale of Australian steelmaking-coal assets for up to $3.875B = $2.3B upfront plus up to $1.575B price-linked earnout (~40% of headline value contingent). Cited (dakota.com / sector reporting via WebSearch). Implication for returns modeling: a headline "8-10x revenue" could in practice be a lower guaranteed component plus contingent earnout/royalty tied to commodity price and deployment milestones, which lengthens and de-risks-for-the-buyer the realized payout. Round 04 should model exit proceeds as a probability-weighted upfront-plus-earnout, not a deterministic multiple. Assumption (Moderate-High).

5. **Private-round valuations are not exit multiples and should not be cited as such.** Jetti Resources, the closest live mining-process-tech analog, was last marked at a **$2.5B private valuation in a venture round** (BHP-backed), not an acquisition; it has no disclosed exit multiple. A venture mark reflects option value and strategic-investor positioning, not what a trade buyer pays per dollar of revenue/EBITDA. Cited (mining.com; cbinsights.com). Do not import Jetti's $2.5B as an exit comp.

---

## Net read for the deliberation

- **8-10x revenue:** Unsupported as a base case. Recenter to ~2-4x revenue base, hold 8x+ as an upside-only scenario contingent on a software-like growth/margin profile that LiT has not yet demonstrated.
- **12-15x EBITDA:** Assumption (Moderate), optimistic edge; likely inapplicable on the stated timeline because LiT is pre/early-EBITDA at the modeled exit. The deal more probably prices on revenue, pipeline, and IP.
- **Structure:** Expect upfront cash + price-linked/milestone earnout in a 2025-era buyer's market, not a full multiple paid at close. Model exit proceeds probabilistically.
- **Named analogs and Jetti's $2.5B:** Do not use as multiple evidence; the analogs are likely acquirers and Jetti's mark is a venture round, not an exit.

This feeds Round 04 exit-value sensitivity (widen the band downward; split guaranteed vs contingent proceeds) and Round 03 exit-path options (trade sale as base, with earnout structure as the likely form). Pairs with W5 (producer-vs-licensing economics) and W7 (entry valuation norms).
