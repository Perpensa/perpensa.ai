## What the company is (grounding summary, Cited to background/)

- A **process-technology licensing company**, not a producer. Two-stage platform: (1) proprietary physical separation effective in fine-particle/ultrafine ranges where conventional methods underperform; (2) low-temperature (sub-500C) chemical extraction, bench-proven for lithium. Claimed 16-19% recovery improvement, ~30% energy reduction, ~20% faster, no acidic byproducts. [Cited: Business Plan, Pitch]
- **Stage of maturity:** bench-scale validation only, across lithium spodumene, REE hard rock, and tailings. Not yet pilot-scale. [Cited: Business Plan milestones, Pitch slide 11]
- **IP position:** a single internationally filed PCT application covering the integrated two-stage process. National-phase filings (US, India, Australia) are a use-of-funds line item, not yet completed. [Cited: Business Plan, Ask section]
- **Lead value proposition:** tailings/fine-particle recovery (no new permits, sunk-cost feedstock, immediate global applicability). [Cited: Business Plan, Pitch slide 4]
- **Business model:** upfront license fee + 1% NSR production royalty. Asset-light; avoids the $300M-$500M cost of a production facility. [Cited: Business Plan, Business Model section]
- **The ask:** $2.0M seed at $13M pre / $15M post (13.33%), eight milestone-gated quarterly tranches, projected to reach self-sustaining revenue in Year 3 without a bridge round. [Cited: Business Plan, The Ask]
- **Projected outcome:** Year 5 revenue ~$51.1M, ~$57.2M royalty run rate, 16 licensees (15 small/mid + 1 major), modeled exit $400M-$510M. [Cited: Business Plan, Financial Plan / Exit]
- **Team:** two principals today (CEO, COO), growing to seven by Year 5; advisory board with Albemarle and Freeport-McMoRan ties; R&D run largely through IIT (ISM) with VT validation. [Cited: Business Plan, Team]

## Where the evidence would read materially differently for an outside investor

To be carried only into the final frame statement, not re-argued throughout:

- The company frame treats the $2M raise and the projected 27x-34x return as **inputs** (capital to be deployed well). An investor frame would treat the return projection as the **output under test** and would discount it heavily for single-PCT IP risk, two-person team, zero signed licensees, bench-only validation, and the optimism embedded in the 10% penetration and major-operator assumptions.
- The company asks "how do we make this real and defensible?" The investor asks "is the modeled outcome credible enough, at this valuation, to fund?" Same evidence, opposite burden of proof.
