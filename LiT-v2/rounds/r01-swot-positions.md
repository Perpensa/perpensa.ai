## Tagging legend and binding discipline (from the evidence lock)

- **Cited** — supported by a named document in the locked base or a `research/r00-*.md` finding.
- **Assumption (confidence band)** — plausible but resting on an item that cannot be confirmed with the tools available; never presented as fact.
- **Unsupported** — asserted in LiT's own documents but corroborated by nothing in the base; may not enter a deliverable as fact.
- Established facts **F-1, F-2, F-3, F-3a** (sponsor stipulations) are treated as Cited facts about LiT's position.
- No claim resting on **U-1..U-20** rises above Assumption this round. The sublicensable-rights gap (U-1, with F-1) and the unvalidated recovery uplift (F-2) are carried as named load-bearing items. No site-specific tailings value, recovery percentage, or revenue figure is asserted as fact (U-13/U-14). The F-3a correction is enforced: the NSR royalty is fully indexed to commodity price, so the concern is full price-downside exposure, not price omission.

## CRUCIBLE — process science and technology

### Strengths
- **CRU-S1.** The chemical stage targets a genuine technical whitespace whose direction is corroborated in the open literature: sub-500C, acid-free lithium-from-spodumene routes are reproducible in principle (a 325C NaOH roast reaching ~99% Li recovery is documented), so the *class* of process LiT pursues is scientifically credible, not speculative. [Cited: `r00-tech-reproducibility.md`; Market Research Sec. 5] **Confidence: High on the route class; this does not validate LiT's specific uplift (see CRU-W1).**
- **CRU-S2.** The physical-separation stage rests on world-class lineage: the Yoon/CAST hydrophobic-hydrophilic separation science is real, high-quality, and demonstrated at commercial scale (MRC, 2.8 TPH). LiT's edge is best framed as *access to and integration of* that separation science with a low-temperature chemical stage. [Cited: `r00-ip-fto.md`] **Confidence: High that the science is real; ownership/rights are a Weakness, not a Strength (see CRU-W2).**
- **CRU-S3.** A fines/ultrafine-capable separation claim, if validated, addresses a documented structural gap: incumbents split the fine-particle field (Jameson Cell, IsaMill, Concorde Cell for fine; HydroFloat coarse), and conventional spodumene flotation deslimes and loses sub-30 micron and mica-hosted lithium to tailings. The headroom LiT aims at is real in the literature. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3; `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: Moderate; the *quantum* of LiT's capture is unvalidated.**

### Weaknesses
- **CRU-W1.** The headline 16-to-19 percent recovery improvement that drives the revenue model is undefined and unvalidated: no baseline, no feedstock-specific result, and no statement of whether it is percentage points or a relative gain. Validation is the explicit purpose of the seed raise, not a present capability. This is a single-point dependency at the center of the platform. [Cited as established fact: F-2; consistent with `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: High that it is unvalidated.**
- **CRU-W2.** LiT's only named background IP in the one executed contract is the chemical stage; the physical separation surface-chemistry method, described as the technical foundation, is not named as LiT background IP and maps onto VTIP-owned art. The platform's foundational stage may not be LiT's invention. [Cited: VT Agreement Art. 13.9(a); `r00-ip-fto.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **CRU-W3.** The repeated claim of "bench-scale validation across lithium spodumene, REE hard rock, and tailings" is not supported by any document in the base. The single executed study is a 3-month, $52,075, lithium-only conceptual-and-preconcentration engagement that disclaims any guaranteed yield. Integrated two-stage, multi-feedstock bench data is absent. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A, Art. 10; if deeper data exists it is off-base] **Confidence: High that the base does not contain it.**
- **CRU-W4.** The chemical stage does not yet extract REEs; REE chemical extraction is an R&D roadmap item (Months 16-36). Feedstock-agnosticism today applies to the physical stage only; the chemical stage is lithium-only. The "three validated feedstock families" claim outruns the present technology. [Cited: Business Plan Milestones] **Confidence: High.**

### Threats
- **CRU-T1.** The lithium-from-spodumene low-temperature field is densely worked by multiple institutions (LBNL's 600-800C alpha-spodumene route; VTIP's own published REE family), raising prior-art and reproducibility-parity risk: a competitor route reaching similar recovery erodes LiT's technical differentiation before it is even validated. [Cited: `r00-ip-fto.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**
- **CRU-T2.** A two-stage flowsheet has inter-stage recycle by construction, so batch bench results do not retire scale-up risk; the process must prove it reaches and holds continuous equilibrium in a continuous pilot. Under-scoping that campaign is the documented leading cause of project cost and schedule overruns. [Cited: `r00-pilot-benchmarks.md`] **Confidence: High.**

## SPOT — market, demand, and commodity-price macro

### Strengths
- **SPO-S1.** LiT addresses commodities with genuine structural demand: lithium demand growth and a battery-share rise toward storage (BESS demand grew ~51% in 2025 to roughly a fifth of battery demand) give the underlying baskets a real long-run pull. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1; `r00-price-update.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High on direction.**

### Opportunities
- **SPO-O1.** The multi-year supply/demand balance is tightening in both target baskets: lithium's 2026 surplus is narrowing toward balance with credible deficit calls (Morgan Stanley ~80kt, UBS ~22kt), a structural spodumene deficit is forming, and NdPr is in a second consecutive year of deficit. This supports the direction of LiT's price exposure. [Cited: `r00-price-update.md`] **Confidence: Medium-High.**
- **SPO-O2.** The tailings resource LiT targets is physically vast (~16 Bt produced annually, ~282 Bt inventory; ~217 billion m3 in ~8,500 facilities globally) and commercially under-exploited (only ~25% of Qualified Persons have evaluated reprocessing), so the addressable category is large and early. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1 and 4; `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: High at category level; site value is Unsupported.**

### Weaknesses
- **SPO-W1.** LiT's documents present demand as one-directional growth and omit the price cycle the revenue model sits on. A plan that does not show the cycle understates its own central risk. [Cited: Business Plan Target Market vs Market Research Sec. 1] **Confidence: High.**

### Threats
- **SPO-T1.** Commodity-price volatility is a first-order threat, demonstrated in real time: lithium round-tripped two-year highs inside H1 2026 (peak ~$26,000/t, mid-year ~$11,000/t LCE near the marginal-cost floor), and NdPr fell ~35% from its end-April peak in roughly two months after a ~160% rally. A royalty booked at any single price point is fragile. [Cited: `r00-price-update.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **SPO-T2.** Lithium upside is structurally capped by fast swing-supply restarts (Mineral Resources restarting Bald Hill, Core Lithium restarting Finniss), so the lithium leg of the royalty should not be modeled with open-ended upside. [Cited: `r00-price-update.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **SPO-T3.** The near-term licensee pipeline is thinner than the demand story implies: lithium feasibility studies collapsed to fewer than 10 in 2025 with 12-month-plus delays, so new-mine licensees, a stated segment, are a constrained near-term channel. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

## CLAIM — feedstock geology and addressable-feedstock realism

### Strengths
- **CLA-S1.** The category-level value premise is directionally supported: fine/ultrafine and mica-hosted lithium is a documented structural loss in conventional spodumene flowsheets (fines below ~30 microns are commonly deslimed and lost; one DMS example loses ~8% of lithium to tailings before flotation), so recoverable headroom exists in principle across the feedstock category. [Cited: `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: Moderate at category level.**

### Weaknesses
- **CLA-W1.** Tailings grade, the variable that determines whether any impoundment is worth treating, is uncharacterized anywhere in the base. Tailings value depends entirely on residual grade, mineralogy, tonnage, and particle-size distribution per site, none of which is established. "Decades of accumulated value" is supported only at the macro inventory level, never site-specifically. [Cited macro: `r00-tailings-grades.md`; site-level: Unsupported, U-13] **Confidence: High that it is uncharacterized.**
- **CLA-W2.** The only contracted test work touched lithium concentrates from mine and aggregate tailings in and around Virginia, targeting a >6% Li2O concentrate grade. No REE ore body and no REE tailings work appears in the base, so the REE and multi-feedstock geological basis is unevidenced. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A Task 2; CRU-W4 adjacent] **Confidence: High.**
- **CLA-W3.** The $229M per-operator and $2.29B major-operator "total mine production value" figures that drive the royalty have no per-site geological basis shown; they are model inputs without a resource foundation. [Cited as inputs: Business Plan Financial Plan; basis: Unsupported] **Confidence: High that the basis is absent.**

### Opportunities
- **CLA-O1.** REEs occur recoverably in tailings as discrete minerals, clay-adsorbed, and organically complexed, and category research suggests recovering ~90% of existing mining byproducts could supply nearly all US critical-mineral needs, so a grade-led site-selection strategy has real, if site-dependent, upside. [Cited: `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: Moderate at category level; site sizing is Assumption pending U-13.**

### Threats
- **CLA-T1.** Heterogeneity is itself a threat to a feedstock-agnostic pitch: REE abundance in tailings varies by source ore, processing era, and weathering with no representative grade, so a one-size process claim collides with per-site reality and forces costly per-site characterization before each deal. [Cited: `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

## ROYALTY — business model, licensing economics, deal terms

### Strengths
- **ROY-S1.** LiT's archetype is commercially proven and well-capitalized: Jetti Resources shows a bolt-on recovery technology at operating mines can be monetized on a licensing-plus-running-royalty basis and attract major-miner capital (BHP, Freeport; >$200M raised). The licensing channel is validated; LiT's model is not sui generis. [Cited: `r00-license-structures.md`, `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: High on the channel.**
- **ROY-S2.** An upfront fee plus running royalty is the standard, defensible shape for process-technology licenses, and a discrete-stream gross-revenue royalty (a located anchor: ~5% of a recovered tailings stream) fits LiT's tailings case cleanly. LiT has precedented, bankable structures available to it. [Cited: `r00-license-structures.md`] **Confidence: High on the structures.**

### Weaknesses
- **ROY-W1.** The central commercial mechanic is unprecedented and structurally mismatched: a 1% royalty on the operator's *total mine* net smelter return for a partial-stream process improvement has no located precedent. Real process licenses are paid on the value the technology adds (incremental metal recovered, throughput processed, or a discrete recovered stream), not on whole-mine output. Sophisticated operator counterparties will reprice this. [Cited: `r00-license-structures.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **ROY-W2.** The "1 cent per dollar for 16-to-19 cents" value-exchange holds only if the recovery uplift and the royalty share the same base. If the uplift applies to a sub-stream while the royalty applies to total NSR, the asserted exchange does not hold, and the uplift magnitude is itself unvalidated. [Cited: Business Plan Fee Structure; F-2] **Confidence: High that the equivalence is unproven.**
- **ROY-W3.** LiT's ability to sublicense to operators depends on holding sublicensable rights to the underlying VT/VTIP IP, which the public record does not establish and the only contract present makes contingent on a separate, royalty-bearing license election within six months of disclosure. A licensor that does not hold sublicensable rights cannot grant a whole-mine royalty. [Cited: VT Agreement Art. 13.4-13.6; `r00-ip-fto.md`; U-1] **Confidence: High that the rights are unconfirmed.**
- **ROY-W4.** LiT cannot anchor its royalty rate to a published comparable: exact terms for the closest deals (Jetti, NovaCell, Jameson) are confidential, so any specific rate LiT cites is Assumption pending primary benchmarking, not a market-validated figure. [Cited: `r00-license-structures.md`; U-5..U-7] **Confidence: High.**

### Threats
- **ROY-T1.** Royalty income is fully indexed to commodity price by construction (NSR is realized metal value), so the royalty base carries full price-downside exposure that compounds with the other aggressive revenue inputs rather than standing apart from them. [Cited as established fact: F-3a, F-3; `r00-price-update.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **ROY-T2.** The best-funded tailings comparator, Phoenix Tailings (~$148M raised), runs a build-own-operate producer model capturing full metal margin, competing for the same domestic-recovery narrative with a richer value-capture architecture than a thin royalty. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3; `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

### Strengths

[Cited: `r00-adoption-deployment.md`] **Confidence: High for modular delivery at pilot scale.**

### Weaknesses

The capacity to deliver the recovery results that justify renewals is thinly resourced. [Cited: Business Plan Personnel; Financial Projections Sheet 1; `r00-adoption-deployment.md`, `r00-pilot-benchmarks.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **MIL-W2.** The integration promise (modular-mobile for tailings, in-plant integration for active operations, low licensee capital) has no engineering basis in the base: no footprint, throughput, or downtime data. Modular throughput is also limited (grinding modules ~15 tph; turnkey modular plants ~500-2,000 t/d), so whole-mine integration means many modules or fixed-plant work, not one mobile skid. Deliverability at whole-mine scale is Assumption (low confidence). [Cited: Business Plan Our Solution; `r00-adoption-deployment.md`] **Confidence: High that it is unevidenced.**
- **MIL-W3.** On-site operating headcount for a continuous recovery circuit is unquantified and a two-person company cannot crew operator-site operations; it would depend on the operator's workforce plus periodic LiT support, a control and quality risk that bears directly on the results renewals depend on. [Cited: `r00-adoption-deployment.md`; U-19] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

### Threats

The Month-25 first-license assumption is aggressive against multi-year operator qualification. [Cited: `r00-adoption-deployment.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **MIL-T2.** Customer-side adoption friction is high: incumbents are embedded in operator flowsheets and procurement, and only ~25% of Qualified Persons have evaluated tailings reprocessing, so LiT must overcome both incumbency and category novelty in the same sale. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1 and 3] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

## CAPITAL — financing, capital structure, valuation, exit

### Strengths
- **CAP-S1.** Investor appetite for domestic critical-minerals recovery is deep and proven: Phoenix Tailings (~$148M), Jetti (>$200M), Ceibo ($30M), Endolith ($13.5M) establish a fundable category and two live exit channels (a royalty re-rate if a portfolio is built; a strategic trade sale on the Honeywell/Johnson Matthey ~$2.4B catalyst-IP template). [Cited: `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: High that the category is fundable.**
- **CAP-S2.** The licensing-only model is deliberately asset-light, so if LiT genuinely funds no client capex, a small raise can in principle reach a validated pilot without the eight-figure plant capital its producer comparables carry. [Cited: `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: Moderate, contingent on the no-build model holding.**

### Weaknesses
- **CAP-W1.** The $2.0M seed is thin and single-tranche against every funded comparable and carries little reserve for IP-prosecution overruns, a failed first pilot, a slipped sales cycle, or any obligation to co-fund deployment capital; it collapses the moment any plant or demonstration capital becomes LiT's. [Cited: `r00-valuation-comps.md`; Business Plan Use of Funds] **Confidence: Medium-Low on adequacy (Assumption).**
- **CAP-W2.** The model embeds a timing inconsistency on the single largest revenue line: the pitch signs the major operator Q2 Year 3, while the plan and financial model place the major's $10M upfront and $14.288M partial-year royalty in Year 5. Both cannot be true; the gap is two years of compounding on the largest line. [Cited: Pitch Slide 10 vs Business Plan Year 5 and Financial Projections Sheet 1] **Confidence: High that the inconsistency exists.**
- **CAP-W3.** The headline 27x-34x return stacks three optimistic assumptions: 10% penetration of the small/mid universe, a major-operator win, and whole-mine NSR royalties holding. Each is individually aggressive; multiplied, the return is fragile (F-3: inputs are aggressive and compound). [Cited: Business Plan Exit; F-3] **Confidence: High that the assumptions stack.**
- **CAP-W4.** The IP budget ($235K for national-phase filings in the US, Australia, India, and Brazil plus ongoing PCT) is modest for contested multi-jurisdiction art, and the pilot budget ($700K) is single-campaign money against a multi-feedstock, two-stage, continuous-piloting plan. [Cited: Business Plan/Prospectus Use of Funds; `r00-pilot-benchmarks.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**
- **CAP-W5.** The R&D-delivery base is unevidenced: the model assumes 75% India / 25% VT R&D spend, yet the only executed agreement is the $52K VT contract, no IIT (ISM) Dhanbad agreement is in the base, and the engagement is described as "upcoming." [Cited: Financial Projections Key Assumptions vs Pitch Slide 11; `r00-ip-fto.md`] **Confidence: High.**

### Opportunities
- **CAP-O1.** The exit math is defensible if framed correctly: 12x-15x EBITDA sits at-to-just-above the realistic process-licensing/specialty-process central tendency (~9x-13x), and a royalty re-rate toward the 15x-25x EV/EBITDA public range becomes available only after a diversified producing portfolio exists. Carrying two exit framings strengthens, rather than weakens, the equity story. [Cited: `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: Moderate.**

### Threats
- **CAP-T1.** The 8x-10x revenue exit is margin-contingent and overstated if presented alone: it is internally consistent with 12x-15x EBITDA only at ~65-80% EBITDA margins, which a near-pure-royalty stream can reach but a staffed R&D-and-BD company at first scale may not. A single-asset, customer-concentrated early stream does not earn the royalty-company multiple. [Cited: `r00-valuation-comps.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

## WATERSHED — regulatory, environmental, permitting, ESG, policy

### Strengths
- **WAT-S1.** The ESG and remediation narrative is a real differentiator in a tightening-governance environment: lower-energy, acid-drainage-eliminating, recover-what-exists processing aligns with where regulation is heading (Global Tailings Management Institute formed Jan 2025, mandating third-party audits). [Cited: Prospectus Environmental Commitment; Market Research Sec. 2 and 4] **Confidence: Moderate-High on direction; specific environmental magnitudes are Unsupported.**

### Opportunities
- **WAT-O1.** Federal policy in 2026 directly endorses LiT's core thesis: the Department of the Interior, citing USGS, launched a formal effort to recover critical minerals from mine waste and tailings, with USGS funding state characterization (~$2M, 13 states, 8 mine-waste grants) and a public data layer LiT can use for site targeting. This validates the category at the level of federal policy. [Cited: `r00-policy-2026.md`] **Confidence: High that the program exists.**
- **WAT-O2.** A buyer-of-last-resort apparatus is standing up (OBBBA $7.5B for critical minerals including ~$2B stockpiles; a proposed $2.5B Strategic Resilience Reserve designed to pay above market; the MP Materials template with a $110/kg NdPr floor), giving the recovered units a partial demand and price floor that the financial model can use as a downside benchmark. [Cited: `r00-policy-2026.md`] **Confidence: High on the instruments.**
- **WAT-O3.** DPA Title III and the March 2026 Defense Industrial Base Consortium critical-minerals RFPP are a concrete, repeatable non-dilutive capital channel LiT or its operator partners could apply into for pilot or scale capital. [Cited: `r00-policy-2026.md`] **Confidence: Medium-High; LiT's own eligibility is Assumption pending U-11.**

### Weaknesses
- **WAT-W1.** Specific environmental claims (no acidic byproducts, abandoned-site remediation economics) are technical assertions not yet evidenced in the base; the ESG story currently outruns its proof. [Cited: Prospectus; Unsupported as to magnitude] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

### Threats
- **WAT-T1.** The abandoned-tailings pitch carries a CERCLA exposure the documents underweight: an entity that disturbs a contaminated site can be pulled in as a potentially responsible party for the whole site even when there to clean it up, and the US retains title to unpatented mining claims so recovered-product ownership at orphaned sites is not automatic. [Cited: `r00-tailings-liability.md`] **Confidence: High on the framework.**
- **WAT-T2.** The 2024 Good Samaritan Act offers only a narrow, capped pathway (a 15-permit, seven-year pilot) and a profit-seeking reprocessor may not cleanly qualify, since the eligibility test centers on a non-owner, non-liable party. "LiT operates at abandoned sites under Good Samaritan cover" is legally untested for a commercial reprocessor. [Cited: `r00-tailings-liability.md`; U-15] **Confidence: Moderate (Assumption, low-to-moderate).**
- **WAT-T3.** The benefits of the policy tailwind flow overwhelmingly to scale producers and processors, not to a pre-commercial process licensor; LiT's access runs through its operator customers, and the trade-protection leg is contingent (the January 2026 Section 232 action imposed no tariffs and opened a 180-day negotiation, status report ~13 July 2026). Treating the policy stack as a present subsidy to LiT overstates it. [Cited: `r00-policy-2026.md`; U-11] **Confidence: High.**

## CHOKEPOINT — geopolitics and supply-chain concentration (active R01-R04)

### Opportunities
- **CHO-O1.** The strategic case for domestic recovery is strong and externally grounded: over 90% of critical-mineral production sits outside the US, China refines 19 of 20 strategic minerals at roughly 70% average share and about 91% of REE refining, and the US is fully import-dependent for 12 critical minerals and over 50% reliant on 29 others. LiT's "domestic, allied, recover-what-exists" positioning sits squarely on a recognized national vulnerability. [Cited: Business Plan; Market Research Sec. 1; `r00-policy-2026.md`] **Confidence: High.**
- **CHO-O2.** China weaponized REE exports in 2025 (a second wave suspended to November 2026), which sharpens allied urgency and the willingness of governments to underwrite domestic and allied supply, creating a policy and offtake tailwind for technologies that unlock domestic units. [Cited: Business Plan; Market Research Sec. 1] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**
- **CHO-O3.** India's status as a favored destination (Strategic Trade Authorization Tier 1, Major Defense Partner, active US-India strategic trade dialogue) means LiT's allied-R&D footprint is geopolitically low-friction relative to alternatives, and IIT (ISM) Dhanbad is a credible, government-backed critical-minerals hub. [Cited: `r00-export-control.md`, `r00-ip-fto.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High that the destination is favorable; the specific LiT-IIT arrangement is Assumption pending U-17.**

### Threats
- **CHO-T1.** China's pricing power over the basket is a direct threat to a price-indexed royalty: the same dominance that makes domestic recovery strategically valuable lets China move prices (the 2025 export actions and the H1-2026 REE round-trip), and a deliberate price suppression would compress LiT's NSR-based royalty regardless of LiT's technical success. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1; `r00-price-update.md`; F-3a] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**
- **CHO-T2.** Developing US-origin critical-minerals process IP through a foreign institution is a supply-chain-control and IP-control vector: the more proprietary and restricted the IIT (ISM) work (good for the moat), the more squarely it sits under US export law (deemed-export, recordkeeping, VT Section 17 flow-down obligations). This coherence tension between the offshore-R&D cost plan and the IP-defensibility story is unmanaged in the base. [Cited: `r00-export-control.md`; VT Agreement Sec. 17] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**
- **CHO-T3.** Government demand-pull is concentrated on scale producers and allied national champions (the MP Materials template), so in a contest for federal capital and offtake a thin process licensor is structurally disadvantaged against producers the government can more directly underwrite. [Cited: `r00-policy-2026.md`] **Confidence: Moderate-High.**

## DISSENT — parallel weakness-and-threat slate (red team)

DISSENT files a parallel slate concentrated on weaknesses and threats, including the two named load-bearing items carried from the evidence lock with kill-switch standing reserved.

### Load-bearing weaknesses (kill-switch standing reserved)
- **DIS-W1 (load-bearing, U-1 + F-1).** The IP foundation may not be LiT's to license. The physical separation core overlaps HHS, which VTIP owns and has licensed to Minerals Refining Company for minerals; the chemical core overlaps a VTIP-owned, 2020-priority, already-published application (US20240368734A1, Yoon et al.); no LiT-assigned patent or PCT could be located; and under the only contract present LiT holds at most an internal-research license unless it separately elects and negotiates a commercial license. F-1 establishes there is no clean exclusive IP. If the LiT-VTIP license does not grant sublicensable rights in LiT's minerals field, the whole-mine-royalty licensing model has no asset to license. This is do-not-proceed-relevant. [Cited: F-1; `r00-ip-fto.md`; VT Agreement Art. 13; U-1, U-2, U-3 unretrieved] **Confidence: High that the rights are unconfirmed; kill-switch standing reserved.**
- **DIS-W2 (load-bearing, F-2).** The single most important performance number, the recovery uplift, is undefined and unvalidated, and the entire revenue model and value-exchange pitch rest on it. The literature confirms headroom exists in direction but pins no number for any LiT feedstock. A revenue model whose spine is an unvalidated single-point assumption is fragile by construction; if validation fails or lands materially below the assumed uplift, the economics do not hold. [Cited: F-2; `r00-tailings-grades.md`] **Confidence: High that it is unvalidated; kill-switch standing reserved.**

### Additional weaknesses
- **DIS-W3.** The validation claim outruns the evidence. "Bench-validated across lithium, REE, and tailings" is unsupported by any document in the base; the one executed study is lithium-only, conceptual, and disclaims guaranteed yield. Any deliverable relying on multi-feedstock validation must flag it NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A, Art. 10] **Confidence: High.**
