## Part 1 — Binding sponsor stipulations ingested as established facts

Source: `background/LiT_Sponsor_Stipulations.md` (Brian Keenum, CEO, 29 June 2026). These settle facts. Every seat still weighs their implications and may dissent on severity; no seat may reopen the underlying facts.

- **F-1 (IP).** Lithium Technologies has **no clean, exclusive IP available to license.** The company's position is a **pending PCT application** with an examiner's written opinion, not a granted patent and not an enforceable exclusive right in any jurisdiction. The IP-moat weakness is a **confirmed fact**, not a contingent or data-room-dependent finding. Every hedge that treated this as "unresolved" or "data-room-dependent" is removed as of this lock.
- **F-2 (recovery uplift).** The headline recovery improvement that drives the entire revenue model is currently **undefined and unvalidated.** Initial research and an initial proof exist; lab- and pilot-scale validation is the **explicit purpose of the seed raise.** Treat the uplift as an **unvalidated assumption**, never an established performance number. Validation is a use of funds, not a present capability. Its single-point dependence is a **load-bearing weakness.**
- **F-3 (revenue inputs).** Every quantitative input to the revenue model is **aggressive, and the errors compound.**
- **F-3a (NSR correction, binding).** A net smelter return royalty is by definition a percentage of net realized metal value, so it is **fully indexed to commodity prices.** Any prior or future finding that the NSR "ignores price" or "does not account for commodity pricing" is **incorrect and corrected here.** The correct framing carried forward: the royalty base is **fully and directly exposed to commodity-price volatility**, and that exposure **compounds** with the other aggressive inputs rather than standing apart from them.

Handling rule: F-1, F-2, F-3, F-3a are facts in every subsequent round. They flow into Round 01 Weaknesses/Threats, are carried explicitly into Round 02 convergence, and appear in `synthesis/` as plain facts about the company's position, never as a decree or an attributed quote.

Interaction note for Round 01: F-1 resolves the *moat* question (there is no exclusive moat) but does **not** resolve whether LiT even holds the **sublicensable rights** it would need to grant a whole-mine royalty. That second question remains open under item U-1 below. The two must not be conflated: "no exclusive IP" is now Cited; "LiT can sublicense the rights it needs" remains Assumption pending retrieval.
