## CRUCIBLE — process science and technology

### Reading notes

- The platform is two staged processes: (1) a physical separation step claimed effective in fine and ultrafine particle ranges, and (2) a sub-500C "low-temperature" chemical leach, bench-proven for lithium only. [Cited: Business Plan Technology Overview; Pitch Slide 3; Prospectus Our Solution]
- Headline performance claims: 16 to 19 percent recovery improvement, roughly 30 percent energy reduction, roughly 20 percent faster, no acidic byproducts, operation below 500C versus 1,000C-plus conventional. [Cited: Pitch Slide 5; Business Plan] The recovery figure is stated as "improvement over conventional methods" but the documents never state the baseline, the feedstock-specific result, or whether 16 to 19 is percentage points of recovery or a relative gain. The "1 cent for 16 to 19 cents" framing in the business model conflates a recovery metric with a value metric. [Cross-tension: Business Plan Fee Structure vs Technology Overview] Treat the precise magnitude as Assumption (low confidence) until the underlying test data is seen.
- The single executed research contract in the evidence base is narrow. The VT Sponsored Research Agreement is a fixed-price $52,075 engagement running March 11 to June 7, 2024, supervised by Dr. Roe-Hoan Yoon, scoped to: a literature/patent review of lithium-from-spodumene routes, "exploratory testing to evaluate the potential to recover lithium concentrates from mine and aggregate tailings," and one or more conceptual flowsheets with a preliminary material balance. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A, Tasks 1 to 3] This is a conceptual and preconcentration study, lithium-focused. It does not, on its face, constitute integrated two-stage bench validation across lithium spodumene, REE hard rock, and tailings.
- That creates a direct tension with the repeated claim of "bench-scale validation across lithium spodumene, rare earth hard rock ores, and tailings feedstocks." [Cited claims: Business Plan Executive Summary, Pitch Slide 11, Prospectus] The evidence base contains no test report, no REE separation data, and no chemical-extraction bench data. The validation claim is therefore Unsupported by any document in the base; it may exist outside the base.
- LiT's own named background IP is the chemical stage: "Extraction and Refinement of Lithium into Lithium Carbonate and Lithium Hydroxide Utilizing Flexible Feedstock and Non-Acidic Reduced-Temperature Leaching Methods." [Cited: VT Agreement Art. 13.9(a)] The physical separation surface-chemistry method is not named as LiT background IP in the only contract present, which matters because the separation step is described as the technical foundation of the value proposition. [Cross-tension: Prospectus Our Solution vs VT Agreement Art. 13]
- The "no lower particle size limit" fine/ultrafine claim is the genuine technical whitespace if real, because the market dossier confirms incumbents split this field (Jameson Cell, IsaMill, Concorde Cell for fine; HydroFloat is coarse, recovering particles above 0.5 mm). [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3] LiT's differentiation rests on a fine/ultrafine claim that the base does not yet quantify.
- Chemical-stage REE extraction does not exist yet; it is an R&D roadmap item, Months 16 to 36. [Cited: Business Plan Milestones] So feedstock-agnostic applies to the physical stage only; the chemical stage is lithium-only today.

- R-CRU-1: Confirm whether a LiT PCT application has published, with application/publication number and earliest priority date. The dossier notes a public-database search did not surface one and the company website sits at a "provisionalpatent" path. [Partly addressed: Market Research Sec. 5; needs confirmation]
- R-CRU-2: Benchmark realistic cost and timeline to take a two-stage mineral-processing flowsheet from bench to pilot across multiple feedstocks, to test the $700,000 pilot budget. [Gap]

## SPOT — market, demand, commodity macro (carrying geo-economics this round)

### Reading notes

- Demand narrative in LiT's documents is one-directional growth: 25 to 30 percent annual demand growth, lithium to 3.3M tonnes LCE by 2030, battery share rising above 95 percent, $600B-plus North American lithium reserves. [Cited: Business Plan Target Market; Pitch Slide 6] These are presented without the price cycle around them.
- The locked market dossier complicates that picture materially. Lithium prices fell more than 80 percent from late-2022 highs to about $8,259/t by mid-2025, then rebounded to roughly $26,278/t in early 2026; 2026 balance is contested (Morgan Stanley deficit ~80kt, UBS ~22kt, Fitch oversupply, Wood Mackenzie surplus 153kt). [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1] LiT's revenue model, a royalty on production value, sits directly on top of this volatility.
- The customer-pipeline channel is weaker than the demand story implies. Lithium feasibility studies collapsed to fewer than 10 in 2025 with 12-month-plus delays. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1] New-mine licensees, a stated segment, are a thinned pipeline near term.
- Demand mix is shifting toward storage: BESS demand grew 51 percent in 2025 to roughly a fifth of battery demand. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1] This is a positive structural pull but does not change LiT's feedstock exposure (hard rock and tailings).
- Tailings, the lead value proposition, is physically vast (about 16 Bt produced annually, about 282 Bt inventory) but commercially early: only about 25 percent of Qualified Persons have evaluated reprocessing. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1 and 4] The market is real; operator behavior is the constraint.
- Geo-economics (carried this round): over 90 percent of critical mineral production sits outside the US; China refines 19 of 20 strategic minerals at about 70 percent average share and roughly 91 percent of REE refining, and weaponized REE exports in 2025 (second wave suspended to November 2026). [Cited: Business Plan; Market Research Sec. 1] This is a tailwind for the "domestic, allied, recover-what-exists" positioning.

### Research requests
- R-SPOT-1: Current (mid-2026) lithium carbonate/hydroxide and key REE (NdPr, Dy) price levels and the latest 2026 supply/demand balance read, to update the dossier's early-2026 snapshot. [Partial gap]
- R-SPOT-2: Status of US and allied critical-minerals demand-pull policy in 2026 (price floors, offtake, stockpiling) beyond the MP Materials DoD deal already cited. [Partial gap; coordinate with WATERSHED]

## CLAIM — feedstock geology and addressable-feedstock realism

### Reading notes

- The addressable-feedstock claim rests on three validated feedstock families: lithium spodumene, REE hard rock, and tailings. [Cited: Business Plan Technology] As CRUCIBLE-adjacent geology, note the only contracted test work touched lithium concentrates from "mine and aggregate tailings ... in and around Virginia," nominally targeting greater than 6 percent Li2O concentrate grade. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A Task 2] No REE ore body or REE tailings work appears in the base.
- Tailings grade is the unstated variable. The value of recovering minerals from a tailings impoundment depends entirely on residual grade, mineralogy, and tonnage at a given site, none of which is characterized in the documents. The "decades of accumulated value" framing is directionally supported by the dossier's 282 Bt inventory figure but is not site-specific or grade-specific anywhere. [Cited macro: Market Research Sec. 1; site-level: Unsupported]
- Spodumene economics are grade-driven: pure spodumene is 8 percent Li2O, brines 0.02 to 0.07 percent, and industrial practice concentrates to greater than 6 percent Li2O before calcination. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A Sec. 1] LiT's fine-particle recovery pitch is most credible where conventional flotation leaves fine spodumene in tailings; the quantum of that loss per site is the missing number.
- The $229M per-operator and $2.29B major-operator "total mine production value" figures that drive royalties are assumptions with no per-site geological basis shown. [Cited as model inputs: Business Plan Financial Plan; basis: Unsupported]

### Research requests
- R-CLAIM-1: Typical residual critical-mineral grades and recoverable fractions in lithium and REE tailings impoundments, and what concentration is economically recoverable, to ground the tailings value proposition in numbers. [Gap]
- R-CLAIM-2: Representative scale of fine-particle/ultrafine losses in conventional spodumene and REE flowsheets (how much target mineral actually reports to tailings), to size LiT's recovery headroom. [Gap]

## ROYALTY — business model, licensing economics, deal terms

### Reading notes

- The core commercial mechanic is a 1 percent NSR royalty on the operator's total mine production value, plus an upfront fee ($2.0M small/mid inclusive of a $250K retainer; $10.0M major). [Cited: Business Plan Business Model and Fee Structure; Prospectus]
- The royalty base is the central commercial-realism question. LiT proposes 1 percent of total mine NSR, not 1 percent of the incremental production its technology enables. [Cited: Business Plan Fee Structure, "1% of the operator's total mine production value"] A process improvement that touches a fine-particle fraction or a tailings stream capturing 1 percent of an operator's entire mine output is a very large ask; operators benchmark technology licenses against the value the technology adds, not whole-mine output. The documents justify the NSR basis on verifiability grounds (audited, dispute-free) but do not address why an operator would grant whole-mine royalty for a partial-stream improvement. [Cross-tension: verifiability rationale vs willingness-to-pay; willingness: Unsupported]
- The "1 cent per dollar in exchange for 16 to 19 cents" equivalence assumes the 16 to 19 percent recovery improvement applies to the same base as the 1 percent royalty. If the recovery uplift applies only to a sub-stream while the royalty applies to total NSR, the value exchange the documents assert does not hold. [Cross-tension within Business Plan Fee Structure]
- Royalty revenue is commodity-price-exposed by construction: NSR is production value, which moves with the lithium and REE prices the dossier shows swinging more than threefold inside a year. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 1 and "and more" item 1]
- Model precedents exist for the licensing architecture: flotation licensing (Jameson's NovaCell licensed to Jord International) and catalyst licensing at existing mines (Jetti at Capstone Pinto Valley). [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3] But the best-funded tailings comparator, Phoenix Tailings ($117.8M), runs a build-own-operate producer model capturing full metal margin, a different value-capture architecture competing for the same narrative. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3 and "and more" item 3]
- IP-licensing dependency: if the separation method derives from VT IP, LiT's ability to sublicense to operators depends on the terms of a separate VT/VTIP license that, in the only contract present, must be elected within six months of disclosure and separately negotiated, with exclusivity being royalty-bearing. [Cited: VT Agreement Art. 13.4 to 13.6] A licensor that does not itself hold sublicensable rights cannot grant them.

### Research requests
- R-ROY-1: How process-technology and mineral-processing licenses are actually structured (upfront plus running royalty basis: throughput, recovered-metal, incremental value, or NSR), and whether whole-mine NSR royalties for a process improvement have precedent. [Gap, high priority]
- R-ROY-2: Terms and economics of comparable catalyst/flotation licensing deals (Jetti, NovaCell/Jord) to benchmark upfront-plus-royalty magnitudes. [Partial gap]

## MILL — operations, integration, customer-side feasibility, support delivery

### Reading notes

- The integration promise is "modular mobile" deployment for tailings and "integrate into existing plant infrastructure" for active operations, with low licensee capital. [Cited: Business Plan Our Solution; Pitch Slide 3] No engineering basis, footprint, throughput, or integration-downtime data appears in the base; treat deliverability as Assumption (low confidence).
- The sales motion the documents describe is correct for the sector and brutal on a two-person team: relationship-intensive, technically rigorous, requiring demonstrations on the operator's own feedstock, technical due diligence, and commercial negotiation per account. [Cited: Business Plan Marketing and Sales]
- Staffing is the operational pinch point. The plan runs on two principals through Year 2, adding a BD Manager mid-Year 2, and reaches only seven people by Year 5 while supporting 16 licensees, NSR verification across operators, and concurrent pilot programs. [Cited: Business Plan Personnel; Financial Projections Sheet 1] A Technical Integration Engineer does not arrive until Year 5. The capacity to deliver the recovery results that justify the royalty, on which renewals and reputation depend, is thinly resourced.
- Customer-side adoption friction is high and externally corroborated: incumbents are embedded in operator flowsheets and procurement, and only about 25 percent of Qualified Persons have evaluated tailings reprocessing. [Cited: Market Research Sec. 3 and 1] Integration into a live, high-throughput plant carries operational risk that operators weigh heavily.
- Technical support is partly outsourced to academic partners. [Cited: Business Plan Technical Support] That is credible for validation but not for 24/7 licensee operational support at commercial scale.

### Research requests
- R-MILL-1: Typical evaluation-to-signature timeline for a new processing technology at a major mining operator (pilot on operator feedstock through procurement), to test the Month-25 first-license assumption. [Gap]
- R-MILL-2: What "mobile/modular" mineral-processing deployment realistically involves in footprint, throughput, and on-site staffing, to test the tailings remediation deployment claim. [Gap]

## DISSENT — parallel red-team read

### Reading notes

- The most load-bearing claim in the entire base, "bench-validated across lithium, REE, and tailings," is not supported by any document in the base. The one executed research contract is a 3-month, $52,075, lithium-only conceptual-plus-preconcentration study that explicitly disclaims any guaranteed outcome or yield. [Cited: VT Agreement Attachment A; Art. 10 "will not guarantee any particular outcome or specific yield"] If the deeper validation exists, it is off-base and must be flagged NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL before any deliverable relies on it.
- The IP foundation may not be LiT's to license. The separation surface chemistry is the field of Yoon's VT/VTIP portfolio, the most relevant branch (HHS) is already licensed worldwide to Minerals Refining Company, and under the only contract present VT owns VT-developed IP, with LiT holding at most an internal-research license unless it separately elects and negotiates a commercial license within six months of disclosure. [Cited: VT Agreement Art. 13; Market Research Sec. 5] A licensing company whose physical-stage IP is owned by a university and partly pre-licensed to a third party has a freedom-to-operate and a have-rights-to-grant problem at the center of its model.
- The royalty design is commercially aggressive at its base: 1 percent of total mine NSR for a partial-stream process improvement, with the value-exchange math ("1 cent for 16 to 19 cents") only holding if recovery uplift and royalty share the same base, which is not shown. [Cited: Business Plan Fee Structure]
- The financials embed a timing inconsistency on the single largest revenue event (major operator Year 3 in the pitch versus Year 5 in the model and plan), and stack 10 percent penetration, a major-operator win, whole-mine royalties, and unbenchmarked exit multiples to reach 27x to 34x. [Cited: Pitch Slide 10 vs Business Plan/Financial Projections] Each assumption is individually optimistic; multiplied, the headline return is fragile.
- The team-to-ambition gap is structural: two people today, seven by Year 5, against a relationship-intensive enterprise sale to the world's largest miners, who are simultaneously funding competing technologies (BHP and Freeport backing Jetti/Ceibo) and running incumbent fine-particle equipment in the very accounts LiT must win. [Cited: Business Plan Personnel; Market Research Sec. 3 and 4]
- A do-not-proceed-as-framed option must remain live into option generation: the evidence supports a narrower, more defensible business (for example tailings-first, single-feedstock, IP-clean) more than it supports the full feedstock-agnostic, whole-mine-royalty, 16-licensee plan as written.

### Research requests
- R-DIS-1: Freedom-to-operate read on whether the worldwide HHS license to Minerals Refining Company and the VT/VTIP separation portfolio foreclose or constrain LiT's field of use. [Gap, highest priority; overlaps R-CRU-1]
- R-DIS-2: Whether any public record corroborates LiT validation, IP, or partnerships beyond the $52K VT study (PCT publication, IIT (ISM) agreement, any pilot or LOI). [Gap]
