## Investment Thesis

LiT is a process technology licensing company built on the insight that the world's existing mines and the tailings they have generated over decades contain enormous quantities of unrecovered critical minerals. LiT's platform recovers minerals from material that has already been mined without new permits, new land, or greenfield capital.

The commercial case is compelling. Our projections with modest engagement from existing operators provides $51.1M in annual revenue at a 92.7% operating margin, and a $57.2M annual royalty run rate at exit. A projected Year 5 exit of $400M–$510M represents a 27x–34x return on the $2M seed investment that we are seeking.

LiT was founded on more than a financial opportunity. The small particles that are discarded in current processing workflows and the tailings impoundments that surround mines worldwide are not just untapped assets, they are environmental liabilities: leaching into waterways, occupying land that could be returned to communities, and representing decades of accumulated harm that no one has yet found a commercially viable way to address. LiT's technology provides a solution. The same platform that recovers minerals can also help rehabilitate these sites.

> LiT's ambition is to be the global standard for low-energy, high-recovery critical minerals processing, and to leave every site that uses our technology in better condition.

## The Problem

The energy transition runs on critical minerals. Lithium, rare earth elements, cobalt, and nickel are foundational to electric vehicles, stationary storage, defense systems, and advanced electronics. Demand is growing at 25–30% annually. New mine development takes 15–25 years from discovery to production. The world cannot wait for new mines to solely address the demand.

The supply gap is not only a pipeline problem. It is a waste and harm problem. Existing mining operations worldwide discard a significant fraction of their target minerals into tailings impoundments during the physical separation phase because conventional physical separation processes cannot efficiently recover fine-particle material. Decades of operations have produced thousands of tailings deposits, globally.

The chemical extraction processes used to refine ore into battery-grade products compound the damage: operating above 1,000°C, consuming enormous amounts of energy, and producing acidic waste streams. The sector is simultaneously under-supplied and under-optimized.

---

## Our Solution

LiT's integrated two-stage platform attacks both dimensions of the problem under a single internationally patented system.

**Stage One — Physical Separation:** LiT's proprietary process applies surface chemistry techniques that selectively modify the properties of target mineral particles, enabling efficient separation and concentration across the full particle size range, including the fine and ultrafine fractions where conventional froth flotation and gravity separation consistently lose effectiveness. This mechanism is the technical foundation of LiT's value proposition, recovering material that conventional methods have already discarded. The process is feedstock-agnostic, with bench-scale validation across lithium spodumene, Rare Earth Elements (REE) hard rock ores, and tailings.

**Stage Two — Chemical Extraction:** A low-temperature leaching process operating well below 500°C, versus 1,000°C+ for conventional methods. Approximately 30% lower energy consumption, ~20% faster processing time, and with no highly acidic byproducts. Conventional acid leaching creates the acid rock drainage that has contaminated watersheds around mining operations for decades. LiT's process eliminates this threat. It is bench-proven for lithium, with an active R&D program targeting REE and other mineral streams.

## The Tailings Opportunity — Recovery and Remediation

Of all the applications LiT's platform addresses, tailings recovery is the most immediately compelling for operators and the most mission-aligned for LiT. Every major mining company in the world manages significant tailings impoundments. Many of those impoundments are abandoned former operations that no longer have active owners or funding for remediation.

## Environmental Commitment and Mission

LiT's founders believe that the critical minerals sector has an obligation to be better than it has been, and we believe that the technology to do so now exists. Every dimension of LiT's platform was designed with that conviction in mind.

**Eliminating acid drainage at the source.** Conventional chemical extraction produces acidic waste streams that, when mismanaged, creates one of the most persistent and damaging forms of mining-related environmental contamination. LiT's low-temperature extraction process produces no highly acidic byproducts.

**Reducing the energy cost of mineral production.** LiT's chemical extraction process requires approximately 30% less energy than conventional methods and can eliminate direct fossil fuel use in the extraction process. Every ton of mineral recovered using LiT's technology carries a materially lower carbon footprint than the same ton recovered conventionally.

**Rehabilitating abandoned tailings sites.** LiT's physical separation platform can be deployed in mobile format to rehabilitate abandoned tailings sites in locations where mining operations have ceased but the environmental liability remains. Minerals are recovered, tailings volumes are reduced, and land that has been occupied by waste for decades can begin returning to productive and beneficial use. This is among the highest-impact environmental applications of LiT's technology, and it is a commercial opportunity that no other available technology can address with the same economics.

**Training the next generation.** LiT's research partnerships with Virginia Tech and IIT (ISM) Dhanbad are not just about extending the platform. They are investments in the mineral processing engineers, doctoral researchers, and applied scientists who will implement, improve, and build upon sustainable processing methods for decades. We believe that the long-term improvement of this industry requires building the knowledge base at the same time that the technology evolves.

> We want our legacy to be one where we not only reduce the impact of mining and mineral processing, but also help repair the past damage that the industry has done to the areas where mines have operated around the world.

## Business Model

**Upfront License Fee:** $2,000,000 per small/mid-tier operator ($250K exclusivity retainer + $1.75M at signing). $10,000,000 for major operators.

**Ongoing Royalty — 1% of Total Production (NSR Basis):** LiT's royalty is 1% of the operator's total mine production value on a Net Smelter Return basis, which is the same standard operators already use for government royalties, streaming agreements, and financial reporting. This structure was deliberately chosen for verifiability: the royalty calculation is transparent, independently auditable, and free of the measurement disputes that make improvement-based royalties difficult to administer.

> At 1% of NSR, LiT costs the operator one cent on every dollar of production in exchange for 16 to 19 additional cents per dollar of recovery, along with a reduction in tailings volume, and the elimination of acidic waste.
