# CR-11 — Independent literature check: sub-500°C, acid-free lithium-from-spodumene routes

**Priority:** P3 (confirm-only). **Source request:** R-CRU-3.
**Disposition executed:** one confirming web pass over 2024–2026 literature, against the dossier anchor in `background/LiT_Market_Research_2026.md` Sec. 5.
**Date run:** 2026-06-29.

## Question

Are sub-500°C, acid-free lithium-from-spodumene routes with the claimed recovery reproducible in the open literature, and how does LiT's claim compare?

## Finding (one line)

**Confirmed.** The class of route LiT claims — sub-500°C alkaline (NaOH) roast plus room-temperature water leach, no sulfuric acid, ~99% Li — is independently published, peer-reviewed, patented by a third party, and reproduced/optimized in multiple 2024–2026 papers. The chemistry is technically plausible and not novel to LiT. This corroborates plausibility; it does not establish that the route is LiT's proprietary or defensible IP. **See [[r00-ip-fto.md]].**

## Evidence

- **Anchor (already in dossier, Sec. 5), Cited.** Two-stage NaOH roasting at 325°C followed by room-temperature water leaching, published with over 99% Li extraction, eliminating 1100°C calcination and sulfuric-acid baking (ScienceDirect, *Chemical Engineering Journal*, Subasinghe & Rezaee, 2025).

- **Mechanism, Cited.** The roast promotes the alkali–silica reaction near the melting point of NaOH (~318°C), producing a water-soluble LiNaSiO4 phase; the water-leach reaction is exothermic with fast kinetics, reaching maximum Li recovery within about one minute at room temperature (ScienceDirect S1385894725004607; SSRN 5030351).

- **Patent status of the comparable route, Cited.** The Penn State α-spodumene NaOH-roast / water-leach / CO2-precipitation process was recently granted patent rights (AZoMining; ScienceDaily 2025-04-30; TechXplore 2025-04-30). This is a third party holding granted IP on the same low-temperature, acid-free mechanism — directly relevant to the IP-crowding concern, not the LiT position.

- **Adjacent confirmed routes (range of the field), Cited.**
  - NaOH autoclave digestion at 250°C: 95–96% Li in ~2 hours.
  - Microwave-assisted alkaline roasting at ~250°C producing water/acid-soluble salts.
  - NaOH + NaAlO2 autoclave: Li extraction raised from 50.5% to 94.7%.
  - Synergistic α-spodumene + lepidolite roasting lowering the phase-transition temperature by ~150°C (ScienceDirect; MDPI Minerals 15(6):574, 2025 review "Alternatives to Sulfuric Acid Digestion").

- **Independent techno-economic confirmation, Cited.** A 2026 holistic TEA–LCA of lithium-carbonate production from spodumene compares conventional, direct, and electrochemical routes, treating low-temperature DLE-from-spodumene as a live, analyzable production pathway (RSC *Green Chemistry*, D5GC04866D, 2026).

## Comparison to LiT's claim

LiT's "below 500°C, no acidic byproducts" chemical stage is consistent with — and at the temperature/recovery level effectively matched by — the open literature. The claim is **technically credible (high confidence)**. The corollary, which the IP and convergence rounds must carry, is that the route is **densely published and partly patented by others**, so technical reproducibility is a point of competitive parity, not differentiation. Any defensibility must come from the specific filed claims, the multi-feedstock two-stage integration, or the fine-particle (Stage One) surface-chemistry coupling, none of which this literature check validates as LiT-owned.
