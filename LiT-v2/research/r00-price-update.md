# CR-4 — Mid-2026 lithium and REE prices and the 2026 supply/demand balance

**Priority:** P2. **Status:** Commissioned; executed in full from public sources.
**Date retrieved:** 2026-06-29. **Moderator-run web research (WebSearch).**

This item updates the dossier's early-2026 price snapshot to a mid-2026 read, so the Threats quadrant and the revenue sensitivities are calibrated to the prices that exist when this deliberation is run, not to a January anchor. LiT's royalty stream is commodity-price-exposed by construction: the value of any whole-mine or incremental-value royalty rises and falls with the lithium and rare-earth basket, so the volatility profile below is itself a finding, not just context.

---

## Bottom line up front

**Both of LiT's target baskets have just been through a violent round-trip, and that volatility is the load-bearing fact, not the spot level.** In the first half of 2026 lithium ran to two-year highs and then moderated; NdPr rallied ~160% off its January low, peaked at end-April, and has since given back about a third. The spot prices LiT would book against today are well off their 2026 peaks but well above the 2023-2025 trough. **For the deliverables this means the revenue model must be run across a wide price band, and the single most defensible Threat is price volatility itself: a royalty booked at mid-2026 highs could be worth 30-50% less within a quarter, as the April-to-June NdPr move just demonstrated in real time.** **Tag: Cited (spot levels and the round-trip). Confidence: High on the moves, Medium on where prices sit by the time LiT books a first royalty (Month ~25, i.e. ~2028), which no source can fix.**

The second finding is structural and cuts the other way: **the supply/demand balance is tightening across both baskets.** Lithium's 2026 surplus is narrowing toward balance with credible analysts already calling a deficit, and NdPr is in a second consecutive year of deficit. A tightening multi-year balance supports the *direction* of LiT's price exposure even as the *near-term* spot is whippy. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High.**

## (a) Lithium — mid-2026 spot and the round-trip

- **Q1 2026 surge then moderation.** Lithium prices surged to **two-year highs** in Q1 2026, with battery-grade lithium carbonate cited near **US$26,000/t** at the peak, before moderating into mid-year. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Carbonate (China, late June 2026):** lithium carbonate traded around **CNY 163,000–169,000/t**, having touched a near two-month low of ~CNY 163,000 in June before seasonal inventory rebuilding lifted bids back toward CNY 169,000. At roughly **US$11,000/t LCE**, the market sits just above the lowest-cost Chilean brine and below the cash cost of the most expensive Chinese lepidolite — i.e. near the marginal-cost floor, not at a euphoric level. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Hydroxide:** remained elevated through May 2026, consolidating the sharp Q1 recovery rather than extending it. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium.**
- **Spodumene SC6:** ~**US$800/t** as of mid-May 2026, down materially from earlier in the year. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Supply response is already switching on.** Higher prices have pulled idled supply back: **Mineral Resources is restarting Bald Hill** (after an ~18-month suspension) and **Core Lithium restarted Finniss**. This is the classic swing-supply cap on any lithium rally and a direct reason to model the royalty against a *capped* upside. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**

## (b) Rare earths — NdPr and dysprosium

- **NdPr oxide:** ~**US$90,300/t (≈US$90.3/kg)** as of 1 June 2026 (SMM benchmark). This is **down ~35% from the end-April peak** of roughly **US$137–140/kg** (NdPr metal), which itself capped a **~160% rally from ~US$53/kg at the January 2026 open**. The basket is therefore well off its 2026 high but still far above where the year began. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Dysprosium (heavy REE):** ~**US$930/kg**, up **~105% since the start of 2026** — the heavy-REE move has been even sharper than NdPr, reflecting magnet-grade heat-resistance demand (EV traction motors, wind, defense). **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Direction:** the **NdPr market is in supply deficit for a second consecutive year in 2026**, a structural underpin beneath the spot volatility. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High.**

## (c) The 2026 supply/demand balance

- **Lithium — surplus narrowing toward balance, deficit calls emerging.** S&P Global sees a **reduced 2026 surplus of ~109,000 t LCE** (down from ~141,000 t in 2025). Other houses already flip to deficit: **Morgan Stanley ~80,000 t LCE deficit, UBS ~22,000 t deficit**. Consumption is forecast **+13.5% YoY to ~1.48M t LCE**; supply **+9.9% to ~1.58M t LCE**. Beneath the nominal balance, a **structural spodumene deficit** (converter overcapacity pulling on upstream rock) is tightening the part of the chain LiT's spodumene-route economics touch. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High** (analyst spread is itself the signal: the market is near the surplus/deficit hinge).
- **Demand swing factor:** **energy-storage (ESS) demand grew ~51% in 2025** and is now capable of tightening the global balance independently of EVs — a demand leg the dossier should not treat as EV-only. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium.**
- **REE:** NdPr in **second-year deficit** (above); no clean 2026 tonnage-balance figure for the full REE basket was retrievable in this pass. **Tag: Partial.** See NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL below.

## What this means for the deliverables (for Round 01 onward)

- **Threats (primary).** **Commodity-price volatility is a first-order Threat, evidenced in real time:** NdPr fell ~35% from peak in roughly two months and lithium round-tripped two-year highs inside H1 2026. A royalty stream booked at any single price point is fragile; the financial model must carry a wide price band and the headline return must be shown at a trough basket, not a mid-2026 spot. **Lithium upside is structurally capped by fast swing-supply restarts** (Bald Hill, Finniss), so the lithium leg of the royalty should not be modeled with open-ended upside.
- **Opportunities.** The **multi-year balance is tightening** in both baskets (lithium surplus narrowing toward deficit; NdPr second-year deficit; ESS demand inflecting). This supports the *direction* of LiT's exposure and the strategic logic of recovering domestic units, even though near-term spot is whippy.
- **Strategy / financial model.** Run royalty revenue across an explicit price band (trough / mid-2026 / peak), not a point estimate. Prefer an **incremental-value or recovered-metal royalty basis** (per CR-2) precisely because it tracks the volatile basket rather than a fixed fee. Flag that LiT's first booked royalty lands ~2028 on current timelines, so today's spot is an input to the *sensitivity*, not a forecast of the *realization*.
- **Confidence discipline.** Spot levels and the H1-2026 round-trip are **Cited/High**. The price LiT actually realizes at first royalty is **Assumption/Medium** at best and must never be presented as Cited.
