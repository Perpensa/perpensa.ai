# CR-5 — 2026 US and allied critical-minerals demand-pull policy

**Priority:** P2. **Status:** Commissioned; executed in full from public sources.
**Date retrieved:** 2026-06-29. **Moderator-run web research (WebSearch).**
Merges R-SPOT-2 and R-WAT-1. Carries the geo/regulatory coverage CHOKEPOINT is not yet seated to provide (CHOKEPOINT files no Round 00 note).

This item maps the US and allied demand-pull instruments in force or in motion in 2026 beyond the MP Materials DoD deal: price floors, government offtake, stockpiling, DPA Title III, the critical-minerals list, Interior's mine-waste/tailings valorization program, and tariffs or domestic-content rules. It is the principal Opportunity tailwind for LiT and the backbone of the "domestic, allied, recover-what-exists" positioning.

---

## Bottom line up front

**The US has, in 2026, become an active "market maker" for critical minerals through equity stakes, offtake, stockpiles and DPA Title III money, and it has explicitly opened a federal program to recover critical minerals from mine waste and tailings.** That second fact is the most direct policy tailwind LiT has: the Department of the Interior, citing USGS research, has launched an effort to recover critical minerals from mine waste, coal refuse, tailings and abandoned mines, with USGS funding state characterization of those wastes. **This validates LiT's core thesis (value sits in existing tailings; recovering domestic units is a national priority) at the level of federal policy, not just market logic. Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**

**The two important qualifiers, both load-bearing for the Threats and Strategy work:** (1) the demand-pull tools are flowing overwhelmingly to **producers and processors at scale** (equity stakes, large offtakes, magnet plants), not to early-stage process-IP licensors, so LiT's path to capturing this money runs **through** its mining-operator customers, not directly; and (2) the administration is **stepping back from broad producer price floors** even as it keeps them in bespoke deals (MP Materials), and the January 2026 Section 232 action **did not impose tariffs** but opened a 180-day negotiation, so the trade-protection tailwind is **contingent, not in force**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High.**

## (a) The federal mine-waste / tailings valorization program — most directly relevant to LiT

- **Interior has launched a formal effort to unlock critical minerals from mine waste, coal refuse, tailings and abandoned uranium mines**, aligned to the Executive Order on increasing American mineral production. A Secretary's Order directs **USGS to map and inventory publicly owned mine-waste resources** as the first step to recovery. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **USGS is funding states** to identify critical-mineral potential in mine waste: ~**US$2M** provided, with **13 states** in joint work and **8 states** receiving mine-waste characterization grants in 2024 (California, Illinois, Michigan, Missouri, Nevada, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Utah) under the **Earth MRI** partnership. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Worked examples cited by Interior:** zinc and germanium from Tar Creek (Oklahoma) lead/zinc waste; tellurium from Bingham Canyon (Utah) copper tailings. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Read for LiT:** the federal government is paying to **characterize and inventory** exactly the resource LiT proposes to monetize. This de-risks the "is there value in the tailings" question at the macro level and creates a public data layer LiT can use for site targeting. It is a genuine Opportunity. **Caveat:** the program funds *characterization and public-resource mapping*, not private process-licensing revenue, and the named examples are zinc/germanium/tellurium, not LiT's lithium/REE focus, so the program legitimizes the category more than it funds LiT's specific basket. **Tag: Cited (program); Assumption/Medium (direct benefit to LiT's lithium-REE niche).**

## (b) Stockpiling and the government as buyer

- **One Big Beautiful Bill Act** appropriated **US$7.5B** to the Pentagon for critical minerals: **~US$2B for stockpiles, ~US$5B for supply-chain investment, ~US$500M for loans/guarantees/technical assistance.** **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **A bipartisan US$2.5B "Strategic Resilience Reserve"** was introduced in the Senate and House in January 2026 to buy and store key metals; notably designed to **pay above prevailing market price when needed**, attract allied co-funding, and recycle proceeds. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High** (introduced, not yet enacted as of this pass).
- **Read for LiT:** a government willing to **buy and to pay above market** puts a partial demand floor under the *units* LiT helps recover, which indirectly supports the royalty basket. But the buyer is buying **refined/stockpilable material**, reached through LiT's operator customers, not LiT's IP. **Tag: Cited (instruments); Assumption (transmission to LiT revenue).**

## (c) Offtake, equity stakes and price floors (the MP Materials template)

- **The DoD / MP Materials deal** set the template: a **US$400M Pentagon equity investment**, a **US$150M loan**, **offtake with GM and Apple**, a **guaranteed price floor of US$110/kg for NdPr oxide** (with DoD entitled to 30% of upside when the second magnet facility runs), and ~US$1B private financing (JP Morgan, Goldman). Built **in part on DPA Title III authorities**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Broader policy shift:** the administration has moved to **state-capital tools** (equity stakes in private companies, long-term offtake to underwrite demand, selective price floors) and is **expanding critical-minerals financing and bilateral partnerships**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **But:** the administration is **stepping back from broad price floors** for domestically produced critical minerals generally, no longer actively pursuing producer-favoring price-floor mechanisms outside bespoke deals. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High.**
- **Read for LiT:** the bespoke price floor (US$110/kg NdPr) is a useful **benchmark for the floor a defense-relevant REE unit can command**, directly usable in the financial model's downside case. But these are **deal-by-deal, scale-producer** instruments. LiT's realistic access is to position its operator-licensees as candidates for such deals, not to expect a floor on its own royalty. **Tag: Cited (the US$110/kg benchmark); Assumption (LiT access).**

## (d) DPA Title III

- **DPA Title III authorities** (managed by the office handling industrial-base policy) enable **direct federal investment to expand domestic capacity** where supply chains are fragile or foreign-dependent; they underpinned the MP deal and a March 2026 Defense Industrial Base Consortium **request for critical-minerals project proposals.** **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Read for LiT:** Title III + the DIB Consortium RFPP is a **concrete, repeatable funding channel** LiT (or its operator partners) could apply into for pilot/scale capital. This is an actionable Execution-phase item, not just background. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium-High** (eligibility for a pre-commercial process licensor is an Assumption to test).

## (e) Tariffs, Section 232 and domestic-content rules

- **14 January 2026 Section 232 Presidential Proclamation** on imports of **processed critical minerals and derivative products (PCMDPs)** **did NOT impose tariffs**; it directed **negotiations**, with a status report due **~13 July 2026** (180 days). **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- The proclamation **contemplates "minimum import prices" for specific critical minerals** and reserves the right to impose **import restrictions** if negotiations stall. Articles at risk in later actions: **processed lithium, cobalt, nickel, manganese, graphite; rare-earth oxides/salts; rare-earth magnets; specialty alloys.** **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- Backdrop: the US is **fully import-dependent for 12 critical minerals** and **>50% reliant on 29 others.** **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Read for LiT:** trade protection that would lift the price of imported processed material (and thus the value of domestically recovered units) is **pending and contingent, not in force.** Model it as upside optionality, not a current tailwind. The minimum-import-price idea, if enacted, would be a stronger and more durable support for the domestic-recovery thesis than spot prices. **Tag: Cited (the action); Assumption (whether/when tariffs or minimum prices land).**

## (f) The critical-minerals list

- The policy stack rests on the federal **critical-minerals designation**; lithium and the rare earths central to LiT's thesis sit on it, which is what makes them eligible for the stockpile, offtake, Title III and Section 232 instruments above. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High** (lithium and REE inclusion); the precise 2026 list revision was not separately isolated in this pass — see NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL.

## What this means for the deliverables (for Round 01 onward)

- **Opportunities (primary, strong).** Federal policy in 2026 **directly endorses recovering critical minerals from tailings/mine waste** (Interior + USGS), and stands up a **buyer-of-last-resort apparatus** (stockpiles, above-market reserve, offtake, Title III). This is the strongest external validation of LiT's "domestic, allied, recover-what-exists" positioning and should anchor the Opportunities quadrant. The **DPA Title III / DIB Consortium RFPP** is an actionable non-dilutive capital channel for Execution.
- **Opportunities (qualified).** The money flows to **scale producers and processors**, reached **through** LiT's operator customers. LiT's strategy should be to make its licensees the natural recipients of offtake/floor deals, positioning LiT's IP as the enabler of a federally favored domestic unit. Do **not** assume direct subsidy to a pre-commercial process licensor without testing eligibility.
- **Threats / fragility.** The **trade-protection leg is contingent**: the January 2026 Section 232 action imposed **no tariffs** and the administration is **retreating from broad price floors**. Any deliverable that leans on tariffs or a general price floor as a present fact is overstated. Treat them as optionality with a 2H-2026 catalyst (the ~13 July negotiation report).
- **Confidence discipline.** Existence of the instruments is **Cited/High**. The **transmission of any of this to LiT's own royalty revenue** is **Assumption/Medium** and must be tagged as such, never Cited.
