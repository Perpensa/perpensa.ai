# CR-2 — How mineral-processing and process-technology licenses are actually structured

**Priority:** P1. **Status:** Commissioned; executed in full from public sources. Deal-specific rate terms (Jetti, NovaCell, Jameson) are confidential and flagged NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL where exact percentages were sought.
**Date retrieved:** 2026-06-29. **Moderator-run web research (WebSearch/WebFetch).**

This item tests the central commercial mechanic of LiT's model. Three questions were posed: (a) what basis do real process-technology and mineral-processing licenses use — upfront plus running royalty on throughput, on recovered metal, on incremental value, or net smelter return (NSR); (b) is there any precedent for a whole-mine NSR royalty granted for a partial-stream process improvement; (c) benchmark the economics of comparable catalyst/flotation licensing deals (Jetti at Capstone Pinto Valley; Jameson NovaCell licensed to Jord International).

---

## Bottom line up front

The public record shows a clear, consistent answer that runs against the dossier's framing. Real process-technology and mineral-processing licenses are paid on the **value the technology itself adds**, not on the whole mine. The standard structure is an **upfront licensing fee plus a running fee tied to throughput processed, metal recovered, or incremental value created**, frequently bundled with an ongoing service fee. The most directly comparable deal in the world — **Jetti Resources' catalyst licensing**, the same archetype as LiT (a bolt-on process improvement that lifts recovery at an operating mine) — is explicitly a **"value-sharing" model: licensing fees, royalties based on incremental copper recovered, and service fees.** Its royalty base is *incremental copper recovered*, the metal the technology unlocks, not the mine's total output.

**Net Smelter Return royalties, by contrast, are an instrument of mineral-rights ownership and project finance, not process licensing.** NSR royalties (typically 2–5% of net smelter revenue) are granted to the holder of the ground, to finders/explorers, to acquirers as deferred purchase price, or to JV partners converting an equity interest. **No precedent was located for a whole-mine NSR royalty granted in exchange for a partial-stream process improvement.** That is the load-bearing finding for Strategy: LiT's pitch of a whole-mine NSR for a sub-stream recovery gain has **no public precedent and is structurally mismatched to how this value is actually priced**. The defensible, precedented base is incremental-value or recovered-metal — the metal LiT's process adds — not the whole mine's NSR. Tag on the central conclusion: **Cited (multiple independent sources); High confidence.**

## (a) What basis do real process-technology / mineral-processing licenses use?

The public sources converge on a small set of bases, used in combination:

- **Upfront / signing fee plus running royalty.** The standard technology-license shape is a one-time upfront fee at signing plus an ongoing royalty. A representative recovery-technology license cited publicly ran 20 years with a **$500,000 upfront fee and a 5% royalty**. **Tag: Cited (illustrative, non-mining-specific). Confidence: Medium** as a numeric anchor; the *structure* (upfront + running %) is **High** confidence as the norm.
- **Throughput / per-ton basis ("Processing-as-a-Service").** Mineral-processing technology providers commonly monetize via **per-ton or throughput-based processing fees** — the licensor is paid on tonnage run through the technology, independent of metal price. Toll-processing and milling fees sit here (a flat "toll" per ton, or a percentage of metal processed, e.g. a milling fee struck as a fraction of the gold passed through the plant). **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Recovered-metal / incremental-recovery basis.** The licensor takes a share of the *metal the technology recovers*. This is the basis used by Jetti (incremental copper recovered) and is the natural fit for a recovery-enhancing process. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Incremental-value / value-sharing basis.** The licensor participates in the *economic uplift* the technology creates — increased production and enhanced profitability — rather than a fixed unit rate. This is how Jetti describes its own model. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Gross-revenue royalty on a defined stream.** Where a discrete stream is created (e.g. tailings reprocessing), a gross-revenue royalty on *that stream* is used — a cited tailings-reprocessing royalty ran **5% of all gross revenue derived from the tailings**. Note the base is the tailings revenue, not the host mine. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**

The throughline: **the royalty base is the technology's own footprint** — the tons it processes, the metal it recovers, or the value it adds — not the entire mine's production. Specialist technology-royalty benchmarking (e.g. IPRA's *Royalty Rates for Technology*) exists precisely to price licenses on this incremental basis. **Tag: Cited (source exists). Confidence: High.**

## (b) Precedent for a whole-mine NSR royalty granted for a partial-stream process improvement?

**Finding: none located. The instrument is mismatched to the use.**

NSR is the net revenue the **owner of a mining property** receives on sale of metal, less transport, smelting, and refining costs; an NSR royalty is a fraction of that paid to a royalty holder, **typically 2–5%**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.** The circumstances in which NSR royalties are actually granted are all **ownership- or finance-derived**, not technology-derived:

- as a **finder's fee** for an exploration project;
- as part of the **purchase price** to acquire a mining project (deferred consideration);
- under a **joint-venture** agreement, to convert a minority/diluted JV interest into a royalty;
- as **project financing**, sold by the operator to a royalty/streaming company for upfront capital.

**Tag: Cited (Clayton Utz; INN; Ecora; Wikipedia NSR). Confidence: High.**

In every documented case the NSR holder has a claim on the **ground or the project economics as a whole** — by ownership, discovery, acquisition, or capital provided. A **process licensor that improves one stream of an existing operation does not, on any located precedent, receive a royalty on the whole mine's net smelter return.** The comparable process-licensing deals (next section) are uniformly structured on the technology's own incremental output. The dossier's whole-mine-NSR-for-a-sub-stream-improvement construct therefore stands as **Unsupported by precedent**, and is the commercial mechanic most likely to be repriced by a sophisticated operator counterparty.

**Implication.** Round 03/04 should treat "whole-mine NSR" not as the base royalty mechanic but as an aggressive negotiating anchor with no precedent. The precedented, defensible bases are: (i) royalty on **incremental metal recovered** by LiT's process; (ii) royalty/fee on **throughput processed**; (iii) gross-revenue royalty on a **discrete recovered stream** (the tailings or fines stream LiT actually treats). These reshape the revenue model and should flow into the financial sensitivities.

## (c) Benchmark economics of comparable catalyst/flotation licensing deals

**Jetti Resources — closest analog (catalytic copper-leach improvement at operating mines).**
- Commercial model is explicitly **"value-sharing": licensing fees + royalties based on incremental copper recovered + service fees** for deployment, support, and optimization. Jetti "participates in the economic benefits generated by increased copper production and enhanced profitability for its partners." **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- Deployed at **Capstone's Pinto Valley** on the existing dump-leach; cathode production per area irrigated **doubled** in year one, with a plan to add **300–350 Mlb** of copper over ~two decades from residual/waste. Jetti has trials/relationships with Teck and is backed by BHP and Freeport (>$200M raised by 2024). This establishes the archetype — a bolt-on recovery process paid on the metal it unlocks — as **commercially real and well-capitalized**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Exact rate terms (the % of incremental copper, the upfront, the service fee) are not public.** **NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL: Jetti license economics** — obtainable only via industry contacts, a counterparty disclosure, or a paid benchmarking report (IPRA). The *structure*, however, is confirmed and is the single most useful precedent for LiT.

**NovaCell (Jord International) and Jameson Cell (Glencore Technology) — flotation technology licensing.**
- **NovaCell** (Prof. Graeme Jameson's coarse-and-fine flotation device) is commercialized by **Jord International under a global exclusive license**; testwork is offered through partners (Core Resources). **Jameson Cell** licensing/marketing is managed by **Glencore Technology** (formerly Xstrata Technology), with **500+ units across 30 countries**. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- These are predominantly **equipment-sale plus technology-license** models (sell/license the cell, plus IP and process support), a different monetization shape from a pure running royalty — useful as a contrast: flotation tech tends to be monetized through **hardware + license**, whereas catalytic/chemical recovery tech (Jetti, and LiT's chemistry stage) tends toward **value-share on recovered metal**. **Exact royalty/equipment economics are confidential. NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL: NovaCell/Jameson commercial terms.**

## What this means for the deliverables (for Round 01 onward)

- **Strengths.** A **proven, well-funded precedent exists** for LiT's exact archetype: Jetti shows that a bolt-on recovery technology at an operating mine can be commercialized on a licensing-plus-incremental-recovery-royalty basis and attract major-miner capital. LiT's model is not sui generis; it has a live comp. Frame this as validation of the *licensing channel*, with the royalty *base* corrected to incremental value.
- **Weaknesses / Threats.** The **whole-mine NSR mechanic is unprecedented and structurally mismatched**; presenting it as the base case overstates capturable value and invites repricing by operator counterparties. Royalty income is **commodity-price- and recovery-rate-exposed** under any recovered-metal or value-share basis, and **deal terms in this field are private**, so LiT cannot anchor its model to a published comparable rate without primary benchmarking.
- **Strategy / financial model.** Re-base the royalty on **incremental metal recovered, throughput processed, or a discrete-stream gross-revenue royalty** (~5% of the recovered stream is a located anchor; technology licenses commonly pair an upfront with a low-single-digit-to-5% running royalty). Carry "whole-mine NSR" only as an aspirational ceiling, clearly labeled unprecedented. The exit/return math built on whole-mine NSR should be stress-tested against the incremental-value base in Round 04.
- **Confidence discipline for Round 01.** The *structure* of process-tech licensing is **Cited/High**. Any *specific royalty percentage* attributed to a comparable deal is **Assumption** pending primary benchmarking, never Cited.
