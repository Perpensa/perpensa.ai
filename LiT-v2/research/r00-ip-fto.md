# CR-1 — IP ownership, freedom to operate, and public corroboration of validation

**Priority:** P1 (highest). **Status:** Commissioned parts executed; legal-opinion and license-terms parts flagged NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL.
**Date retrieved:** 2026-06-29. **Moderator-run web research (WebSearch/WebFetch).**

This item carries the single most load-bearing uncertainty in the base: if the process IP is not LiT's to sublicense, the whole-mine-royalty licensing model is at risk. Three questions were posed: (a) has a LiT PCT published, and what is its number and priority date; (b) does the worldwide HHS license to Minerals Refining Company and the broader Yoon/VT/VTIP separation portfolio constrain LiT's field of use; (c) does any public record corroborate LiT validation, an IIT (ISM) Dhanbad agreement, or any pilot/LOI beyond the $52,075 VT study.

---

## Bottom line up front

The physical-separation and low-temperature-leaching IP that LiT's platform rests on is, on the public record, **owned by Virginia Tech Intellectual Properties, Inc. (VTIP) and predates LiT's formation**, with the same inventors named in LiT's own VT sponsored-research agreement. No patent or application assigned to "Lithium Technologies" or any LiT entity could be located in the public databases, and the dossier's "internationally filed PCT" has no publicly retrievable publication number. No independent public record corroborates LiT's bench-scale validation claim, an IIT (ISM) Dhanbad agreement, or any pilot or LOI. This converts the dossier's existing "Technology/IP: High risk" rating from a caution into a concrete, sourced ownership exposure. **LiT's position appears to be that of a prospective sublicensee of VTIP-held IP, not an owner of granted IP** — pending the LiT-VTIP license terms and an FTO opinion, both of which require external retrieval.

## (a) Is there a published LiT PCT? — Finding: none located; the public IP in this field is VTIP's

**No patent or published application assigned to "Lithium Technologies," "Lithium Technologies Inc," or the lithiumtechnologies.co entity could be located.** Searches across Google Patents, Justia, and general web for LiT-assigned filings, a LiT WO/PCT publication number, and the company's claimed "internationally filed PCT" returned nothing attributable to LiT. **Tag: Unsupported (the existence of a LiT-owned, LiT-assigned PCT is asserted in the dossier but not publicly corroborated). Confidence the absence is real: Medium-High** — a PCT filed less than ~18 months ago would not yet have published, so absence is not proof the filing does not exist; but it does mean no public claim scope, priority date, or ownership can be verified.

What *is* publicly retrievable in exactly this technology space is assigned to **VTIP**, not LiT:

- **US20240368734A1 — "Methods for extracting rare earth elements from rare earth element sources."** Assignee: **Virginia Tech Intellectual Properties Inc.** Inventors: Roe-Hoan Yoon, Christopher Aaron Noble, Roberto McAlister Council-Troche, Wei Liu. Earliest priority: **December 4, 2020** (US provisional 63/121,435). National-phase application US18/255,665, published November 7, 2024, status pending. Claims a low-temperature route (**≤100 °C**) using base (NaOH/KOH) pretreatment followed by ion-exchange/chelant leaching (EDTA, ammonium sulfate) on clayey/coal-refuse and monazite REE sources. **Tag: Cited (Google Patents). Confidence: High.**
  - The US18/255,665 national-phase designation implies a PCT was filed in this family. That PCT/family is **VTIP's**, priority **2020**, and names the same VT inventors (Yoon, Noble) who appear on LiT's VT agreement. It is not LiT-assigned.

This is the crux: the low-temperature REE leaching chemistry that the dossier describes as a LiT "patentable core" maps onto a **VTIP-owned, pre-existing, already-published application with a 2020 priority date**, four years before the 2024 LiT-VT sponsored study. The chemical stage LiT markets is, on the public record, VTIP's invention.

A separate, **competing academic route exists and is not VT's or LiT's**: Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's "New low-temperature process for extracting lithium from alpha spodumene" (LBNL tech 2024-019), operating at 600–800 °C and replacing sulfuric acid/soda ash. **Tag: Cited (LBNL IPO). Confidence: High.** This confirms the dossier's point that the lithium-from-spodumene field is densely worked by multiple institutions, deepening prior-art risk on LiT's lithium claims.

## (b) Does the HHS / VTIP separation portfolio constrain LiT's field of use? — Finding: yes, materially; precise carve-out needs external retrieval

The fine/ultrafine particle physical-separation core LiT markets is the **Hydrophobic-Hydrophilic Separation (HHS) process**:

- HHS was developed in VT's **Center for Advanced Separation Technologies (CAST)** under Dr. Roe-Hoan Yoon (and Dr. Gerald Luttrell), with **VTIP as the patent assignee**. **Tag: Cited (Virginia Tech / MRC / ResearchGate). Confidence: High.**
- **Minerals Refining Company (MRC)** was launched in 2012 by E. Morgan Massey specifically to commercialize HHS; it has demonstrated the process at commercial scale (2.8 TPH pilot plant). MRC's stated field spans **coal and minerals — "the finest fractions of minerals and coal."** **Tag: Cited (mineralsrefining.com). Confidence: High.**
- MRC's public materials do **not** state whether its VT license is exclusive or worldwide, nor do they publish the field-of-use boundary. The dossier's market research asserts that at least one HHS branch "carries a worldwide license to a third party, Minerals Refining Company." **Tag: Assumption (dossier-asserted, not independently confirmed in public MRC/VTIP documents). Confidence: Medium.**

**Implication for LiT.** LiT's physical stage (selective fine/ultrafine surface-chemistry separation) overlaps HHS, which VTIP owns and has licensed to MRC for minerals. If that MRC license is exclusive and worldwide in the minerals field, LiT cannot obtain rights to the same core for the same field, and its physical stage would be foreclosed or narrowed to a carve-out. If non-exclusive or coal-restricted, room may exist. **This boundary is the determinative fact and is not on the public record.**

**NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL:** (i) the MRC-VTIP license field-of-use and exclusivity scope; (ii) the LiT-VTIP license (or option) terms governing whether LiT has secured rights to the HHS-adjacent separation IP and the 2020-priority REE leaching family; (iii) a patent attorney freedom-to-operate opinion on the VT/VTIP field-of-use carve-out. These were pre-flagged in the register (CR-1(b)) and stand confirmed by this pass — the public record cannot close them.

## (c) Public corroboration of LiT validation, IIT (ISM) Dhanbad agreement, or any pilot/LOI? — Finding: none located

- **No public record of "Lithium Technologies Inc," "Brian Keenum," or lithiumtechnologies.co** in connection with critical-minerals processing, bench-scale validation, licensing, or partnerships was located. **Tag: Unsupported (the validation and partnership claims are dossier-internal). Confidence the absence is real: Medium-High.**
- **IIT (ISM) Dhanbad is an active critical-minerals research hub** with multiple 2025-2026 partnerships (TEXMiN, CoRE-MiN centre under India's National Critical Minerals Mission, University of Pretoria MoU, NLC India MoU, UK-India TSI satellite campus). **None of these public agreements name LiT.** **Tag: Cited for the existence of IIT (ISM) Dhanbad's critical-minerals activity; Unsupported for any LiT-specific agreement. Confidence: High that IIT (ISM) is active in this field; Medium-High that no LiT agreement is public.**
- The only documented, dollar-quantified engagement remains the **$52,075 VT sponsored-research study (2024)** in the locked evidence base. No second-site pilot, operator trial, or LOI is publicly corroborated.

The IIT (ISM) finding cuts two ways: it confirms Dhanbad is a credible, government-backed critical-minerals research institution (supporting the plausibility of an R&D relationship), while showing that LiT is not among its publicly announced partners (weakening the claim of an executed, load-bearing agreement). The 75%-of-R&D-in-India assumption and the "majority of applied R&D at IIT (ISM)" claim therefore rest on undisclosed private arrangements.

## What this means for the deliverables (for Round 01 onward)

- **Strengths.** The genuine, verifiable strength is the *lineage*: Yoon/CAST/VTIP separation IP and a published VTIP low-temperature REE leaching family are real, high-quality, and inventor-aligned with LiT's VT relationship. Frame LiT's edge as *access to and integration of* world-class VT separation science, not ownership of granted patents.
- **Weaknesses / Threats.** Treat LiT's IP ownership as **unproven on the public record**. The physical core overlaps HHS (VTIP→MRC); the chemical core overlaps a VTIP-owned 2020-priority application; LiT's own PCT is unverifiable. The whole-mine-royalty model presumes LiT controls sublicensable IP — a presumption that fails unless the LiT-VTIP license grants it. This is a do-not-proceed-relevant fragility.
- **Confidence discipline for Round 01:** per the register's carried limitations, all claims of LiT IP ownership, FTO, and validation are to be tagged **Assumption or Unsupported, never Cited**, pending external retrieval.
