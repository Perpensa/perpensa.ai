# CR-3 — Valuation multiples and seed-stage capital for process-tech licensing / tailings-recovery businesses

**Priority:** P1. **Status:** Commissioned; executed in full from public sources. Deal-specific private multiples (the exact exit multiple a small single-asset licensor would command) are not directly observable and are reasoned from the nearest public comparables, tagged Assumption where inferred.
**Date retrieved:** 2026-06-29. **Moderator-run web research (WebSearch/WebFetch).**

This item underwrites two load-bearing numbers in the base: the asserted **8x-10x revenue / 12x-15x EBITDA exit multiple** that drives the **27x-34x headline return**, and the judgment that **$2.0M of seed capital is adequate to reach first license**. Two questions were posed: (a) what revenue and EBITDA multiples are actually paid for process-technology licensing businesses and royalty streams; (b) what seed-stage capital have comparable mineral-processing and tailings-recovery startups raised.

---

## Bottom line up front

**The exit-multiple assumption is directionally defensible but framed at the optimistic end, and it conflates two different comparison sets.** There are two distinct valuation worlds LiT could exit into, and they price very differently:

1. **Royalty / streaming businesses** (Franco-Nevada, Wheaton, Metalla) trade at **1.5x-2.0x NAV and roughly 15x-25x EV/EBITDA** — well above LiT's assumed 12x-15x. But that premium is earned by *diversification, de-risked producing assets, near-zero operating risk, and decades of cash-flow visibility*. A pre-scale, single-technology, concentration-heavy private royalty stream does **not** command franchise multiples; it is valued at a steep discount to those public comps. **Tag: Cited (public comps) + Assumption (discount for a small private single-asset stream). Confidence: High on the comp range, Medium on the discount.**

2. **Technology-licensing / specialty-process businesses** trade and transact at **roughly 9x-15x EBITDA** (specialty-chemicals M&A median ~12x in 2025; smaller tech/IP licensors 6x-10x; quality strategic assets above public trading). On this set, LiT's **12x-15x EBITDA is at-to-slightly-above the central tendency**, and credible if the business is genuinely high-margin, growing, and IP-defended. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High on the range.**

The honest reading: **12x-15x EBITDA is reasonable as a target if LiT is valued as a process-licensing business; 8x-10x revenue is only internally consistent with that EBITDA range if EBITDA margins land near 65-80%, which a near-pure-royalty model can support but a staffed R&D-and-BD company at first scale may not.** The headline return should be stress-tested at a lower multiple (e.g. 8x-10x EBITDA) in Round 04, not presented only at the optimistic anchor.

**The $2.0M seed figure is an order of magnitude below what every located comparable raised**, but the comparables are not apples-to-apples: they are building plants and refineries (capex-heavy), whereas LiT's licensing model is deliberately asset-light. **The right finding is not "too small" in absolute terms but "thin and single-tranche": $2.0M can plausibly fund a capital-light path to a validated pilot, but leaves no reserve for IP prosecution overruns, a slipped sales cycle, or a second pilot, and assumes no plant capital is ever LiT's to carry.** Capital-adequacy is therefore an **Assumption, Medium-Low confidence**, contingent on the licensing-only (no-build) model holding.

---

## (a) Multiples actually paid — the two comparison sets

### Royalty / streaming businesses (the aspirational comp)

- **Price-to-NAV:** royalty and streaming companies trade at **1.5x-2.0x NAV**, versus **0.7x-0.9x for operating miners** (BMO Capital Markets, Jan 2026). **Franco-Nevada trades at ~1.8x NAV.** The premium reflects fixed-income-like returns with commodity upside and no capex/operating exposure. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **EV/EBITDA:** Franco-Nevada and Wheaton Precious Metals trade in the **~15x-25x EV/EBITDA** range. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Method note:** for royalty companies the primary valuation lens is a **cash-flow multiple and P/NAV**, with asset-coverage review secondary, rather than EV/EBITDA per se. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Caveat (load-bearing):** these are large, diversified, public, de-risked portfolios of *producing* royalties. The premium is a function of diversification and durability LiT will not have at first exit. A single-technology, customer-concentrated, early-stage private royalty stream is valued at a **material discount** to this range — typically priced off the underlying contracted cash flows at a private-market discount rate, not at a Franco-Nevada multiple. **Tag: Assumption (standard private-vs-public, single-vs-diversified discount). Confidence: Medium.**

### Technology-licensing / specialty-process businesses (the realistic comp)

- **Smaller technology / IP-licensing companies:** EBITDA multiples commonly **6x-15x**, with **4x-10x** typical for smaller tech businesses and higher multiples reserved for high-growth, high-retention, defensible-IP names. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High on range.**
- **Specialty-chemicals / performance-materials M&A (2025):** private-equity deal median **12.3x EV/EBITDA** (down from 13.8x in 2024); strategic-buyer deals **~10.3x**; average purchase multiple **~9x** YTD 2025; public specialty-chemicals **~13.4x EV/EBITDA, ~2.65x EV/Sales** as of Jan 2026. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Process/catalyst-technology transaction comp:** **Honeywell's acquisition of Johnson Matthey's catalyst technologies for ~$2.4B (announced 2025)** is a clean strategic comp for monetizing a process/catalyst-IP business via trade sale. (Headline multiple not isolated here.) **Tag: Cited (transaction). Confidence: High that the comp exists; multiple NEEDS EXTERNAL RETRIEVAL.**
- **Recurring-licensing / SaaS analog:** median public SaaS ~**6.7x-7x revenue**; profitable SaaS has cleared **20x+ EV/EBITDA** in strong years. Useful only as a ceiling for *recurring, high-margin* license revenue, not a base case for mineral-processing royalties. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: Medium relevance.**

### Reconciling the dossier's 8x-10x revenue / 12x-15x EBITDA

- **12x-15x EBITDA** sits at-to-just-above the specialty-process / tech-licensing central tendency (~9x-13x) and well below the royalty-company range (15x-25x). It is **defensible as a target** if LiT exits as a *process-licensing* business with real growth and IP — but it is **not conservative**; it is the upper-middle of the realistic set. **Tag: Assumption anchored to Cited comps. Confidence: Medium.**
- **8x-10x revenue** is internally consistent with 12x-15x EBITDA **only at ~65-80% EBITDA margins** (revenue-multiple ÷ EBITDA-multiple ≈ margin). A near-pure-royalty stream can reach those margins; a company still carrying R&D, BD, and pilot support at first exit may not. If margins come in lower (say 50%), the two multiples diverge and the revenue-multiple framing **overstates value**. **Tag: Assumption. Confidence: Medium-Low.** Round 04 should run the exit at **8x-10x EBITDA** as the downside and reserve 12x-15x as the upside.

## (b) Seed-stage capital raised by comparable startups

Every located comparable raised **one to two orders of magnitude more than $2.0M** to commercialize — but each is building physical processing/refining capacity, not licensing IP:

- **Phoenix Tailings** (rare-earth recovery from mining waste — closest tailings analog): total funding ~**$147.8M**; Series B reached **~$116.6M** across closes ($43M initial → $76M → $40.2M B-3 amplification incl. $10M venture debt), at a **~$360M post Series B valuation**; strategic backing including Traxys. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Ceibo** (catalytic copper extraction at operating mines — closest process-licensing analog): **$30M Series B** led by Energy Impact Partners, backed by **BHP Ventures, Khosla Ventures, Aurus**, CoTec, Orion. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Endolith** (AI-guided microbial metals recovery): **$13.5M seed** (+ ~$3M second tranche), national-security-focused investors (Squadra, Draper, Collaborative Fund), under a BHP-led program. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Jetti Resources** (catalytic copper-leach licensing — see CR-2): **>$200M raised**, BHP and Freeport backing. **Tag: Cited. Confidence: High.**
- **Lithios** (MIT spin-out, electrochemical lithium recovery): venture-funded scale-up, exact figure not isolated here. **Tag: Cited (exists). Confidence: Medium.**

**Interpretation for capital adequacy.** The comparables that raised $30M-$200M are funding **plants, refineries, and field demonstrations** — capex LiT's licensing-only model claims to avoid. The relevant question is not "why so much less than Phoenix" but "is $2.0M enough for the capital-light path LiT actually proposes": fund a **bench-to-pilot validation (~$700k pilot per the dossier), IP prosecution, a small technical-and-BD team, and the multi-year sales cycle to a first license at ~Month 25.** On those line items $2.0M is **plausible but tight and single-tranche**: it carries little reserve for IP overruns, a failed first pilot, a slipped sales cycle, or any obligation to co-fund deployment capital. The moment LiT must put *any* plant or demonstration capital on its own balance sheet, the comparables show the number jumps to eight figures. **Tag: Assumption. Confidence: Medium-Low.** Round 05 should size the raise with an explicit second-tranche/bridge contingency and a hard line that LiT funds no client capex.

## What this means for the deliverables (for Round 01 onward)

- **Strengths.** A **deep, well-capitalized comparable set exists** (Phoenix Tailings $148M, Jetti >$200M, Ceibo $30M, Endolith $13.5M), proving investor appetite for domestic critical-minerals recovery and for the catalytic-process-at-operating-mine archetype. The exit channels are real: both a **royalty re-rating** (if a portfolio is built) and a **strategic trade sale** (Honeywell/Johnson Matthey-type) are live.
- **Weaknesses / Threats.** The **8x-10x revenue exit is optimistic and margin-contingent**; presented alone it overstates the return. LiT's **single-asset, customer-concentrated** early stream does **not** earn the 15x-25x royalty-company multiple. The **$2.0M raise is thin and single-tranche** against every funded comparable, and **collapses entirely if any deployment capital becomes LiT's**.
- **Strategy / financial model.** Carry **two exit framings**: a *licensing-business* base at **8x-12x EBITDA** (realistic, Cited-anchored) and a *royalty-rerate* upside at higher multiples **only after a diversified producing portfolio exists** (clearly labeled aspirational). Run the **27x-34x headline return at the 8x-10x EBITDA downside** in the Round 04 sensitivities, not only at the optimistic anchor.
- **Confidence discipline for Round 01.** The public comp *ranges* are **Cited/High**. The *specific exit multiple LiT will achieve* is **Assumption/Medium**. The *capital-adequacy of $2.0M* is **Assumption/Medium-Low**, contingent on the no-build licensing model holding.
